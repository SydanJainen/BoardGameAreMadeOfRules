# Coral Reefs of the Pacific

## Theme
In Coral Reefs of the Pacific, players take on the role of coral reef stewards tasked with building a thriving ecosystem that balances species populations and promotes biodiversity. As they place habitat tiles, attract marine species, and score points for their reef's unique patterns, players must navigate the delicate balance between over-population and under-provision. The conflict arises from the limited resources available, forcing players to make difficult decisions about which species to prioritize and how to manage their reef's growth. Will players focus on creating a diverse and resilient ecosystem, or will they sacrifice some of their species for short-term gains? By simulating this complex interplay, Coral Reefs of the Pacific challenges players to become eco-warriors, balancing the needs of individual species with the well-being of the entire reef.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Habitat Placement**: Players place habitat tiles on the hexagonal grid board, which represents the coral reef. Each tile has a specific resource cost, which is deducted from the player's hand.
2. **Species Attraction**: Marine species are attracted to the reef based on matching conditions between the habitat tile and the species' requirements. Players can choose to attract a specific species by spending Resource Cards that match the species's requirements.
3. **Biodiversity Scoring**: Players score points for their reef's biodiversity patterns, which are determined by the number and variety of species attracted to the reef.
- **Players**: Player 1: Alice
- **Duration**: 60 minutes

## Rules
**Rules:**

### A) TURN STRUCTURE

A turn consists of three phases:

1. **Action Phase**: Players take individual turns performing one action (see Action Catalog).
2. **Resource Management Phase**: Players manage their resources, replenishing their hand and modifying their score.
3. **End Phase**: The game ends the player's turn and prepares for the next round.

### B) ACTION CATALOG

**Attract Species**

* Name: Attract
* When: Action Phase
* Cost:
	+ 2 Wood Resource Cards
	+ 1 Iron Resource Card (optional)
* Step-by-step resolution:
	+ Draw a species card that matches the required conditions.
	+ Place the species token on the corresponding hexagonal region.
	+ Add points to your score based on the species's value.
* Restrictions and limits: Once per turn, requires adjacent tile.

**Modify Habitat**

* Name: Modify
* When: Action Phase
* Cost:
	+ 1 Wood Resource Card
	+ 1 Stone Resource Card
* Step-by-step resolution:
	+ Spend resources to modify the current hexagonal region.
	+ Update the habitat tile's resource cost and adjacency constraints.
* Restrictions and limits: Once per turn, requires adjacent tile.

**Increase/Decrease Population**

* Name: Manage
* When: Action Phase
* Cost:
	+ 1 Iron Resource Card (increase)
	+ 1 Wood Resource Card (decrease)
* Step-by-step resolution:
	+ Spend resources to increase or decrease a species's population.
	+ Update the species token's count and adjacency constraints.
* Restrictions and limits: Once per turn, requires adjacent tile.

**Trade Resources**

* Name: Trade
* When: Action Phase
* Cost: None
* Step-by-step resolution:
	+ Offer Resource Cards to another player in exchange for one of their cards.
	+ Resolve the trade by transferring resources and/or cards.
* Restrictions and limits: Anytime, no limitations.

**Rest**

* Name: Rest
* When: Action Phase
* Cost: None
* Step-by-step resolution:
	+ Pass your turn without taking any actions.
	+ Replenish your hand with one Resource Card.
* Restrictions and limits: Anytime, no limitations.

### C) PLAYER INTERACTION

Players can interact with each other through:

* **Trading Resources**: Players can trade Resource Cards with each other (see Action Catalog).
* **Blocking and Area Control**: Players can place habitat tiles to block or restrict other players' actions.
* **Alliances and Negotiations**: Players can form temporary alliances or negotiate trades with each other.

### D) RESOURCE FLOW

Resources are gained through the placement of habitat tiles, attracting marine species, and modifying existing populations. Resources are spent on various actions, such as attracting new species or modifying existing populations.

**Resource Market**

Players can buy, sell, or trade Resource Cards from a shared pool.

* Players start with a hand of 5 Resource Cards.
* The resource market refreshes at the end of each turn, allowing players to replenish their hand.

### E) SPECIAL RULES

The game includes several special rules that add depth and complexity to the gameplay:

* **Event Deck**: A deck of event cards that introduces random challenges and opportunities. Events can affect all players or individual species.
* **Environmental Effects**: The coral reef's ecosystem is affected by environmental factors, such as pollution or climate change. Players must adapt their strategies to mitigate these effects.

### F) END GAME

The game ends when all players have exhausted their Resource Cards (see Resource Flow). The player with the highest score wins.

**Victory Conditions**

* Points are awarded based on the number and variety of species present in each hexagonal region.
* Players can use Resource Cards to modify their score or manipulate other players' scores.

**Tiebreaker Rules**

In case of a tie, the player with the most unique biodiversity patterns (species combinations) wins. If still tied, the player with the highest score in any individual hexagonal region wins.

### G) EXAMPLE ROUND

Let's consider an example round with 2-3 players:

Player 1: Alice
Player 2: Bob
Player 3: Charlie

Turn 1:

* Alice (Action Phase): Attract Species (2 Wood, 1 Iron)
	+ Draw species card: Lionfish (requires 2 Wood and 1 Stone)
	+ Place Lionfish token on hex A1
	+ Add 5 points to score
* Bob (Action Phase): Modify Habitat (1 Wood, 1 Stone)
	+ Spend resources to modify hex B3
	+ Update habitat tile's resource cost and adjacency constraints
* Charlie (Action Phase): Rest
	+ Pass turn without taking any actions

Turn 2:

* Alice (Action Phase): Manage Population (1 Iron)
	+ Increase Lionfish population by 2
	+ Update species token's count and adjacency constraints
* Bob (Action Phase): Attract Species (2 Wood, 1 Stone)
	+ Draw species card: Parrotfish (requires 2 Coral and 1 Wood)
	+ Place Parrotfish token on hex C2
	+ Add 3 points to score
* Charlie (Action Phase): Trade Resources (2 Wood with Bob)
	+ Offer resources in exchange for a Resource Card

And so on... The game continues until all players have exhausted their Resource Cards, and the player with the highest score wins.
