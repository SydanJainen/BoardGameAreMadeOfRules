# Silk Road Showdown

## Theme
In the sprawling expanse of the ancient silk road, you are one of four ambitious merchants seeking to amass fortune and fame. The royal court has issued lavish orders for exotic spices, precious metals, and rare treasures. Your fate lies in your ability to navigate treacherous routes, forge alliances, and outmaneuver your rivals. The Silk Road is a dangerous yet lucrative arena where only the most cunning and fortunate will thrive.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Caravan Management:** On their turn, players draft cards from three offered piles (Spice Market, Trade Route, Event), choosing one to keep and passing the others clockwise. This mechanic encourages strategic decision-making as players weigh the immediate benefits of keeping a card against the potential value of the passed cards.

2. **Trade & Upgrade:** Players collect and trade spices using the market board, spending them to upgrade their caravans with new camels (adding cargo space) or hire guards (defensive bonuses). This mechanic balances resource management and strategic investment in personal growth.

3. **Route Navigation:** The silk road is represented by a modular hex grid, with tiles revealing different trade routes, hazards, and encounters based on the number of players. Players move their caravans along these routes, navigating random events and making strategic choices to optimize their journey.

4. **Order Fulfillment:** Royal orders are represented by cards that specify required spices, metals, or treasures along with reward points. Players deliver completed orders to claim rewards and victory points. This mechanic drives the game's competitive nature as players race to fulfill high-scoring orders before their opponents.

5. **Encounter Resolution:** When moving through certain tiles or completing specific tasks, players draw encounter cards that may present hazards (like bandit attacks), opportunities (like valuable trade-offs), or special events (affecting all players). This mechanic introduces variability and tension into the game flow.
- **Players**: 2-4
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes

## Components
- - 48 Spice Cards (12 types: Cinnamon
- Pepper
- Ginger
- Nutmeg
- etc.)
- 36 Royal Order Cards (9 unique orders)
- 24 Encounter Cards
- 12 Market Board tiles (displaying current spice values)
- 1 Silk Road Map (modular hex grid with 37 tiles)
- 8 Caravan Tokens (colored for players)
- 32 Upgrade Cards (20 Camels
- 8 Guards
- 4 Specialists)
- 1 First Player Token
- 4 Player Mats (for tracking resources and upgrades)

## Rules
**SETUP:**
1. Lay out the modular hex grid according to player count, connecting tiles as indicated in the rulebook.
2. Shuffle Spice Market, Trade Route, Event decks separately and place them face down near the board.
3. Distribute each player a mat, caravan token, and starting resources (5 Cinnamon, 3 Pepper, 1 Gold).
4. Choose a first player randomly and give them the First Player Token.

**RULES:**

**A) TURN STRUCTURE:**
Each turn consists of four phases in this sequence:
- **Draft Phase:** Players draft cards from three offered piles.
- **Movement Phase:** Players move their caravans along trade routes, optionally resolving encounters or upgrading caravans.
- **Trade Phase:** Players buy and sell spices at the market board to gain resources or gold.
- **End Phase:** Players discard down to their hand limit (7 cards), pass the First Player Token clockwise, and refresh event deck if depleted.

**B) ACTION CATALOG:**

1. **Move Caravan**
   - *When:* During Movement Phase.
   - *Cost:* None.
   - *Resolution:* Move caravan token along connected trade routes to an unoccupied tile. Draw an encounter card if starting or ending the turn on a hazard tile (bandit camps, storms, etc.).
   - *Limitations:* Must have enough action points to move across each tile (cost varies by terrain).

2. **Upgrade Caravan**
   - *When:* During Movement Phase.
   - *Cost:* Spices and/or gold as indicated on Upgrade Card.
   - *Resolution:* Discard required resources, gain the upgrade's benefit immediately (e.g., +1 cargo space for Camels).
   - *Limitations:* Once per turn. May only have 3 guards or specialists at end of game.

3. **Trade Spices**
   - *When:* During Trade Phase.
   - *Cost:* None.
   - *Resolution:* Buy spices from the market board by spending gold equal to current price, or sell spices for gold equal to current price minus a discount (1 gold per type sold).
   - *Limitations:* May only hold up to 20 spices of each type at end of game.

4. **Fulfill Royal Order**
   - *When:* During End Phase.
   - *Cost:* Discard required spices, metals, or treasures as indicated on the order card.
   - *Resolution:* Gain victory points equal to reward value plus any bonuses (e.g., +1 VP for each additional requirement fulfilled beyond the first).
   - *Limitations:* Once per order. Orders become unavailable once 2 players have fulfilled them.

5. **Draw Additional Encounter Card**
   - *When:* After resolving an encounter card during Movement Phase.
   - *Cost:* None.
   - *Resolution:* Draw and resolve another encounter card immediately.
   - *Limitations:* Once per turn. Discard both cards at end of turn if unable to fulfill requirements.

**C) PLAYER INTERACTION:**
Players primarily interact through trading spices at the market board, competing over high-scoring royal orders, and influencing each other's movement via encounter cards (e.g., blocking paths with bandits). Trading occurs openly among all players, while encounters can be played strategically to hinder opponents or aid allies.

**D) RESOURCE FLOW:**
Resources include Spices (12 types), Gold (used for trading and purchases), and Victory Points (earned through fulfilling royal orders and certain encounters). Players gain spices by drafting cards, trading at the market board, or resolving favorable encounters. Gold is gained through selling spices or encounter rewards. Resources are spent on upgrading caravans, fulfilling orders, or resolving hazards. Hand size limit is 7 cards; excess cards must be discarded.

**E) SPECIAL RULES:**
- **Market Board:** Each turn, one spice type becomes unavailable for trading (indicated by a 'Sold Out' token). Prices adjust based on supply and demand.
- **Encounter Cards:** Some encounters require dice rolls to resolve successfully. On a failure, players may discard resources to retry or suffer penalties.
- **Alliances:** Players may form alliances during the game, sharing resources and strategizing together (e.g., combining cargo space for bulk trading). Alliances do not affect final scoring.

**F) END GAME:**
The game ends immediately when one of the following conditions is met:
1. One player has fulfilled five royal orders.
2. The last royal order card has been claimed three times.
3. All tiles have been explored, and players have returned to the starting city.

Players score victory points for completed royal orders, unfulfilled orders they hold, excess spices (1 VP per 5), and remaining gold (1 VP per 3). In case of a tie, the player with the most fulfilled royal orders wins.

**G) EXAMPLE ROUND:**

*Round 1:*
- Alice drafts Spice Market, choosing Cinnamon.
- Bob drafts Trade Route, moving his caravan to 'Storms' tile (drawing an encounter card: 'Heavy Storm'), then upgrades with a Camel (+1 cargo space).
- Charlie drafts Event deck, gaining the 'Alliance' event allowing him and Alice to combine cargo space this turn.
- Dave trades 3 Pepper for gold at the market board.

*Movement Phase:*
- Alice moves through 'Desert' tile (drawing encounter: 'Bandit Camp'), retries with Gold, succeeds, gains 1 VP. She then moves to 'Cities' tile and fulfills her 'Pepper Order' card.
- Bob resolves his 'Heavy Storm,' paying Gold to retry successfully.
- Charlie trades with Alice using their Alliance event, selling him Cinnamon at discounted price.

*Trade Phase:*
- All players trade spices or gold as desired.

*End Phase:*
- Players discard down to hand limit (7 cards), pass the First Player Token clockwise, and refresh Event deck if depleted.
