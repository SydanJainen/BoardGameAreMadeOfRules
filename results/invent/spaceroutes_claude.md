# GAME NAME

**Spaceroutes**

---

# THEME

In the year 2487, the galactic frontier has been shattered into contested sectors by five rival factions, each commanding a fleet of colony ships and fuel tankers. The Galactic Transit Authority has collapsed, and the sprawling network of space stations that once connected civilized space has gone dark. Each faction now races to reactivate these stations, forge new hyperspace corridors between them, and establish dominance over the most lucrative trade routes in the galaxy. But space is unforgiving: asteroid fields choke the lanes between sectors, fuel is scarce and must be carefully rationed, and secret mission directives from faction high command push players toward conflicting objectives. The faction that builds the most efficient route network, completes the most missions, and controls the critical junction stations will be crowned the new Galactic Transit Sovereign.

---

# MECHANICS

**Route Building (Hyperspace Corridors).** Players claim and build routes between space station nodes on the hex-grid galaxy map. Each route segment connects two adjacent stations and costs a specific combination of Energy Crystals (resource cards). Longer routes and routes through hazardous sectors cost more but score higher. Once a player claims a route, no other player may build along that exact corridor, creating a race for critical connections. Players score points for completed routes and bonus points for connecting distant stations in a single unbroken chain.

**Fuel Management (Reactor Core).** Each player has a Reactor Core track on their player board with a maximum capacity of 8 fuel units. Every action a player takes on their turn — building a route segment, scouting an asteroid field, moving their flagship, or activating a station ability — costs between 1 and 3 fuel. Players begin each round with a partial refuel (gaining 3 fuel), but may also spend actions to dock at a controlled station for additional fuel or play Fuel Cache cards for emergency reserves. Running out of fuel forces a player to pass for the remainder of the round, so reckless expansion without fuel planning is punished.

**Asteroid Field Navigation (Hazard Encounters).** Certain hex tiles on the galaxy map are marked as asteroid fields. When a player builds a route that passes through an asteroid field hex, they must draw from the Asteroid Deck before the route is completed. Asteroid cards can impose fuel costs, force resource discards, damage the route (requiring repair on a future turn), or — rarely — reveal hidden resource caches or shortcut wormholes. Players can invest in Shield Upgrades (purchased with resources) that allow them to ignore or mitigate specific asteroid effects, rewarding long-term planning.

**Mission Cards (Faction Directives).** At the start of the game and at certain milestones, players draw Mission Cards that specify secret objectives — connecting specific pairs of stations, controlling a cluster of adjacent stations, accumulating certain resource types, or building through a required number of asteroid fields. Completed missions award substantial victory points and sometimes grant permanent abilities. Uncompleted missions held at game end impose a penalty. Players must balance pursuing their own missions against blocking opponents whose objectives they can infer from board behavior.

**Station Control (Sector Influence).** When a player builds routes connecting to a neutral station from at least two directions, they may spend resources to activate and claim that station, placing their faction marker on it. Controlled stations provide passive benefits at the start of each round: fuel refills, extra resource draws, or shield tokens. Stations at high-traffic junctions are fiercely contested, since an opponent can seize control by connecting more routes to a station than the current controller.

# COMPONENTS

- 1 Galaxy Map board (modular hex grid, 37 double-sided sector tiles arranged in a randomized layout each game)
- 5 Faction Player Boards (each with Reactor Core fuel track, resource storage slots, and shield upgrade track)
- 100 Energy Crystal cards (25 each of four types: Plasma (red), Helium-3 (blue), Dark Matter (purple), Solarium (gold))
- 30 Mission Cards (10 Route Missions, 10 Station Missions, 10 Special Missions)
- 24 Asteroid Deck cards (8 Damage, 6 Fuel Drain, 4 Resource Loss, 3 Wormhole Discovery, 3 Hidden Cache)
- 60 Route Claim markers (12 per player color: red, blue, green, yellow, white)
- 5 Flagship miniatures (1 per faction)
- 5 Faction Control markers (cubes, 10 per player color, 50 total)
- 15 Neutral Station tokens
- 20 Shield Upgrade tokens (4 per player)
- 40 Fuel tracking cubes (8 per player)
- 10 Wormhole shortcut tiles
- 1 Round tracker board with marker
- 1 First Player token (metal asteroid)
- 1 Rulebook
- 1 Quick Reference card per player (5 total)

---

# PLAYERS

2 to 5 players.

---

# DURATION

45 to 75 minutes (shorter with 2 players, longer at higher counts).

---

# SETUP

1. **Build the Galaxy Map.** Shuffle the 37 sector hex tiles face-down. Draw and place tiles one at a time to form a hex grid: 5 columns of 7-8-7-8-7 tiles. Flip all tiles face-up. Place Neutral Station tokens on every hex marked with a station icon (typically 12-15 stations per layout). Place Asteroid Field markers on every hex showing an asteroid icon.

2. **Prepare Decks.** Shuffle the Energy Crystal deck and place it face-down beside the board. Reveal 5 cards face-up in a market row (the Galactic Exchange). Shuffle the Asteroid Deck and Mission Card deck separately and place them face-down.

3. **Choose Factions.** Each player selects a faction, takes the matching Player Board, 12 Route Claim markers, 10 Faction Control cubes, 1 Flagship miniature, 4 Shield Upgrade tokens, and 8 Fuel tracking cubes. Place 3 fuel cubes on the Reactor Core track (starting fuel = 3).

4. **Starting Hands.** Deal each player 5 Energy Crystal cards from the deck. Deal each player 3 Mission Cards; each player keeps 2 and returns 1 to the bottom of the Mission deck without revealing it.

5. **Place Flagships.** Starting with a randomly chosen first player and proceeding clockwise, each player places their Flagship on any unoccupied station hex. That station is immediately claimed by that player (place a Faction Control cube on it).

6. **Set Round Tracker.** Place the round marker on space 1 of the Round Tracker. The game lasts 8 rounds (2 players), 7 rounds (3 players), or 6 rounds (4-5 players).

# RULES

## A) TURN STRUCTURE

Each round consists of individual player turns taken clockwise from the First Player. On your turn you perform the following three phases in order:

**Phase 1 — Refuel.** Gain 3 fuel (advance your Reactor Core track by 3, up to the maximum of 8). If you control a station with a Fuel Depot icon, gain 1 additional fuel.

**Phase 2 — Actions.** You have a budget of 3 Action Points (AP) to spend. Each action costs AP and fuel as listed in the Action Catalog (Section B). You may take the same action multiple times if you can afford it. You may also choose to take fewer than 3 actions.

**Phase 3 — Draw.** Draw 2 Energy Crystal cards from either the Galactic Exchange (face-up market) or the top of the deck, in any combination. Immediately refill the Exchange to 5 cards. Your hand limit is 10 cards; if you exceed it, discard down to 10.

After all players have taken their turns, advance the Round Tracker by 1 and pass the First Player token clockwise.

---

## B) ACTION CATALOG

**Build Route (1 AP, 1 Fuel).** Discard Energy Crystal cards matching the cost printed on the route segment between two adjacent station hexes. Place one of your Route Claim markers on that segment. Short routes (1 hex apart) cost 2 matching crystals. Medium routes (crossing 1 intermediate hex) cost 3 crystals of exactly 2 types. Long routes (crossing 2 intermediate hexes) cost 4 crystals of at least 2 types. If the route passes through an asteroid field hex, you must also resolve an Asteroid Encounter (draw 1 card from the Asteroid Deck and apply its effect) before placing the marker.

**Claim Station (1 AP, 2 Fuel).** If you have routes connecting to a neutral or opponent-controlled station from at least 2 different directions, you may spend 2 fuel and 1 resource card of any type to place your Faction Control cube on that station. If the station was controlled by an opponent, remove their cube first. The new controller immediately gains the station's passive benefit starting next round.

**Scout Asteroid Field (1 AP, 1 Fuel).** Move your Flagship to an adjacent asteroid field hex and draw 2 Asteroid Deck cards. You may choose to resolve one and discard the other. This allows you to fish for Wormhole Discoveries or Hidden Caches while mitigating risk. You do not need to build a route to scout.

**Upgrade Shields (1 AP, 0 Fuel).** Discard 3 Energy Crystal cards of the same type to take 1 Shield Upgrade token from your supply. Shield Upgrades are placed on your player board and are permanent. Each upgrade lets you cancel 1 negative Asteroid card effect per round (Damage, Fuel Drain, or Resource Loss). You may hold a maximum of 4 Shield Upgrades.

**Move Flagship (1 AP, 1 Fuel).** Move your Flagship up to 2 hexes along any route segments you have claimed. Your Flagship must be at or adjacent to a station to Claim it. Moving through opponent-claimed routes costs 1 additional fuel per segment.

**Draw Mission (1 AP, 0 Fuel).** Draw 2 Mission Cards, keep 1, return the other to the bottom of the deck. You may hold a maximum of 5 incomplete missions at any time.

*Restrictions:*
- You cannot build a route on a segment already claimed by another player.
- You cannot claim a station if you have fewer than 2 routes connecting to it.
- If you run out of fuel during your Action Phase, your turn ends immediately (you still complete Phase 3).

---

## C) PLAYER INTERACTION

**Route Blocking.** Since each route segment can only be claimed by one player, building a route denies that corridor to all opponents. Experienced players will identify critical junction routes early and race to claim them, forcing opponents into longer, costlier paths.

**Station Contests.** Stations can change control. If an opponent has claimed a station but you later build more connecting routes to it, you may spend an action to seize it. This creates an ongoing tug-of-war at high-value junction stations, especially those granting fuel bonuses.

**Galactic Exchange Competition.** All players draw from the same face-up crystal market. Taking a card an opponent needs is a legitimate and important tactical consideration. Timing purchases to deny key resources can stall an opponent's route-building plans for an entire round.

**Trading.** On your turn, before or after your Action Phase, you may propose a trade with any one other player. Trades may include Energy Crystal cards, fuel (transferred cube-for-cube), or promises of future cooperation. Both players must agree. No more than 1 trade per turn.

**Mission Inference.** Since missions are secret, players must watch opponents' building patterns to deduce their objectives. Building toward a distant corner station strongly signals a Route Mission targeting that station, allowing savvy opponents to block.

---

## D) RESOURCE FLOW

**Gaining Resources:**
- Draw 2 Energy Crystal cards per turn (Phase 3).
- Controlled stations with a Crystal Mine icon grant 1 bonus crystal draw at the start of your turn.
- Hidden Cache asteroid cards grant 2 crystal cards immediately.
- Completing certain missions grants a one-time resource bonus (as stated on the card).

**Spending Resources:**
- Building routes costs 2-4 Energy Crystal cards depending on route length.
- Claiming stations costs 1 Energy Crystal card of any type plus 2 fuel.
- Upgrading shields costs 3 identical Energy Crystal cards.

**Fuel Economy:**
- Gain 3 fuel per round (Phase 1 Refuel).
- Controlled Fuel Depot stations grant +1 fuel per round.
- Fuel Cache asteroid cards grant +2 fuel immediately.
- Every action except Upgrade Shields and Draw Mission costs fuel.
- Maximum fuel capacity is 8. Fuel beyond 8 is lost.

**Resource Scarcity Tension.** With only 25 cards per crystal type and a 10-card hand limit, players must spend resources regularly rather than hoarding. The Galactic Exchange creates a shared economy where one player's purchase directly affects others' options.

---

## E) SPECIAL RULES

**Wormhole Shortcuts.** When a Wormhole Discovery card is drawn from the Asteroid Deck, the player places a Wormhole tile connecting two asteroid field hexes of their choice on the board. Any player may use a wormhole as a free movement connection (costs 0 fuel to traverse, but the route still counts as passing through an asteroid field for building purposes). Wormholes are permanent once placed.

**Faction Abilities.** Each faction has a unique passive ability printed on its Player Board:
- **Void Runners (Red):** Routes through asteroid fields cost 1 fewer crystal card (minimum 1).
- **Fuel Barons (Blue):** Maximum fuel capacity is 10 instead of 8. Gain +1 fuel during Refuel phase.
- **Star Cartographers (Green):** When scouting asteroid fields, draw 3 cards instead of 2.
- **Nexus Syndicate (Yellow):** Claiming a station costs 0 crystal cards (still costs 2 fuel and 1 AP).
- **Frontier Corps (White):** May build 1 route per round that ignores asteroid encounters (still pay crystal cost).

**Emergency Docking.** If a player ends their turn with 0 fuel and 0 Energy Crystal cards in hand, they may perform an Emergency Dock: their Flagship is moved to the nearest station they control (or their starting station if they control none), and they gain 2 fuel. This prevents a player from being completely locked out of the game.

**Round Events (Optional Variant).** For added variability, shuffle 8 Round Event cards and reveal one at the start of each round. Events affect all players equally — examples include Solar Flare (all players lose 1 fuel), Trade Boom (the Galactic Exchange is refilled entirely), or Pirate Raid (each player must discard 1 crystal card or lose 1 fuel).

---

## F) END GAME AND SCORING

The game ends after the final round (determined by player count as described in Setup step 6). Players then tally victory points:

| Category | Points |
|---|---|
| Short route claimed (2-crystal cost) | 2 VP |
| Medium route claimed (3-crystal cost) | 4 VP |
| Long route claimed (4-crystal cost) | 7 VP |
| Station controlled at game end | 3 VP per station |
| Completed Route Mission | 5-12 VP (as printed on card) |
| Completed Station Mission | 4-8 VP (as printed on card) |
| Completed Special Mission | 6-15 VP (as printed on card) |
| Uncompleted Mission penalty | -3 VP per card |
| Longest Continuous Chain bonus | 10 VP (awarded to the player whose claimed routes form the longest unbroken path measured in route segments; ties split the bonus, rounding down) |
| Most Stations Controlled bonus | 5 VP (awarded to the player controlling the most stations at game end; ties: each tied player gets 3 VP) |
| Each unused Shield Upgrade | 1 VP |

The player with the most VP wins. In case of a tie, the tied player who completed more missions wins. If still tied, the player who controls more stations wins. If still tied, the players share victory.

## G) EXAMPLE ROUND

**Setup context:** 3-player game, Round 3. Alice (Void Runners/Red), Bob (Fuel Barons/Blue), Carol (Star Cartographers/Green). The Galactic Exchange shows: Plasma, Plasma, Helium-3, Dark Matter, Solarium.

**Alice's Turn:**

*Phase 1 — Refuel:* Alice has 2 fuel. She gains 3 (base) + 1 (she controls a Fuel Depot station) = 4 fuel, bringing her to 6.

*Phase 2 — Actions:*
- Action 1: **Build Route** (1 AP, 1 fuel). Alice discards 2 Plasma cards to build a short route from Station Kepler-7 to Station Vega-12. The route passes through an asteroid field. She draws an Asteroid card: Fuel Drain (-2 fuel). She has a Shield Upgrade and cancels this effect. She places her red Route Claim marker. Fuel: 6 - 1 = 5.
- Action 2: **Claim Station** (1 AP, 2 fuel). Alice now has routes connecting to neutral Station Vega-12 from 2 directions. She discards 1 Solarium card and spends 2 fuel to claim it, placing her red control cube. Fuel: 5 - 2 = 3.
- Action 3: **Move Flagship** (1 AP, 1 fuel). Alice moves her Flagship to Station Vega-12 to be in position for future expansion. Fuel: 3 - 1 = 2.

*Phase 3 — Draw:* Alice takes 1 card from the Exchange (the Dark Matter) and 1 from the deck. The Exchange is refilled to 5 cards. Her hand is now 6 cards (under the 10 limit).

**Bob's Turn:**

*Phase 1 — Refuel:* Bob has 4 fuel. He gains 3 (base) + 1 (Fuel Barons faction bonus) = 4 fuel, bringing him to 8.

*Phase 2 — Actions:*
- Action 1: **Build Route** (1 AP, 1 fuel). Bob discards 1 Helium-3 and 1 Plasma and 1 Dark Matter to build a medium route from Station Orion-3 to Station Cygnus-9. No asteroid field on this path. Fuel: 8 - 1 = 7.
- Action 2: **Build Route** (1 AP, 1 fuel). Bob discards 2 Solarium cards to build a short route from Station Cygnus-9 toward the board edge. Fuel: 7 - 1 = 6.
- Action 3: **Draw Mission** (1 AP, 0 fuel). Bob draws 2 Mission Cards, sees one requiring connection between Orion-3 and the far station Andromeda-1 (worth 12 VP). He keeps it and returns the other. Fuel remains at 6.

*Phase 3 — Draw:* Bob takes 2 cards from the deck. Hand is now 5 cards.

**Carol's Turn:**

*Phase 1 — Refuel:* Carol has 1 fuel. She gains 3, bringing her to 4.

*Phase 2 — Actions:*
- Action 1: **Scout Asteroid Field** (1 AP, 1 fuel). Carol moves her Flagship to an adjacent asteroid hex and draws 3 Asteroid cards (Star Cartographers ability). She draws: Fuel Drain, Hidden Cache, Damage. She resolves Hidden Cache (gains 2 Energy Crystal cards from the deck) and discards the other two. Fuel: 4 - 1 = 3.
- Action 2: **Upgrade Shields** (1 AP, 0 fuel). Carol discards 3 Helium-3 cards to gain a Shield Upgrade token.
- Action 3: **Build Route** (1 AP, 1 fuel). Carol discards 2 Dark Matter cards to claim a short route adjacent to Station Vega-12 — the same station Alice just claimed. Carol now has 1 route connecting to Vega-12 and notes she will need a second route before she can contest control. Fuel: 3 - 1 = 2.

*Phase 3 — Draw:* Carol takes 2 cards from the Exchange. Hand is now 4 cards.

The round ends. The Round Tracker advances to Round 4 and the First Player token passes from Alice to Bob.
