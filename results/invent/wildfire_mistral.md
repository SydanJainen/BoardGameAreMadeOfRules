# Ember's Edge: Wildfire Defenders

## Theme
In "Ember's Edge," players step into the roles of elite firefighters tasked with protecting the breathtaking yet fragile Serenity National Park from a series of devastating wildfires. Caused by an unusually hot summer and reckless arsonists, these infernos threaten to consume the park's precious ecosystems and endanger its inhabitants. Players must work together, coordinating their efforts across variable terrains and unpredictable winds to contain and extinguish fires before they reach three critical towns, ensuring the safety of civilians and preserving the park's delicate balance.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Wind Cards & Fire Spread:** At the start of each round, wind cards determine how the fire spreads across Serenity National Park. These cards display wind direction (light or strong) and intensity (light or strong), creating dynamic challenges for players as fires change course or intensify.
   - Light Wind: Spreads one hex in a straight line, stopping at obstacles like towns or forests.
   - Strong Wind: Spreads two hexes if the first is empty; otherwise, it stops at the nearest obstacle.

2. **Action Point Allocation & Action Cards:** Each player starts with 3 action points per turn, which can be spent on various actions such as water drops, constructing defenses, evacuating inhabitants, or scouting the park's perimeter using Action Cards. Careful allocation is crucial for managing multiple tasks simultaneously and balancing resource acquisition with active firefighting efforts.
   - Action Cards include standard actions like Move (1 AP), Scout (1 AP), Activate (0 AP), Fight Fire (variable AP cost), Evacuate Inhabitants (2 Medical Supplies, 1 AP), Construct Firebreak (2 Fuel, 1 Manpower), and specialist abilities (see Specialist Cards).
   - Players gain two resource cards at the end of each turn to replenish spent Action Cards.

3. **Resource Management & Economy:** To fund their firefighting efforts, players must gather resources (water, fuel, manpower) by moving around the park's hex grid or drawing resource cards. Balancing resource acquisition with active firefighting is essential for maintaining momentum and preventing fire spread.
   - Resources are gained through Park Perimeter Cards, selling excess resources at the Supply Depot action, or trading with other players using Specialist abilities like Shared Sacrifice.
   - Players start with a hand limit of six cards, increasing to eight at the end of their third round. No storage limits exist for resources gained through Park Perimeter Cards.
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: 45-60 minutes

## Rules
**SETUP:**

1. Assemble the game board and place it in the center of the table.
2. Shuffle the Wind Deck separately from the Action Deck, Park Perimeter Deck, and Resource Deck; place them face down near the board.
3. Each player chooses a color and takes the matching player token, placing it on their starting hex (designated by colored markers).
4. Distribute the Town Markers evenly around the park's perimeter, ensuring no two towns share an edge or vertex.
5. Deal 6 resource cards to each player as starting resources.
6. Choose a first player randomly and give them the die.

**RULES:**

*A) TURN STRUCTURE:*

1. **Wind Reveal**: Draw a Wind Card from the Wind Deck. Resolve its effects on fire spread according to the Special Rules section.
2. **Action Phase**: Each player takes turns performing actions simultaneously but follows these phases in order:
   - Move: Players move their tokens up to two hexes along roads or through forest terrain (1 Action Point).
   - Scout: Players may draw a Park Perimeter Card from the hex they occupy if it contains one (1 Action Point).
   - Activate: Players may perform optional actions like constructing defenses, evacuating inhabitants, or using Specialist abilities (0 Action Points unless specified otherwise).
   - Fight Fire: Players use their firefighting equipment and resources to combat flames on adjacent hexes (variable Action Point cost).
3. **Resource Gain**: Players draw two resource cards from the top of the Resource Deck.
4. **End Phase**: Discard any unused Action Cards or Firebreaker Cards played this turn. Check for victory conditions.

*B) SPECIALIST CARDS:*

Each player receives a Specialist Card at the start of their third round, granting them unique abilities that can benefit others at a cost:

- *Shared Sacrifice*: Allow another player to gain 1 resource of your choice by discarding one resource from your hand.
- *Mutual Aid*: Activate another player's Firefighting Equipment once per turn by spending an additional action point.
- *Fire Marshal*: Once per round, force the fire to spread in a specific direction (light wind intensity) by discarding 1 Fuel and 1 Water.
- *Smoke Jumper*: Move through flames without restriction during one Move phase; discard 2 Manpower.

**D) PLAYER INTERACTION:**

Players cooperate throughout the game, sharing resources and coordinating efforts to fight fires collectively. However, each player has unique specialist abilities (revealed through drawing Specialist Cards), allowing them to perform actions that benefit others at a cost:

- *Shared Sacrifice*: Allow another player to gain 1 resource of your choice by discarding one resource from your hand.
- *Mutual Aid*: Activate another player's Firefighting Equipment once per turn by spending an additional action point.

*E) RESOURCE FLOW:*

Players start with a hand limit of six cards, increasing to eight at the end of their third round. At the end of each turn, players gain two resource cards. Resources are discarded when performing actions or playing cards. No storage limits exist for resources gained through Park Perimeter Cards.

*F) SPECIAL RULES:*

1. **Wind Cards**: Each card shows a wind direction and intensity (light or strong). During Wind Reveal, fire spreads according to the indicated direction(s).
   - Light wind: Spreads one hex.
   - Strong wind: Spreads two hexes if the first is empty; otherwise, it stops at the nearest obstacle (e.g., town marker, firebreak).

2. **Firebreaker Cards**: These powerful actions can be played once per turn during Activate phase or Fight Fire phase. They offer unique advantages but require discarding resources.

3. **Towns on Fire**: If a town catches fire, it remains ablaze until evacuated (see Evacuate Inhabitants). If three towns are burning simultaneously, the game ends immediately in defeat for all players.

4. **Supply Depot**: Players can sell excess resources at the Supply Depot by moving their token onto the Supply Depot hex and discarding two cards of the same type to gain 1 resource card of another type or 2 Water (minimum hand limit enforced).

*G) END GAME:*

The game ends when either:

- All fires have been extinguished, and no towns are burning; players win together.
- Three town markers are burning simultaneously; players lose collectively.

In case of a tie between multiple winning scenarios (e.g., fire is spreading rapidly but still fewer than three towns are ablaze), the player with the fewest resource cards wins.

*Round 1:*

- **Player 3**:
  - Moves to hex D6.
  - Activates helicopter using Mutual Aid from Player 2, extinguishing one fire at hex E7 with no resource cost.
  - Ends turn with 5 cards in hand (gains 2 Water).
