# Coral Bloom

## Theme
In Coral Bloom, players embody marine biologists dedicated to revitalizing a once-thriving coral reef. The Great Bleaching has left behind an underwater wasteland, and it's up to the players to restore biodiversity by attracting various marine species through strategically placing habitat tiles that match their specific needs. As populations grow and diverse patterns emerge, the reef begins to flourish once more. Competing for limited resources and space, players must balance their reef's ecosystem carefully, as too much of a good thing can lead to species depletion.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Tile Laying:** Players place habitat tiles from their hand onto the modular game board. Each tile has different combinations of coral structures, water depths, and other features that attract specific marine species.
   - *Decisions:* Choosing which tile to lay based on its effects, available space, and adjacency to existing tiles.
   - *Consequences:* Tiles laid too close together may lead to overpopulation and species depletion.

2. **Species Attraction:** At the end of each turn, marine species cards are drawn based on the number of compatible habitat tiles placed that round. Species cards have requirements (e.g., certain coral structures or water depths) and effects (e.g., score bonus for patterns or population growth).
   - *Decisions:* Balancing tile placement to attract desired species while avoiding overpopulation.
   - *Consequences:* Too many of a single species can cause it to go extinct if its population reaches zero.

3. **Biodiversity Patterns:** Players score points for creating unique patterns of habitat tiles, such as clusters or lines. These patterns encourage strategic planning and placement decisions.
   - *Decisions:* Planning ahead to create desired patterns with tile placement.
   - *Consequences:* Focusing too much on patterns may lead to neglecting species attraction or imbalanced ecosystems.

4. **Resource Management:** Players collect and spend resources (tropical storms, nutrients, algae) throughout the game to perform actions like placing tiles, drawing additional species cards, or mitigating environmental threats.
   - *Decisions:* Managing resource collection and spending efficiently to maximize points.
   - *Consequences:* Inadequate resource management can hinder progress or cause penalties for running out of resources.
- **Players**: 1-4 players
- **Duration**: 40-60 minutes

## Components
- - 1 Game Board (modular hex grid with 37 tiles)
- 80 Habitat Tiles (20 each of four types: Shallow Reef
- Deep Reef
- Coral Mounds
- Kelp Forest)
- 60 Marine Species Cards (15 each of four types: Fish
- Invertebrates
- Corals
- Other Creatures)
- 4 Player Mats
- 8 Resource Tokens (2 each of tropical storms
- nutrients
- algae)
- 1 First Player Token
- 1 Scorepad and Pencil per player

## Rules
**SETUP:**

1. Assemble the game board by placing the central tile and surrounding it with hex tiles to form a circle.
2. Shuffle each type of Habitat Tile separately and place them face down near the board as draw piles.
3. Shuffle Marine Species Cards together and create a draw pile.
4. Each player chooses a color and takes the corresponding player mat, scoring track, resource tokens (2 tropical storms, 1 nutrient, 1 algae), and starting habitat tiles (6 shallow reef, 2 deep reef, 1 coral mounds, 1 kelp forest).
5. Players place their starting habitat tiles on the central board tile according to the layout shown on their player mat.
6. Determine the first player randomly.

**RULES:**

**A) TURN STRUCTURE:**

1. **Gain Phase:** Gain 1 resource token of each type (tropical storms, nutrients, algae).
2. **Placement Phase:** Place up to two habitat tiles from your hand onto the board. Mandatory.
3. **Attraction Phase:** Draw marine species cards based on compatible habitat tiles placed this round (one Fish, one Invertebrate, and additional species if you have enough compatible tiles). Optional.
4. **Species Management Phase:** Resolve population growth or depletion for each species card in your reef. Optional, but required at the end of the game.
5. **Resource Management Phase:** Trade resource tokens with other players (1:1), discard excess resources to gain points (1 point per 2 resources discarded), or mitigate environmental threats (discard nutrients to remove tropical storms). Optional.

**B) ACTION CATALOG:**

1. **Place Habitat Tile:**
   - *When:* Placement Phase.
   - *Cost:* None.
   - *Resolution:* Choose a tile from your hand and place it adjacent to any of your existing tiles or the central board tile, matching water depth edges.
   - *Limitations:* A player may not have more than 15 habitat tiles in play at one time. Tiles cannot be placed overlapping other tiles.

2. **Draw Additional Species Card:**
   - *When:* Attraction Phase.
   - *Cost:* Spend 1 nutrient token.
   - *Resolution:* Draw an additional marine species card from the top of the deck.
   - *Limitations:* Once per turn, up to a maximum of three species cards drawn in total during one player's turn.

3. **Trade Resources:**
   - *When:* Resource Management Phase.
   - *Cost:* None.
   - *Resolution:* Offer resource tokens to other players (1:1). Players may accept or decline trades independently.
   - *Limitations:* Each trade is final once agreed upon by both parties. No player can have more than 3 of any single resource token.

4. **Mitigate Environmental Threats:**
   - *When:* Resource Management Phase.
   - *Cost:* Spend nutrients equal to the number of tropical storms in play.
   - *Resolution:* Remove one tropical storm token from the game.
   - *Limitations:* Once per turn, up to a maximum of two environmental threats mitigated per player's turn.

5. **Discard Resources for Points:**
   - *When:* Resource Management Phase.
   - *Cost:* Spend resources equal to twice your current score (rounded down).
   - *Resolution:* Gain points equal to the number of resources discarded, rounded down.
   - *Limitations:* Once per game, after the final round but before scoring.

**C) PLAYER INTERACTION:**

Players interact indirectly through competition for limited space on the board and compatible habitat tiles. There is no trading or combat among players. Players may negotiate alliances or alliances at any time outside of the gameplay phases.

**D) RESOURCE FLOW:**

Resources are gained during the Gain Phase, spent during various actions (placing tiles, drawing species cards, mitigating threats), discarded for points during Resource Management Phase, and lost when environmental threats occur (tropical storms cause nutrients to be lost).

**E) SPECIAL RULES:**

1. **Marine Species Cards:** Each type of marine species card has unique requirements and effects:
   - Fish require specific coral structures.
   - Invertebrates require matching water depth.
   - Corals grow best in clusters.
   - Other Creatures have varied requirements.

2. **Environmental Effects:** Tropical storms occur at the start of each round if the total number of habitat tiles in play exceeds a certain threshold (16 for 1-2 players, 24 for 3 players, and 32 for 4 players). When this happens, each player loses one nutrient token.

**F) END GAME:**

The game ends immediately when one of these conditions is met:

1. One player reaches exactly 20 points.
2. The total number of habitat tiles in play exceeds the threshold for triggering tropical storms (as described above).

Players score points based on their reef's biodiversity patterns, unique species attracted, and excess resources discarded. In case of a tie, the player with more unique species wins.

**G) EXAMPLE ROUND:**

*Player 1:*

- Gain Phase: Gain one tropical storm, nutrient, and algae token.
- Placement Phase: Places two shallow reef tiles adjacent to existing habitat tiles on the central board tile.
- Attraction Phase: Draws one Fish and one Invertebrate species card due to compatible habitat tiles placed this round. Decides not to draw additional cards.
- Species Management Phase: Resolves population growth for both species cards in their reef.
- Resource Management Phase: Trades one nutrient token with Player 2 for one algae token, then discards two tropical storm tokens to gain one point.

*Player 2:*

- Gain Phase: Gain one tropical storm, nutrient, and algae token.
- Placement Phase: Places one deep reef tile adjacent to an existing deep reef tile, then places one coral mounds tile adjacent to the shallow reef tiles placed by Player 1.
- Attraction Phase: Draws one Fish species card due to compatible habitat tiles placed this round. Decides not to draw additional cards.
- Species Management Phase: Resolves population growth for their Fish species card in their reef.
- Resource Management Phase: Accepts trade offer from Player 1, then mitigates one environmental threat by discarding two nutrient tokens.

*The game continues with Players 3 and 4 taking their turns.*

This example demonstrates the tile-laying, species attraction, and resource management aspects of the game, as well as player interaction through trading.
