# GAME NAME

**Caravans of the Golden Road**

---

# THEME

You are a merchant prince of the ancient Silk Road, commanding a caravan of camels, guards, and artisans across thousands of miles of treacherous desert, mountain passes, and bandit-plagued valleys. The Great Khan has issued a series of royal decrees demanding exotic spices, rare silks, and precious gems — and the merchant who fulfills the most prestigious orders will earn the title of Imperial Trade Vizier. But the road is long, and you are not the only merchant racing to answer the Khan's call. Rival caravans compete for the same scarce goods at every bazaar, bandits stalk the mountain passes, and sandstorms can bury an entire season's profit overnight. Every decision — which route to take, which goods to hoard, which rivals to trade with or undercut — could mean the difference between glory and ruin.

---

# MECHANICS

**Set Collection (Spice Gathering).** Players collect cards from four spice families — Saffron, Cardamom, Cinnamon, and Silk — to fulfill Royal Order cards that require specific combinations. Each Royal Order demands a different set, ranging from simple pairs to elaborate five-card combinations worth far more prestige. The tension lies in deciding whether to grab easy-to-complete small orders quickly or gamble on assembling the rare, high-value sets while opponents snatch the goods you need.

**Action Point Allowance (Caravan Actions).** On each turn a player has exactly 3 Action Points (AP) to spend across a menu of possible actions: moving along trade routes, buying goods at bazaars, trading with rivals, upgrading their caravan, or fulfilling orders. Because 3 AP is never quite enough to do everything you want, players must constantly prioritize — do you spend this turn stocking up on spices, or do you rush to the capital to deliver before a rival beats you to a lucrative order?

**Route Network and Movement (The Silk Road Map).** The game board is a network of 12 location nodes connected by trade routes of varying danger levels (Safe, Risky, Perilous). Moving along safer routes costs more AP but avoids Hazard draws, while Perilous shortcuts cost only 1 AP but force you to draw from the Hazard Deck, risking bandits, storms, or breakdowns. Players must choose between speed and safety, and since certain spices are only available at certain bazaars, the route you choose determines the goods you can access.

**Push-Your-Luck (Hazard Navigation).** Whenever a player moves along a Risky or Perilous route, they draw one or more Hazard cards that can steal resources, delay movement, or — occasionally — reward bravery with bonus goods found along the road. Players with upgraded caravans can mitigate or ignore certain hazards, creating an incentive to invest in upgrades early. The question is always whether the shortcut is worth the risk, especially when you are carrying a full load of valuable spices.

**Market Fluctuation (The Bazaar).** The Bazaar Display is a shared row of 5 face-up Spice Cards that changes every round. When a player buys spices, they take from this display and new cards are drawn from the Spice Deck to replace them. Because all players draw from the same pool, buying decisions ripple through the economy — grabbing the last Saffron card might leave your opponent unable to complete their order. Timing your purchases to deny opponents key resources is just as important as filling your own hand.

# COMPONENTS

- 80 Spice Cards (20 per type: Saffron, Cardamom, Cinnamon, Silk)
- 24 Royal Order Cards (varying difficulty levels: 8 Bronze, 8 Silver, 8 Gold)
- 30 Hazard Cards
- 16 Caravan Upgrade Cards (4 unique upgrades, 4 copies each)
- 12 Bazaar Tokens (round wooden discs, placed on location nodes to mark active bazaars)
- 1 Game Board (a network map of 12 location nodes connected by trade routes, printed on a folding board approximately 50 cm × 50 cm)
- 4 Caravan Meeples (1 per player, in Red, Blue, Green, Yellow)
- 4 Player Boards (individual tableau boards showing caravan cargo hold, upgrades, and completed orders)
- 20 Coin Tokens (cardboard punch-out coins in denominations: 10× "1 Coin" and 10× "3 Coin")
- 4 Action Point Trackers (small cubes, one per player color, used to track AP spent each turn)
- 1 Round Marker (wooden disc placed on the round track printed on the game board)
- 1 First Player Token (ornate camel-shaped wooden token)
- 3 Bazaar Display Holders (small card stands to hold the face-up Bazaar Display row)
- 1 Rulebook

# SETUP

1. **Unfold the game board** and place it in the center of the table so all players can reach it. The board shows 12 named location nodes (see the node list below) connected by trade routes marked as Safe (green lines), Risky (yellow lines), or Perilous (red lines). A round track running along the top edge of the board shows rounds 1 through 10.

2. **Place the Round Marker** on the space marked "1" on the round track.

3. **Prepare the Spice Deck.** Shuffle all 80 Spice Cards together into a single face-down deck. Place this deck beside the board.

4. **Create the Bazaar Display.** Draw the top 5 cards from the Spice Deck and lay them face-up in a row beside the board. This is the Bazaar Display. Use the Bazaar Display Holders if desired.

5. **Prepare the Royal Order Deck.** Separate the 24 Royal Order Cards into three stacks by tier: Bronze, Silver, and Gold. Shuffle each stack independently. Place the Gold stack face-down, then place the Silver stack face-down on top of it, then place the Bronze stack face-down on top of that. The resulting combined deck should have Bronze orders on top, Silver in the middle, and Gold at the bottom. Draw the top 3 Royal Order Cards and lay them face-up beside the board. These are the Available Orders.

6. **Prepare the Hazard Deck.** Shuffle all 30 Hazard Cards into a face-down deck. Place this deck beside the board.

7. **Prepare the Upgrade Market.** Shuffle all 16 Caravan Upgrade Cards together. Deal 4 face-up in a row beside the board. Place the remaining 12 face-down as the Upgrade Deck.

8. **Distribute starting resources to each player.** Each player receives:
   - 1 Caravan Meeple in their chosen color.
   - 1 Player Board placed in front of them.
   - 1 Action Point Tracker cube in their color (place it on the "0" space of the AP track printed on their Player Board).
   - 3 Coins (taken from the Coin Token supply).
   - 2 Spice Cards drawn from the top of the Spice Deck (these go into the player's hand, kept secret from other players).

9. **Place Caravan Meeples.** Every player places their Caravan Meeple on the location node labeled "Kashgar" (the starting city, marked with a star on the board).

10. **Select the first player.** The player who most recently ate something spicy takes the First Player Token. If no one qualifies or players cannot agree, the youngest player takes it. Play proceeds clockwise from the first player.

# RULES

## A) TURN STRUCTURE

A game of Caravans of the Golden Road is played over a series of rounds. Each round consists of every player taking one turn in clockwise order, followed by a shared End-of-Round Phase. Within a single player's turn, the following phases occur in strict order:

**Phase 1 — Refresh (Mandatory).** Reset your Action Point Tracker to 3. If any of your Caravan Upgrade Cards have "once per turn" abilities, they become available again. This phase is automatic and requires no decisions.

**Phase 2 — Actions (Mandatory to attempt; you must spend at least 1 AP).** Spend your 3 Action Points on any combination of actions from the Action Catalog below. You may perform the same action multiple times in a turn if you can afford it. You must spend at least 1 AP during this phase (you cannot pass your entire turn). Any unspent AP at the end of this phase are lost; they do not carry over.

**Phase 3 — Discard Check (Mandatory if over limit).** Check your hand size. Your hand limit is 7 Spice Cards (this can be increased by upgrades). If you hold more than your hand limit, you must discard down to the limit. Discarded Spice Cards go face-up to a discard pile beside the Spice Deck.

**After all players have completed their turns, the End-of-Round Phase occurs:**

**End-of-Round Phase (Shared, Mandatory).**
1. Refill the Bazaar Display back up to 5 cards by drawing from the Spice Deck. If the Spice Deck is empty, shuffle the discard pile to form a new Spice Deck.
2. If fewer than 3 Royal Order Cards are in the Available Orders row, draw from the Royal Order Deck until there are 3 again. If the Royal Order Deck is empty, do not refill.
3. If fewer than 4 Caravan Upgrade Cards are in the Upgrade Market, draw from the Upgrade Deck until there are 4. If the Upgrade Deck is empty, do not refill.
4. Advance the Round Marker one space forward on the round track.
5. Pass the First Player Token one player to the left. That player will go first next round.

## B) ACTION CATALOG

### Action 1: Move (Cost: 1–2 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2 (Actions), at any point. No prerequisites.

**Cost:** 1 AP to move along a Risky or Perilous route. 2 AP to move along a Safe route.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare which adjacent location node you are moving to and which connecting route you will use (if multiple routes connect the same two nodes, you choose one).
2. Pay the AP cost by advancing your AP Tracker.
3. Move your Caravan Meeple to the destination node.
4. If the route was **Safe**, your move is complete with no further effect.
5. If the route was **Risky**, draw 1 card from the Hazard Deck and resolve it immediately (see Section E for Hazard card types).
6. If the route was **Perilous**, draw 2 cards from the Hazard Deck and resolve them both in the order drawn.

**Restrictions and limits:** You may move multiple times in a single turn if you have the AP. You may move back to a node you just left. You cannot move to a non-adjacent node in a single Move action — you must move one connection at a time.

**Example:** Bob's caravan is in Samarkand. He wants to reach Tabriz, which is two nodes away. The route from Samarkand to Merv is Risky (1 AP), and the route from Merv to Tabriz is Safe (2 AP). Bob spends 1 AP to move to Merv, draws a Hazard card (which turns out to be "Clear Skies" — no effect), then spends his remaining 2 AP to move safely from Merv to Tabriz. He has used all 3 AP this turn.

---

### Action 2: Buy Spices at the Bazaar (Cost: 1 AP + Coins)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your Caravan Meeple must be on a location node that has a Bazaar Token on it. (At the start of the game, the following locations have Bazaar Tokens: Kashgar, Samarkand, Merv, Tabriz, Antioch, and Xi'an — 6 of the 12 nodes. Bazaar Tokens are never moved or removed during the game.)

**Cost:** 1 AP plus 1 Coin per Spice Card purchased.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare that you are buying spices. Pay 1 AP (this single AP cost covers the entire Buy action regardless of how many cards you buy).
2. Choose any number of Spice Cards from the Bazaar Display. For each card you take, pay 1 Coin to the general supply.
3. Add the purchased cards to your hand.
4. Do NOT refill the Bazaar Display now — it refills during the End-of-Round Phase. (The display may have fewer than 5 cards for the remainder of the round.)

**Restrictions and limits:** You may only perform the Buy Spices action once per turn. You cannot buy more cards than you can afford in Coins. You cannot buy cards if the Bazaar Display is empty. You must be on a Bazaar node.

**Example:** Alice is on Samarkand (a Bazaar node). The Bazaar Display shows: Saffron, Silk, Cardamom, Silk, Cinnamon. She spends 1 AP and 3 Coins to buy the Saffron, Silk, and Cardamom cards, adding them to her hand. The display now has only 2 cards remaining (Silk and Cinnamon) until the End-of-Round refill.

---

### Action 3: Trade with a Rival (Cost: 1 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your Caravan Meeple must be on the same location node as at least one other player's Caravan Meeple.

**Cost:** 1 AP (only the active player pays AP; the trade partner pays nothing from their AP).

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare that you wish to trade and name which player you want to trade with (they must be on your node).
2. Negotiate openly. Both players may show or describe cards from their hands. You must trade at least 1 Spice Card for at least 1 Spice Card. Coins may also be included on either side of the deal to sweeten it. There is no required exchange ratio — any deal both players agree to is valid.
3. Once both players verbally agree to exact terms, the trade is executed simultaneously: both players hand over the agreed cards and/or Coins at the same time.
4. If no agreement can be reached, the action still costs 1 AP but nothing is exchanged.

**Restrictions and limits:** You may perform the Trade action multiple times per turn (with the same or different opponents), paying 1 AP each time. You cannot trade Royal Order Cards, Caravan Upgrade Cards, or the First Player Token. The trade partner cannot refuse to negotiate, but they can refuse any deal (resulting in no exchange). You cannot trade with yourself.

**Example:** Charlie and Alice are both on Merv. Charlie spends 1 AP to initiate a trade. He offers Alice 2 Cardamom cards in exchange for 1 Silk card and 1 Coin. Alice agrees. They swap the cards and Coin simultaneously.

---

### Action 4: Fulfill a Royal Order (Cost: 1 AP + Spice Cards)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your Caravan Meeple must be on the location node labeled "The Khan's Palace" (the node marked with a crown symbol on the board). You must hold the exact combination of Spice Cards listed on the Royal Order Card you wish to fulfill.

**Cost:** 1 AP plus the Spice Cards specified on the Royal Order Card.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare which Available Royal Order Card you are fulfilling.
2. Reveal the required Spice Cards from your hand and discard them face-up to the Spice discard pile.
3. Take the Royal Order Card and place it face-up on your Player Board in the "Completed Orders" area.
4. Receive the Coin bonus printed on the Royal Order Card (take that many Coins from the supply). Bronze orders pay 1 Coin, Silver orders pay 2 Coins, Gold orders pay 3 Coins.
5. If there are now fewer than 3 Available Orders, draw a replacement from the Royal Order Deck immediately.

**Restrictions and limits:** You may fulfill multiple Royal Orders in a single turn if you have the AP and the cards for each. You must be at The Khan's Palace. You cannot partially fulfill an order. If no Available Orders match what you can deliver, you cannot perform this action.

**Example:** Alice is at The Khan's Palace and holds 2 Saffron, 1 Cinnamon, and 1 Silk in her hand. One of the Available Orders (a Silver tier) requires exactly 2 Saffron + 1 Cinnamon + 1 Silk and is worth 6 Prestige Points. She spends 1 AP, discards those 4 Spice Cards, places the Silver Order on her Player Board, and collects 2 Coins as the Silver bonus.

---

### Action 5: Purchase a Caravan Upgrade (Cost: 1 AP + Coins)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your Caravan Meeple must be on any location node with a Bazaar Token (the same nodes where you can buy spices).

**Cost:** 1 AP plus the Coin cost printed on the Upgrade Card (ranges from 2 to 5 Coins).

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Choose one of the face-up Caravan Upgrade Cards from the Upgrade Market.
2. Pay its Coin cost to the general supply.
3. Place the Upgrade Card on your Player Board in one of the 3 Upgrade Slots. If all 3 slots are already full, you must either discard one of your existing upgrades (return it to the box, it is out of the game) to make room, or cancel this action (you still lose the 1 AP but get your Coins back).
4. The upgrade's effect is active immediately.

**Restrictions and limits:** Maximum 3 Upgrade Cards per player at any time. You may purchase multiple upgrades in a single turn if you have the AP and Coins. The Upgrade Market is not refilled until the End-of-Round Phase.

**Example:** Bob is on Tabriz (a Bazaar node). The Upgrade Market includes a "Reinforced Wagon" (cost: 3 Coins, effect: +2 hand limit). Bob spends 1 AP and 3 Coins, takes the Reinforced Wagon, and places it on his Player Board. His hand limit increases from 7 to 9 for the rest of the game.

---

### Action 6: Forage (Cost: 1 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Can be performed on any location node, including nodes without Bazaar Tokens.

**Cost:** 1 AP.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Draw 2 cards from the top of the Spice Deck.
2. Keep 1 card and place the other on the bottom of the Spice Deck (not the discard pile).

**Restrictions and limits:** You may Forage multiple times per turn if you have the AP. This action is available everywhere and has no Coin cost, making it the fallback way to gather resources when you are far from a Bazaar or low on funds.

**Example:** Charlie is on Dunhuang, which has no Bazaar Token. He spends 1 AP to Forage, drawing a Silk and a Cinnamon. He keeps the Silk (which he needs for an order) and places the Cinnamon on the bottom of the Spice Deck.

---

### Action 7: Rest and Resupply (Cost: 1 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Can be performed on any location node.

**Cost:** 1 AP.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Gain 2 Coins from the general supply.

**Restrictions and limits:** You may perform Rest and Resupply only once per turn.

**Example:** Alice is low on funds after a costly shopping spree. She spends 1 AP on Rest and Resupply, gaining 2 Coins. She still has 2 AP left for other actions.

## C) PLAYER INTERACTION

### Trading

Direct resource exchange between players is handled through the Trade action described above (Action 3). Trading is entirely voluntary — no player can be forced to accept a deal. However, since the trade initiator pays 1 AP regardless of outcome, there is a real cost to opening negotiations that fail. Shrewd players may refuse trades specifically to waste an opponent's AP.

### Competitive Bazaar Access

All players draw from the same Bazaar Display. When a player buys spices, the display shrinks and does not refill until the End-of-Round Phase. This means early buyers in a round have the best selection, while later players may find the display stripped of key resources. Turn order therefore matters significantly, and the First Player Token rotation ensures no one player always gets first pick.

### Order Racing

Only one player can fulfill each Royal Order Card. Since there are only 3 Available Orders visible at any time, players directly compete to reach The Khan's Palace first with the right combination of spices. If you see an opponent collecting Saffron and a high-value Saffron-heavy order is available, you face a choice: race to fulfill it yourself, or pivot to a different order they cannot easily complete.

### Route Blocking

While multiple caravans may occupy the same node, certain Hazard Cards (specifically "Bandit Ambush") have increased severity when multiple caravans are nearby, as bandits target the richest-looking group. Occupying the same space as a rival at the wrong moment can be costly.

### Denial and Hate-Drafting

When purchasing from the Bazaar Display or the Upgrade Market, players may deliberately buy cards they don't need in order to deny them to opponents. Since Bazaar cards cost only 1 Coin each, denial purchases are cheap but consume precious hand space.

## D) RESOURCE FLOW

### Gaining Resources

Spice Cards enter players' hands through three channels: buying from the Bazaar Display (Action 2), Foraging from the Spice Deck (Action 6), or receiving cards through trades (Action 3). Some Hazard Cards also grant bonus spice cards as a lucky find.

Coins are gained through: starting supply (3 Coins per player), the Rest and Resupply action (2 Coins), Coin bonuses from fulfilling Royal Orders (1–3 Coins depending on tier), and occasional Hazard Card rewards.

### Spending Resources

Spice Cards are spent (discarded) to fulfill Royal Orders. They are never spent on any other action. Coins are spent to buy Spice Cards at the Bazaar (1 Coin each), to purchase Caravan Upgrades (2–5 Coins each), and occasionally lost to Hazard Card penalties.

### Losing Resources

Hazard Cards are the primary way resources are involuntarily lost. Bandit Ambush cards force the player to discard Spice Cards or Coins. Sandstorm cards can force a player to discard their entire most-recently-purchased set of spice cards if they bought any this turn.

### Hand Limit

The default hand limit is 7 Spice Cards. The Reinforced Wagon upgrade increases this to 9. There is no limit on the number of Coins a player can hold. During Phase 3 (Discard Check), any player over the hand limit must discard down. The player chooses which cards to discard.

### Spice Deck Recycling

When the Spice Deck is empty, immediately shuffle the discard pile to form a new Spice Deck. This ensures spices are always available, though the composition of the deck shifts as orders are fulfilled and cards are removed from circulation via the bottom-of-deck Forage mechanic.

### There is no Persistent Market Pricing

Spice prices at the Bazaar are always fixed at 1 Coin per card. The scarcity comes from the limited Bazaar Display (only 5 cards, refreshed once per round), not from fluctuating prices.

## E) SPECIAL RULES

### Hazard Card Types

The Hazard Deck contains 30 cards divided into the following types:

**Bandit Ambush (8 cards).** Effect: Discard 1 random Spice Card from your hand. If you have no Spice Cards, lose 1 Coin instead. If you have neither, no effect. The "Armed Escort" upgrade negates this card entirely.

**Sandstorm (6 cards).** Effect: Your movement ends immediately. If you were moving through multiple nodes in one turn, you stop at the node you just entered and may not move again this turn (any remaining AP can be used for non-movement actions). The "Desert Navigator" upgrade allows you to ignore this effect and continue moving.

**Broken Axle (5 cards).** Effect: Lose 1 AP immediately (reduce your remaining AP for this turn by 1; if you have 0 AP remaining, this has no effect beyond the unlucky draw). The "Reinforced Wagon" upgrade negates this card.

**Lucky Find (6 cards).** Effect: Draw 1 Spice Card from the top of the Spice Deck and add it to your hand. This is always a welcome surprise.

**Oasis Rest (3 cards).** Effect: Gain 2 Coins from the supply. A respite on the harsh road.

**Toll Gate (2 cards).** Effect: Pay 2 Coins to the supply. If you cannot pay 2 Coins, pay whatever Coins you have and discard 1 Spice Card from your hand (your choice). If you have neither Coins nor Spice Cards, no effect.

### Caravan Upgrade Card Types

There are 4 distinct types of Caravan Upgrade, with 4 copies of each in the deck (16 total):

**Reinforced Wagon (Cost: 3 Coins).** Passive effect: Your hand limit increases by 2 (from 7 to 9). Additionally, "Broken Axle" Hazard Cards have no effect on you.

**Armed Escort (Cost: 4 Coins).** Passive effect: "Bandit Ambush" Hazard Cards have no effect on you. Additionally, when you draw a Bandit Ambush, you may instead treat it as a Lucky Find (draw 1 Spice Card) — your guards capture the bandits' stash.

**Desert Navigator (Cost: 3 Coins).** Passive effect: "Sandstorm" Hazard Cards have no effect on you. Additionally, Perilous routes cost 0 AP instead of 1 AP for you (you still draw 2 Hazard Cards, but you save the AP).

**Merchant's Seal (Cost: 5 Coins).** Passive effect: When you fulfill a Royal Order, gain 1 additional Coin beyond the normal tier bonus. Additionally, once per turn when you Buy Spices, you may take 1 card from the Bazaar Display for free (0 Coins) as part of the same Buy action.

### Royal Order Card Details

Each Royal Order Card specifies a required set of Spice Cards and a Prestige Point value:

**Bronze Tier (8 cards, requirements and values):**
- 2 of any single spice type → 2 Prestige Points
- 1 Saffron + 1 Cardamom → 2 Prestige Points
- 1 Cinnamon + 1 Silk → 2 Prestige Points
- 3 of any single spice type → 3 Prestige Points
- 1 Saffron + 1 Cinnamon → 2 Prestige Points
- 1 Cardamom + 1 Silk → 2 Prestige Points
- 2 Saffron + 1 Cardamom → 3 Prestige Points
- 2 Silk + 1 Cinnamon → 3 Prestige Points

**Silver Tier (8 cards):**
- 2 Saffron + 1 Cinnamon + 1 Silk → 6 Prestige Points
- 2 Cardamom + 2 Cinnamon → 6 Prestige Points
- 1 of each type (Saffron, Cardamom, Cinnamon, Silk) → 5 Prestige Points
- 3 Saffron + 1 Cardamom → 7 Prestige Points
- 2 Silk + 2 Saffron → 6 Prestige Points
- 3 Cinnamon + 1 Silk → 7 Prestige Points
- 2 Cardamom + 1 Saffron + 1 Cinnamon → 6 Prestige Points
- 3 Silk + 1 Saffron → 7 Prestige Points

**Gold Tier (8 cards):**
- 2 of each type (8 cards total) → 12 Prestige Points
- 3 Saffron + 2 Cinnamon → 10 Prestige Points
- 3 Silk + 2 Cardamom → 10 Prestige Points
- 4 of any single type + 1 of any other → 11 Prestige Points
- 3 Cardamom + 2 Saffron + 1 Silk → 11 Prestige Points

- 2 Saffron + 2 Cardamom + 2 Cinnamon → 11 Prestige Points

## F) END GAME

### Game End Trigger

The game ends immediately at the conclusion of the round in which **any one** of the following conditions is met:

1. **The Round Marker reaches space 10.** After completing Round 10, the game is over.
2. **Any single player has fulfilled 5 or more Royal Orders.** The current round is completed (all remaining players take their turns), and then the game ends.
3. **The Royal Order Deck is empty AND all 3 Available Order slots are empty** (all orders have been fulfilled or there are none left to display). The current round is completed, and then the game ends.

### Scoring

When the game ends, each player totals their score as follows:

- **Prestige Points from completed Royal Orders:** Add up the Prestige Points printed on every Royal Order Card on your Player Board. This is the primary source of points.
- **Remaining Spice Cards:** 1 point per 3 Spice Cards remaining in hand (rounded down). For example, 5 remaining cards = 1 point; 7 remaining cards = 2 points.
- **Remaining Coins:** 1 point per 4 Coins remaining (rounded down).
- **Caravan Upgrades:** 1 point per Caravan Upgrade Card on your Player Board.
- **Order Diversity Bonus:** If you have completed at least 1 order from each of the three tiers (Bronze, Silver, and Gold), gain a bonus of 5 Prestige Points.

### Tiebreaker

If two or more players are tied on total Prestige Points, apply the following tiebreakers in order:

1. The tied player with more completed Royal Orders wins.
2. If still tied, the player with more Gold-tier orders wins.
3. If still tied, the player with more total Spice Cards remaining in hand wins.
4. If still tied, the players share the victory.

## G) EXAMPLE ROUND

This example demonstrates Round 3 of a 3-player game with Alice, Bob, and Charlie. Alice holds the First Player Token.

**Current board state:**
- Bazaar Display: Saffron, Cinnamon, Silk, Cardamom, Saffron
- Available Royal Orders: Bronze "2 Saffron" (2 PP), Silver "2 Saffron + 1 Cinnamon + 1 Silk" (6 PP), Bronze "1 Cinnamon + 1 Silk" (2 PP)
- Upgrade Market: Armed Escort (4 Coins), Desert Navigator (3 Coins), Reinforced Wagon (3 Coins), Merchant's Seal (5 Coins)

---

### Alice's Turn

**Location:** Samarkand. **Hand:** Saffron, Saffron, Cardamom, Silk. **Coins:** 4. **Upgrades:** None.

**Phase 1 — Refresh.** Alice resets her AP to 3.

**Phase 2 — Actions.**

*Action 1: Move from Samarkand to Merv (Safe route, 2 AP).* Alice pays 2 AP and moves her meeple from Samarkand to Merv along the Safe (green) route. No Hazard draw required. She has 1 AP remaining.

*Action 2: Buy Spices at the Bazaar (1 AP + Coins).* Merv is a Bazaar node. Alice spends her last 1 AP and pays 2 Coins to buy the Cinnamon and the first Saffron from the Bazaar Display. Her hand is now: Saffron, Saffron, Saffron, Cardamom, Silk, Cinnamon. Her Coins drop to 2. The Bazaar Display is now: Silk, Cardamom, Saffron (3 cards remaining).

**Phase 3 — Discard Check.** Alice has 6 cards in hand (limit 7). No discard needed.

Alice is collecting for the Silver order (2 Saffron + 1 Cinnamon + 1 Silk = 6 PP). She already has the cards, but she needs to reach The Khan's Palace, which is 2 Safe-route moves away via Tabriz. She plans to deliver next turn.

---

**Location:** Kashgar. **Hand:** Cardamom, Cardamom, Cinnamon. **Coins:** 5. **Upgrades:** None.

**Phase 1 — Refresh.** Bob resets his AP to 3.

**Phase 2 — Actions.**

*Action 1: Move from Kashgar to Balkh (Perilous route, 1 AP).* Bob decides to take the dangerous shortcut. He pays 1 AP and moves to Balkh. Because the route is Perilous, he draws 2 Hazard Cards:
- First card: **Bandit Ambush.** Bob must discard 1 random Spice Card. He shuffles his hand face-down, and draws out his Cinnamon card, which goes to the discard pile. His hand is now: Cardamom, Cardamom.
- Second card: **Lucky Find.** Bob draws 1 Spice Card from the Spice Deck: it's a Silk. His hand is now: Cardamom, Cardamom, Silk.

Net result of the perilous journey: lost a Cinnamon, gained a Silk. Acceptable.

*Action 2: Move from Balkh to Merv (Risky route, 1 AP).* Bob pays 1 AP and moves to Merv. He draws 1 Hazard Card: **Clear Skies** (Lucky Find). He draws 1 Spice Card from the deck: another Cardamom. Hand is now: Cardamom, Cardamom, Silk, Cardamom. He has 1 AP remaining.

*Action 3: Purchase a Caravan Upgrade (1 AP + Coins).* Bob is on Merv, a Bazaar node. He decides to invest in the Armed Escort (cost: 4 Coins) to protect against future bandit ambushes. He spends 1 AP and 4 Coins, takes the Armed Escort card, and places it on his Player Board. His Coins drop to 1. From now on, Bandit Ambush cards have no effect on Bob — and he can treat them as Lucky Finds instead.

**Phase 3 — Discard Check.** Bob has 4 cards in hand (limit 7). No discard needed.
