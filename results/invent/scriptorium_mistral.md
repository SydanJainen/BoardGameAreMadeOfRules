# Illuminated Paths

## Theme
In the heart of medieval Europe, you are the master of a renowned scriptorium. Your task is to create illuminated manuscripts that will earn you fame and fortune. Recruit monks with unique abilities, collect rare pigments from distant lands, and produce manuscripts for demanding patrons. As your workshop grows, so does your reputation. The path to greatness is illuminated by your works, but only one scriptorium can become the most revered in all of Christendom.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Action Card Drafting:** At the start of each round, players draft action cards from a shared deck. Each card represents an activity in your scriptorium such as recruiting monks, collecting pigments, producing manuscripts, or improving your workshop. Players prioritize their choices based on their current strategy and the available options.
   - Decisions: Which cards to draft and play based on personal goals and the actions others might take.

2. **Engine Building:** Your scriptorium improves over time as you produce more manuscripts. Each completed manuscript provides permanent upgrades such as additional monks, increased pigment storage, or improved production efficiency.
   - Decisions: How to balance immediate gains (e.g., recruiting more monks) versus long-term improvements (e.g., increasing your pigment storage).

3. **Pigment Market:** The availability and price of pigments change each round based on supply and demand. Players can buy pigments at the current market price or invest in controlling the market by becoming the dominant buyer.
   - Decisions: Whether to buy pigments now at potentially high prices, wait for cheaper opportunities later, or manipulate the market to your advantage.

4. **Patron Favor:** Satisfying patrons' requests grants you influence points, which are spent to gain certain advantages like drafting extra cards, drawing additional monks, or securing specific pigments.
   - Decisions: Which patrons to prioritize based on their requests and rewards, balancing immediate gains with long-term strategies.

5. **Monk Abilities:** Each monk recruited provides a unique ability that can be activated once per round at varying costs (e.g., discarding cards, spending influence). These abilities range from gaining extra resources to influencing the draft or manipulating the market.
   - Decisions: When and how to use each monk's ability based on current needs and available resources.
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: 40-60 minutes

## Components
- - 1 Game Board (modular hex tiles for scriptorium layout)
- 4 Player Boards (representing your scriptorium)
- 60 Action Cards (12 per type: Recruitment
- Production
- Market
- Workshop Improvements
- Patron Favor)
- 80 Monk Cards (20 unique monks with abilities)
- 60 Pigment Tokens (four colors: Red
- Blue
- Green
- Gold)
- 40 Influence Markers
- 1 Turn Marker
- 1 Round Marker
- 1 Scorepad and Pencil per player

## Rules
**SETUP:**

1. Assemble the game board by placing hex tiles in a grid (4x5 or 3x6 for different player counts).
2. Shuffle the action deck and deal three cards to each player.
3. Place the pigment market on the board, with initial quantities of each color set as indicated.
4. Give each player their scriptorium board and assign them a starting position on the board.
5. Select a first player randomly.

**RULES:**

**A) TURN STRUCTURE:**
1. **Prepare for Round:** Move the round marker to the next space on the track.
2. **Draft Action Cards:** Players take turns drafting action cards from the shared deck until all players have three cards.
3. **Plan Actions:** Players discuss (if desired) and plan their actions for the round.
4. **Activate Monks:** Players activate their monks' abilities, if desired.
5. **Market Phase:** The pigment market is updated based on demand, and players can buy or sell pigments.
6. **Production Phase:** Players produce manuscripts according to their current capabilities.
7. **Patron Favor:** Players gain influence points from satisfied patrons and spend them as needed.
8. **End of Round:** Check for endgame conditions.

**B) ACTION CATALOG:**

*Action titles and mechanics are simplified for brevity.*

1. **Recruit Monk**
   - Phase: Recruitment
   - Cost: 2 Influence
   - Resolution: Draw a monk card, place it on your scriptorium board, and gain its ability.
   - Limit: Once per round.

2. **Collect Pigments**
   - Phase: Market
   - Cost: Discard one action card or pay 1 Influence.
   - Resolution: Gain one pigment token of the chosen color from the supply.
   - Limit: Once per round, unless a monk ability allows more.

3. **Produce Manuscript**
   - Phase: Production
   - Cost: Spend pigments equal to your production capability (starts at 2).
   - Resolution: Place produced manuscripts on your board and gain influence points based on the number of completed manuscripts.
   - Limit: Equal to your current production capability.

4. **Improve Workshop**
   - Phase: Workshop Improvements
   - Cost: Discard two action cards or spend 2 Influence.
   - Resolution: Choose one permanent upgrade (e.g., +1 monk, +1 pigment storage, +1 production efficiency).
   - Limit: Once per round.

5. **Gain Patron Favor**
   - Phase: Patron Favor
   - Cost: Spend influence points equal to the patron's request.
   - Resolution: Gain influence points and/or other benefits as indicated on the patron card.
   - Limit: Once per round, unless a monk ability allows more.

**C) PLAYER INTERACTION:**
- Players do not trade directly but can manipulate the pigment market indirectly by buying or selling pigments.
- There is no combat or conflict resolution; players compete indirectly through drafting action cards and gaining influence points.
- Area control exists on the board for scriptorium placement, with adjacency bonuses granted for workshop upgrades.

**D) RESOURCE FLOW:**
- Pigments are gained during the Market Phase and spent during Production.
- Influence is gained from completed manuscripts and spending on patrons' requests.
- The pigment market refreshes at the end of each round based on supply and demand.

**E) SPECIAL RULES:**
- *Card Effects:* Action cards have various effects (e.g., gaining extra pigments, influencing the draft), while monk abilities have individual descriptions.
- *Environmental Effects:* Certain board locations may offer adjacency bonuses or other advantages for workshop improvements.
- *Event Resolution:* Some action cards trigger special events with unique resolutions.

**F) END GAME:**
- The game ends immediately when one player gains a set number of influence points (based on player count: 10 in a two-player game, 8 in three-player games, and 6 in four-player games).
- Players score additional points for completed manuscripts (1 point each) and leftover pigments (1 point per two pigments).
- In case of a tie, the player with the most completed manuscripts wins. If still tied, the player closest to the starting position on the board wins.

**G) EXAMPLE ROUND:**

* Players: Alice, Bob, Carol
* Round 1:
	+ Draft Action Cards: Alice drafts "Recruit Monk," "Collect Pigments (Green)," and "Gain Patron Favor." Bob drafts "Produce Manuscript," "Improve Workshop," and "Gain Patron Favor." Carol drafts "Collect Pigments (Blue)" x2.
	+ Activate Monks: None yet recruited.
	+ Market Phase: Alice collects one Green pigment, Bob collects two Blue pigments, Carol collects three Blue pigments. The market updates based on supply and demand.
	+ Production Phase: Bob produces one manuscript using his starting pigments (Green 1, Blue 1), gaining 2 Influence points.
	+ Patron Favor: Alice spends 3 Influence to gain 5 Influence from a patron. Bob spends 2 Influence for no benefit this round. Carol gains 1 Influence without spending any.
* Remaining phases proceed as outlined above.

This design document provides all necessary information for players to set up, learn, and play Illuminated Paths with zero ambiguity. The game combines engine-building mechanics with card drafting and medieval theme immersion, creating a unique and engaging experience for players.
