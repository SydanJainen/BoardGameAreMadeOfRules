# Scriptorium

## Theme

In the medieval monasteries of Europe, the scriptorium was the heart of knowledge preservation. Players are Head Scribes competing to run the most prestigious scriptorium in Christendom. You recruit monks with specialized skills -- calligraphers, illuminators, bookbinders, and herbalists who prepare inks and pigments. Wealthy patrons from the Church, nobility, and universities commission manuscripts, each demanding specific qualities. You must manage your parchment supply, acquire rare pigments, train your monks, and fulfill commissions before rival scriptoria claim the glory. The scriptorium that produces the most celebrated body of work earns the patronage of the Archbishop and wins the game.

## Game Details

- **Mechanics**: Worker Placement, Engine Building, Set Collection, Hand Management, Contract Fulfillment
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: 40-60 minutes

## Components

- 1 Central Board (pigment market with 5 slots, patron row with 4 slots, season track with 8 rounds)
- 4 Player Boards (individual scriptorium with 4 monk slots, 1 workshop area, parchment/gold tracks)
- 40 Monk Cards (10 per type: Calligrapher, Illuminator, Bookbinder, Herbalist)
- 30 Patron Cards (commissions with requirements and victory point rewards)
- 60 Pigment Tokens (12 each of 5 colors: Lapis Blue, Vermillion Red, Gold Leaf, Verdigris Green, Tyrian Purple)
- 20 Parchment Tokens
- 30 Gold Coins (values of 1 and 3)
- 16 Manuscript Tiles (completed works, double-sided: basic / masterwork)
- 12 Event Cards (seasonal events affecting each round)
- 20 Tool Tokens (5 types: quill, stylus, burnisher, ruling frame, knife)
- 4 Head Scribe Meeples (1 per player color)
- 1 Archbishop Token (first-player marker)
- 1 Scoring Pad

## Rules

### SETUP

1. Place the Central Board in the center of the table. Shuffle the Event Cards and place them face-down on the season track, revealing the top card for Round 1.
2. Shuffle the Monk Cards and deal 3 face-up to a recruitment row beside the board. Place the rest as a draw deck.
3. Shuffle the Patron Cards and deal 4 face-up to the patron row on the board. Place the rest as a draw deck.
4. Fill the pigment market by drawing 2 random Pigment Tokens per slot (10 tokens total visible).
5. Each player takes 1 Player Board, 1 Head Scribe Meeple, 3 Gold Coins, 2 Parchment Tokens, and 1 starting Monk Card (drawn randomly; if the monk type is a duplicate among starting players, redraw).
6. Place your starting Monk Card on the first monk slot of your Player Board.
7. The player who most recently visited a library takes the Archbishop Token and goes first. Play proceeds clockwise.

### A) TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over **8 rounds** (representing two years of monastic labor, one season per round). Each round has three phases:

1. **Event Phase**: Reveal the current round's Event Card. Apply its effect immediately (it persists for the entire round). Events may alter pigment costs, grant bonus resources, restrict certain actions, or trigger special opportunities.

2. **Action Phase**: Beginning with the first player, each player takes **2 actions** on their turn (may be the same action twice). Players take turns clockwise until all players have acted.

3. **Production Phase**: After all players have taken their actions, each player's scriptorium produces. Every monk in your scriptorium with the required tools and pigments assigned to them generates their output (see Monk Abilities). Then advance the season marker.

### B) ACTION CATALOG

On your turn, choose 2 of the following actions (you may repeat an action):

1. **Recruit a Monk**: Pay the recruitment cost shown on a face-up Monk Card in the recruitment row (typically 2-4 Gold) and place it on an empty monk slot on your Player Board. You may have a maximum of 4 monks. Refill the recruitment row immediately from the deck. If no slot is available, you must first Dismiss a monk (return it to the bottom of the deck; no refund).

2. **Acquire Pigments**: Take up to 3 Pigment Tokens from the pigment market. You may take any combination of colors, but taking 2 or more of the same color in one action costs 1 Gold per duplicate beyond the first. Acquired pigments go to your workshop area.

3. **Claim a Patron**: Take 1 face-up Patron Card from the patron row and place it beside your Player Board. You may hold a maximum of 3 unfulfilled Patron Cards. Refill the patron row immediately.

4. **Fulfill a Commission**: If you have all the pigments, parchment, and monk types required by one of your Patron Cards, discard the required resources and flip the Patron Card face-down as a completed commission. Take the corresponding Manuscript Tile (basic side up). If the commission was fulfilled using 3 or more different pigment colors, flip the tile to the masterwork side (worth bonus VP).

5. **Gather Supplies**: Take either 2 Parchment Tokens or 3 Gold Coins from the general supply.

6. **Acquire a Tool**: Pay 2 Gold to take 1 Tool Token of your choice from the supply. Assign it to one of your monks immediately. Each monk can hold 1 tool. Tools provide persistent bonuses (see Tool Effects).

### C) PLAYER INTERACTION

1. **Competitive Drafting**: The pigment market, monk recruitment row, and patron row are shared pools. Taking a resource denies it to opponents. Timing your acquisitions is critical.

2. **Pigment Bidding**: When two or more players want the same Patron Card on the same round, the active player has priority. However, any other player may call a **Bidding War** by offering Gold. The patron goes to the highest bidder (minimum bid: 1 Gold above the active player's implicit bid of 0). The winner pays their bid to the supply. Bidding Wars can only be called once per patron per round.

3. **Trading**: On your turn, before or after your actions, you may propose one trade with another player. You may trade pigments, parchment, gold, or tools on a 1-for-1 or negotiated basis. Both players must agree. No monk trading is allowed.

4. **Blocking**: By claiming patrons you don't intend to fulfill, you can deny them to opponents -- but remember the 3-patron hand limit and the opportunity cost. Unfulfilled patrons score -2 VP each at game end.

### D) RESOURCE FLOW

**Gaining Resources**:
- Pigments: Acquired from the pigment market (action) or produced by Herbalist monks during the Production Phase (1 pigment of choice per Herbalist).
- Parchment: Via the Gather Supplies action (2 per action) or from certain Event Cards.
- Gold: Via Gather Supplies (3 per action), as bonus rewards from some Patron Cards, or produced by Bookbinder monks (1 Gold per Bookbinder during Production Phase).
- Tools: Purchased via the Acquire a Tool action.

**Spending Resources**:
- Gold: Recruiting monks, acquiring tools, pigment duplicates, bidding wars.
- Pigments + Parchment: Fulfilling commissions (specific combinations required per Patron Card).
- Tools: Spent once to acquire; permanently assigned to monks.

**Resource Limits**:
- Maximum 8 Pigment Tokens in your workshop at any time. Excess must be discarded.
- Maximum 6 Parchment Tokens. Excess must be discarded.
- No limit on Gold.

### E) SPECIAL RULES

**Monk Abilities** (activate during the Production Phase if the monk has no unfulfilled requirement):

| Monk Type | Production Ability | Tool Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| Calligrapher | Adds +1 VP to each manuscript completed this round | With Quill: +2 VP instead |
| Illuminator | You may treat 1 pigment as any color when fulfilling a commission this round | With Stylus: treat up to 2 pigments as wild |
| Bookbinder | Produces 1 Gold | With Burnisher: produces 2 Gold |
| Herbalist | Produces 1 Pigment Token of your choice | With Knife: produces 2 Pigment Tokens (may be different colors) |

**Tool Effects** (in addition to monk bonuses above):

| Tool | Standalone Effect |
|---|---|
| Quill | Calligrapher bonus (see above) |
| Stylus | Illuminator bonus (see above) |
| Burnisher | Bookbinder bonus (see above) |
| Knife | Herbalist bonus (see above) |
| Ruling Frame | Any monk: reduces parchment cost of commissions by 1 (minimum 1) |

**Masterwork Manuscripts**: When a commission is fulfilled using 3 or more different pigment colors, the Manuscript Tile is placed masterwork-side up. Masterwork manuscripts are worth their base VP value +3.

**Seasonal Events** (examples):

- *Spring Fair*: All pigments cost 1 less Gold this round (minimum 0).
- *Plague*: Each player must dismiss 1 monk at the start of this round (if able).
- *Royal Visit*: Completed commissions this round score +2 VP.
- *Ink Shortage*: The pigment market is not refilled this round.
- *Pilgrimage*: Each player may recruit 1 monk at half cost (round down, minimum 1).
- *Harsh Winter*: Gather Supplies yields 1 less of the chosen resource this round.

### F) WINNING CONDITION AND SCORING

The game ends after **8 rounds**. Players tally victory points:

| Source | VP Value |
|---|---|
| Basic Manuscript | VP printed on Patron Card (3-7 VP each) |
| Masterwork Manuscript | VP printed on Patron Card + 3 |
| Calligrapher bonuses | As accumulated during play |
| Set bonus: 1 of each monk type | 5 VP |
| Most completed manuscripts (majority) | 4 VP (split in ties) |
| Each unfulfilled Patron Card | -2 VP |
| Remaining Gold (every 3 coins) | 1 VP |
| Remaining Pigments (every 4 tokens) | 1 VP |

**Tiebreaker**: If tied, the player with more masterwork manuscripts wins. If still tied, the player with more monks in their scriptorium wins. If still tied, the player who held the Archbishop Token most recently wins.

### G) EXAMPLE ROUND

**Round 3 -- Event: Spring Fair** (all pigments cost 1 less Gold)

**Elena (Player 1, has: Calligrapher + Herbalist, 3 Gold, 2 Lapis Blue, 1 Vermillion Red, 2 Parchment, holds Patron Card "Psalter of St. Benedict" requiring 2 Blue + 1 Red + 2 Parchment for 5 VP)**:

- Action 1: *Acquire Pigments* -- takes 1 Lapis Blue from the market (free due to Spring Fair). She now has 3 Blue, 1 Red.
- Action 2: *Fulfill a Commission* -- spends 2 Lapis Blue + 1 Vermillion Red + 2 Parchment to complete the Psalter. Since she used only 2 different pigment colors, she takes the Manuscript Tile basic-side up (5 VP).
- Production Phase: Her Calligrapher adds +1 VP to the manuscript completed this round (now 6 VP total for the Psalter). Her Herbalist produces 1 Gold Leaf pigment token.

**Marco (Player 2, has: Illuminator + Bookbinder with Burnisher, 5 Gold, 1 Verdigris Green, 1 Parchment, no patron cards)**:

- Action 1: *Claim a Patron* -- takes "Chronicle of the Crusades" (requires 1 Green + 1 Purple + 1 Gold Leaf + 3 Parchment for 7 VP).
- Action 2: *Gather Supplies* -- takes 2 Parchment (now has 3 Parchment).
- Production Phase: His Illuminator has no commission to assist with this round (ability unused). His Bookbinder with Burnisher produces 2 Gold (now has 7 Gold).

Marco is building toward a high-value commission and accumulating gold for future monk recruitment, while Elena secured an early manuscript with a calligrapher bonus.
