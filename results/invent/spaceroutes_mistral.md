# Stellar Connexus

## Theme
In the far reaches of the cosmos lies the expansive galaxy of Veridian, where humanity has established a network of space stations connected by hyperlane routes. As CEOs of interstellar transportation corporations, players are tasked with expanding and optimizing these routes to facilitate trade, communication, and scientific research across the vast cosmic expanse. Secret mission objectives, fuel management, asteroid field navigation, and strategic alliances create an engaging and dynamic gaming experience in Stellar Connexus.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Route Building:**
   Players connect space stations by placing hexagonal route tiles, each with two edges allowing for branching pathways. Decision-making involves strategizing which stations to connect based on potential benefits from nearby asteroid fields, mission rewards, or strategic positioning.
   - *Tile Types:* Red (short-range, low fuel cost), blue (medium-range, moderate fuel cost), and yellow (long-range, high fuel cost).
   - *Route Limitations:* Players cannot create loops in their routes nor connect stations that are more than two tiles apart without an intermediary station or asteroid field.

2. **Fuel Management:**
   Shuttle fleets require fuel to traverse routes, gained from connected space stations and spent when connecting routes or completing missions.
   - *Income:* Each connected space station provides 1 fuel per turn, while activated navigation tool cards offer additional fuel income.
   - *Expenditure:* Connecting route tiles costs fuel equal to the tile's color; completing secret mission cards requires spending a set amount of fuel; moving shuttle fleets along routes costs 1 fuel per crossed edge.
   - *Storage:* Players can store up to 7 fuel tokens, discarding excess fuel at the end of their turn.

3. **Mission Objectives and Secret Agendas:**
   Each player receives five secret mission cards at the start of the game, rewarding specific routing achievements or other strategic goals like mining asteroids or forming alliances.
   - *Decision:* Players must prioritize missions based on rewards while keeping their intentions hidden from opponents until completing a card.
   - *Reveal & Complete:* Secret mission cards can be revealed and completed during the Action Phase, with some effects applying immediately (e.g., gaining victory points) and others triggering ongoing or end-game effects.
- **Players**: 2-5 players
- **Duration**: 60-90 minutes

## Rules
**Setup:**

1. Assemble the game board by placing hexagonal tiles in a symmetrical pattern.
2. Shuffle route tiles separately into red, blue, and yellow decks; place them face down near the board.
3. Each player selects a player mat and takes their corresponding shuttle fleet tokens.
4. Deal each player five secret mission cards (18 total); place remaining public mission cards face up on the table.
5. Distribute space station markers evenly across the galaxy map according to the setup diagram (3 stations per player for 2-3 players, 2 stations per player for 4-5 players).
6. Shuffle asteroid field tokens and place them randomly on the board following the setup guidelines.
7. Give each player 10 fuel tokens to start.
8. Determine the first player by having everyone choose a favorite space station; the player who chose the most popular station goes first.

**Turn Structure:**

*A) TURN STRUCTURE:*
Each turn consists of five phases:

1. **Income Phase:** Gain fuel from connected space stations and activated navigation tool cards (if any).
2. **Action Phase:** Perform three actions in any order, such as deploying shuttle fleets, connecting routes, mining asteroids, acquiring navigation tools, or completing missions.
3. **Event Phase:** Resolve ongoing events like asteroid field hazards, mission card effects, or end-game triggers.
4. **Movement Phase:** Move your shuttle fleet tokens along connected routes, spending fuel equal to the number of crossed edges.
5. **End Phase:** Discard down to 7 fuel tokens, pass the turn counter clockwise, and check for end-game conditions.

*B) ACTION CATALOG:*

1. **Deploy Shuttle Fleet**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: None
   - Resolution: Place one of your shuttle fleet tokens on any connected space station not occupied by another player's token.
   - Restriction: You cannot deploy a shuttle fleet on a station with two opponent tokens.

2. **Connect Route**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: Draw and place one route tile, spending fuel equal to its color (1 for red, 2 for blue, 3 for yellow).
   - Resolution: Place the tile connecting two adjacent space stations or asteroid fields with your shuttle fleet token on it. Gain fuel equal to its color upon placement.
   - Restriction: You cannot connect routes that loop back onto themselves.

3. **Mine Asteroid Field**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: Spend 2 fuel tokens, discard a navigation tool card (if any), pay another player 1 fuel token, or acquire the "Mining Drone" tool.
   - Resolution: Remove an asteroid field token from the board and gain its corresponding fuel bonus (1-3 fuel tokens). Draw an event card if available.
   - Restriction: You cannot mine asteroids without proper equipment; you must have at least one activated navigation tool card, discard a card, pay another player, or possess the "Mining Drone" tool.

4. **Acquire Navigation Tool**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: Spend 2 fuel tokens and draw a navigation tool card.
   - Resolution: Add the drawn card to your collection; activate its immediate effect (if any) and place it on your player mat. You can only have three activated navigation tool cards at a time.

5. **Complete Mission**
   - When: Action Phase or Event Phase
   - Cost: Spend fuel equal to the mission's requirement.
   - Resolution: Discard one of your secret mission cards and gain its reward (victory points, fuel, navigation tools, etc.). Some missions require additional actions or conditions to be met before completion.
   - Restriction: You cannot complete missions unless you have a shuttle fleet deployed on the required space station(s) or fulfill other specified prerequisites.

*C) PLAYER INTERACTION:*
Players indirectly compete by connecting routes, mining asteroids, completing missions, and forming alliances. Direct interaction includes:

- Trading fuel tokens with another player (they must agree).
- Blocking opponents from connecting certain routes or reaching specific stations.
- Stealing opponent's secret mission cards during the Action Phase for 2 fuel tokens.
- Forming temporary alliances by exchanging fuel tokens or offering mutual assistance.

*D) RESOURCE FLOW:*
Fuel is gained through connecting route tiles, mining asteroids, activating navigation tool cards, and receiving mission rewards. It's spent when deploying shuttle fleets, connecting routes, completing missions, moving along routes, and resolving some event cards.

*E) SPECIAL RULES:*

- **Card Effects:** Navigation tool cards provide immediate effects (like gaining extra fuel or protecting against asteroid hazards), ongoing benefits (placed on player mat), one-time bonuses, or end-game scoring modifiers.
- **Asteroid Hazards:** When a shuttle fleet crosses an asteroid field, the current player draws a hazard card (if available). They may discard a navigation tool card to avoid losing fuel or drawing another event card.

*F) END GAME:*
The game ends immediately when any of these conditions are met:

1. One player completes five secret mission cards.
2. The route tile deck runs out during a Connect Route action.
3. All asteroid fields have been mined.
4. A player reaches 20 victory points (VPs), triggering the end-game scoring phase.

Players score victory points based on completed missions, connected space stations, remaining fuel tokens, and navigation tool cards in hand. In case of a tie, the player with more connected stations wins. The solo play variant involves AI-controlled opponents and adjusted setup conditions for an engaging single-player experience.

*G) EXAMPLE ROUND:*
**Player 1 (Red):** Deploys shuttle fleet on Station A; connects Route Tile B-C (spending 2 fuel); mines Asteroid Field D (spending 2 fuel, discarding "Advanced Scanner"); gains 3 fuel from Income Phase.
**Player 2 (Blue):** Completes mission "Connect Stations E-F" (gaining 1 VP and drawing an event card; "Resource Scarcity" requires each player to discard a navigation tool card); deploys shuttle fleet on Station F; connects Route Tile G-H (spending 1 fuel).
**Player 3 (Yellow):** Acquires navigation tool card ("Pulse Drive"); gains 2 fuel from activated cards; moves shuttle fleet along Routes I-J-K, spending 3 fuel.
**Player 4 (Green):** Forms an alliance with Player 5 by trading 1 fuel token each; both deploy their shuttle fleets on Station L; connects Route Tile M-N (spending 2 fuel); completes mission "Form Alliance" (gaining 2 VPs).
**Player 5 (Purple):** Steals Player 3's secret mission card during the Action Phase, paying 2 fuel tokens.

This example demonstrates various actions, decision-making, and direct/indirect player interaction. Players strategically connect routes, manage fuel, complete missions, form alliances, and sabotage opponents' plans while considering the ever-changing galaxy map and event cards.

Stellar Connexus offers a rich, immersive gaming experience that combines strategic route-building with dynamic resource management, varied mission objectives, and engaging player interactions.
