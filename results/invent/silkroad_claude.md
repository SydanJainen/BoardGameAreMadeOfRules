# GAME NAME

**Silkroad: Merchants of the Eastern Trade**

---

# THEME

The year is 1200 CE. The great trade routes connecting Chang'an to Constantinople are alive with caravans carrying silk, spices, jade, and incense across deserts, mountain passes, and oasis cities. You are an ambitious merchant house leader, dispatching caravans along the Silk Road to acquire exotic goods, build a trading network, and fulfill lucrative royal orders from emperors, sultans, and khans who crave the riches of distant lands. But you are not alone on these roads. Rival merchants compete for the same scarce commodities at every caravanserai, bandits lurk in mountain passes, and seasonal dust storms can halt trade for weeks. Your success depends on reading the shifting markets, choosing the right routes, investing in your caravan's capacity, and delivering the right goods to the right courts before your rivals do. The merchant house that accumulates the most prestige through fulfilled orders and trade mastery will be remembered as the greatest trading dynasty of the Silk Road.

---

# MECHANICS

**Set Collection (Goods Acquisition).** Players collect Goods Cards from four commodity families — Silk, Spice, Jade, and Incense — to fulfill Royal Order cards that demand specific combinations. Orders range from simple two-card pairs to elaborate five-card sets worth significantly more prestige. The strategic tension lies in deciding whether to rush easy orders for quick points or accumulate rare high-value sets while opponents drain the market of what you need.

**Action Point Allowance (Caravan Operations).** Each turn, a player has exactly 3 Action Points to spend across a menu of actions: moving along trade routes, purchasing goods at caravanserais, trading with rival merchants, upgrading their caravan, or delivering orders at royal courts. Three AP is deliberately tight — players must constantly prioritize between acquiring goods, positioning their caravan, and racing to deliver before opponents claim the same orders.

**Route Network and Movement (The Trade Map).** The board depicts a network of 12 city nodes connected by trade routes of three danger levels: Guarded (safe but costly in AP), Open (standard), and Lawless (cheap in AP but hazardous). Movement choices shape which goods a player can access, since each caravanserai offers different commodity availability. Route selection creates a constant speed-versus-safety dilemma.

**Market Fluctuation (The Caravanserai).** A shared Goods Display of 6 face-up cards represents the available commodities at market. When players buy from this display, it shrinks and does not refill until the end of the round. Early buyers in a round get the best selection; late buyers may find key resources stripped. The display refreshes each round, creating a cyclical economy where timing purchases is as important as choosing what to buy.

**Push-Your-Luck (Hazard Navigation).** Moving along Open or Lawless routes forces players to draw from the Hazard Deck, risking bandit raids, dust storms, or broken wheels — but occasionally stumbling upon lucky finds or oasis resupply. Caravan upgrades can mitigate specific hazards, rewarding players who invest in preparation before venturing into dangerous territory.

# COMPONENTS

- 72 Goods Cards (18 per type: Silk, Spice, Jade, Incense)
- 24 Royal Order Cards (8 Bronze, 8 Silver, 8 Gold tier)
- 24 Hazard Cards
- 12 Caravan Upgrade Cards (4 types, 3 copies each)
- 1 Game Board (network map of 12 city nodes connected by trade routes, folding board approximately 50 cm x 50 cm)
- 4 Caravan Meeples (wooden camel-shaped pieces in Red, Blue, Green, Yellow)
- 4 Player Boards (individual tableaux showing cargo hold, upgrades, and completed orders)
- 4 Action Point Tracker Cubes (one per player color)
- 24 Coin Tokens (cardboard, 14x "1 Coin" and 10x "3 Coin")
- 1 Round Marker (wooden disc)
- 1 First Player Token (ornate compass-rose shaped wooden token)
- 4 Market Stall Tokens (placed on cities with active caravanserais)
- 1 Rulebook

# SETUP

1. **Unfold the game board** and place it in the center of the table. The board shows 12 named city nodes connected by trade routes colored green (Guarded), yellow (Open), or red (Lawless). A round track along the top edge shows rounds 1 through 8.

2. **Place the Round Marker** on the space marked "1" on the round track.

3. **Place Market Stall Tokens** on the 6 caravanserai cities: Chang'an, Kashgar, Samarkand, Merv, Tabriz, and Antioch. These tokens remain in place for the entire game.

4. **Prepare the Goods Deck.** Shuffle all 72 Goods Cards into a single face-down deck. Place it beside the board.

5. **Create the Goods Display.** Draw 6 cards from the Goods Deck and lay them face-up in a row. This is the Goods Display (the shared market).

6. **Prepare the Royal Order Deck.** Separate the 24 Royal Order Cards by tier (Bronze, Silver, Gold). Shuffle each stack independently. Place Gold face-down, then Silver face-down on top, then Bronze face-down on top. Draw the top 3 cards and place them face-up beside the board as the Available Orders.

7. **Prepare the Hazard Deck.** Shuffle all 24 Hazard Cards into a face-down deck beside the board.

8. **Prepare the Upgrade Market.** Shuffle all 12 Caravan Upgrade Cards. Deal 3 face-up in a row beside the board. Place the rest face-down as the Upgrade Deck.

9. **Distribute starting resources to each player.** Each player receives:
   - 1 Caravan Meeple in their chosen color.
   - 1 Player Board placed in front of them.
   - 1 Action Point Tracker cube (placed on "0" on their Player Board's AP track).
   - 3 Coins from the supply.
   - 2 Goods Cards drawn from the top of the Goods Deck (kept secret in hand).

10. **Place Caravan Meeples.** All players place their meeple on the city node labeled "Kashgar" (the starting city, marked with a star).

11. **Determine the first player.** The player who most recently traveled to a foreign country takes the First Player Token. If no one qualifies or players cannot agree, the youngest player takes it. Play proceeds clockwise.

# RULES

## A) TURN STRUCTURE

A game of Silkroad is played over a series of rounds. Each round consists of every player taking one turn in clockwise order, followed by a shared End-of-Round Phase. A single player's turn has these phases in strict order:

**Phase 1 — Refresh (Mandatory).** Reset your Action Point Tracker to 3. Any "once per turn" abilities on your Caravan Upgrade Cards become available again. This phase is automatic and requires no decisions.

**Phase 2 — Actions (Mandatory; you must spend at least 1 AP).** Spend your 3 Action Points on any combination of actions from the Action Catalog (Section B). You may perform the same action multiple times if you can afford it. You must spend at least 1 AP (you cannot pass your entire turn). Unspent AP at the end of this phase are lost and do not carry over.

**Phase 3 — Cargo Check (Mandatory if over limit).** Check your hand size. The default hand limit is 6 Goods Cards (upgradeable). If you hold more than your limit, discard down to the limit. You choose which cards to discard. Discarded cards go face-up to a discard pile beside the Goods Deck.

**After all players have taken their turns, the End-of-Round Phase occurs:**

**End-of-Round Phase (Shared, Mandatory).**
1. Refill the Goods Display back to 6 cards by drawing from the Goods Deck. If the Goods Deck is empty, shuffle the discard pile to form a new deck.
2. If fewer than 3 Royal Order Cards are in the Available Orders row, draw from the Royal Order Deck until there are 3. If the deck is empty, do not refill.
3. If fewer than 3 Caravan Upgrade Cards are in the Upgrade Market, draw from the Upgrade Deck until there are 3. If the deck is empty, do not refill.
4. Advance the Round Marker one space.
5. Pass the First Player Token one seat to the left.

---

## B) ACTION CATALOG

### Action 1: Move (Cost: 1–2 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2, at any point. No prerequisites.

**Cost:** 2 AP to move along a Guarded route. 1 AP to move along an Open or Lawless route.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare which adjacent city node you are moving to and which connecting route you will use.
2. Pay the AP cost by advancing your AP Tracker.
3. Move your Caravan Meeple to the destination node.
4. If the route was **Guarded**, your move is complete with no further effect.
5. If the route was **Open**, draw 1 card from the Hazard Deck and resolve it immediately (see Section E for Hazard types).
6. If the route was **Lawless**, draw 2 cards from the Hazard Deck and resolve both in draw order.

**Restrictions:** You may move multiple times per turn if you have AP. You may revisit nodes. Each Move action covers exactly one connection (no multi-hop moves).

**Example:** Fatima's caravan is in Samarkand. She wants to reach Merv. The route from Samarkand to Merv is Open (1 AP). She pays 1 AP, moves to Merv, and draws 1 Hazard Card: "Clear Road" (no effect). She has 2 AP remaining.

---

### Action 2: Buy Goods at a Caravanserai (Cost: 1 AP + Coins)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your meeple must be on a city with a Market Stall Token (Chang'an, Kashgar, Samarkand, Merv, Tabriz, or Antioch).

**Cost:** 1 AP plus 1 Coin per Goods Card purchased.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare you are buying goods. Pay 1 AP (this covers the entire Buy action regardless of quantity).
2. Choose any number of Goods Cards from the Goods Display. For each card, pay 1 Coin to the supply.
3. Add purchased cards to your hand.
4. The Display is NOT refilled now — it refills during the End-of-Round Phase.

**Restrictions:** You may only perform Buy Goods once per turn. You cannot buy more than you can pay for. You must be on a caravanserai city.

**Example:** Marco is on Tabriz. The Display shows: Silk, Spice, Jade, Jade, Incense, Silk. He pays 1 AP and 2 Coins to buy the Jade and the Incense. The Display shrinks to 4 cards.

---

### Action 3: Trade with a Rival (Cost: 1 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your meeple must be on the same city node as at least one other player's meeple.

**Cost:** 1 AP (only the active player pays; the trade partner pays nothing).

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare you wish to trade and name which player.
2. Negotiate openly. Both players may describe or reveal cards. The trade must involve at least 1 Goods Card for at least 1 Goods Card. Coins may be included on either side.
3. If both players agree to exact terms, execute the trade simultaneously.
4. If no agreement is reached, the AP is still spent but nothing is exchanged.

**Restrictions:** You may trade multiple times per turn (same or different partners), paying 1 AP each time. You cannot trade Royal Order Cards, Upgrade Cards, or the First Player Token.

**Example:** Fatima and Marco are both on Merv. Fatima pays 1 AP and offers Marco 2 Spice cards for 1 Jade card. Marco agrees. They swap simultaneously.

---

### Action 4: Deliver a Royal Order (Cost: 1 AP + Goods Cards)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your meeple must be on a city marked with a crown symbol — there are two Royal Courts: **The Sultan's Palace** and **The Khan's Court**. You must hold the exact combination of Goods Cards listed on the order.

**Cost:** 1 AP plus the Goods Cards specified on the Royal Order.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Declare which Available Royal Order you are fulfilling.
2. Reveal the required Goods Cards from your hand and discard them to the discard pile.
3. Take the Royal Order Card and place it face-up on your Player Board in the "Completed Orders" area.
4. Receive the Coin bonus: Bronze orders pay 1 Coin, Silver pay 2 Coins, Gold pay 3 Coins.
5. If fewer than 3 Available Orders remain, draw a replacement immediately.

**Restrictions:** You may fulfill multiple orders in a single turn if you have the AP and cards. You must be on a Royal Court city. You cannot partially fulfill orders.

**Example:** Wei is at The Khan's Court holding Silk, Silk, Spice, Jade. A Silver order requires 2 Silk + 1 Spice + 1 Jade for 6 Prestige Points. He pays 1 AP, discards the 4 cards, places the order on his board, and collects 2 Coins.

---

### Action 5: Purchase a Caravan Upgrade (Cost: 1 AP + Coins)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Your meeple must be on any caravanserai city.

**Cost:** 1 AP plus the Coin cost printed on the Upgrade Card (2–4 Coins).

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Choose one face-up Caravan Upgrade from the Upgrade Market.
2. Pay its Coin cost to the supply.
3. Place the card on your Player Board in one of your 3 Upgrade Slots. If all slots are full, you must discard one existing upgrade (remove it from the game) to make room, or cancel the action (AP is lost, Coins are refunded).
4. The upgrade takes effect immediately.

**Restrictions:** Maximum 3 upgrades per player. You may buy multiple per turn if you have AP and Coins. The Market refills at End-of-Round only.

**Example:** Fatima is on Samarkand. She pays 1 AP and 3 Coins for the "Camel Train" upgrade, increasing her hand limit from 6 to 8.

---

### Action 6: Scout (Cost: 1 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Can be performed on any city node.

**Cost:** 1 AP.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Draw 2 cards from the top of the Goods Deck.
2. Keep 1 card and place the other on the bottom of the Goods Deck.

**Restrictions:** You may Scout multiple times per turn. This is the fallback resource acquisition when far from caravanserais or low on Coins.

**Example:** Marco is on Balkh, which has no market. He pays 1 AP to Scout, draws Jade and Silk, keeps the Jade, and places the Silk on the bottom of the deck.

---

### Action 7: Rest (Cost: 1 AP)

**When it can be performed:** During Phase 2. Any city node.

**Cost:** 1 AP.

**Step-by-step resolution:**
1. Gain 2 Coins from the supply.

**Restrictions:** You may Rest only once per turn.

**Example:** Wei is broke after buying upgrades. He spends 1 AP to Rest and gains 2 Coins.

---

## C) PLAYER INTERACTION

### Trading

Direct goods exchange is handled through the Trade action (Action 3). Trading is voluntary — no player can be forced into a deal. The initiator pays 1 AP regardless of outcome, so failed negotiations have a real cost. Players may bluff about what they hold or refuse trades to waste an opponent's AP.

### Competitive Market Access

All players draw from the same Goods Display. When one player buys, the display shrinks until the End-of-Round refill. Early buyers in a round see the full selection; later players may find it picked clean. The rotating First Player Token ensures no one always buys first.

### Order Racing

Only one player can claim each Royal Order. With only 3 visible at a time, multiple players often pursue the same lucrative order. Seeing a rival stockpile Silk while a Silk-heavy Gold order is available forces a decision: race them, or pivot to a different order.

### Route Contention

While multiple caravans may occupy the same city, certain Hazard Cards ("Bandit Raid") inflict harsher penalties when other caravans are nearby — bandits target the largest group. Clustering with rivals can be risky.

### Denial Purchases

At only 1 Coin per card, buying goods you do not need just to deny them to opponents is a viable tactic. However, this fills precious cargo space in your hand, creating tension between denial and efficiency.

---

## D) RESOURCE FLOW

### Gaining Goods

Goods Cards enter players' hands through three channels: buying from the Goods Display (Action 2), Scouting from the deck (Action 6), or receiving cards through trades (Action 3). Certain Hazard Cards ("Lucky Find") also grant bonus draws.

### Gaining Coins

Coins are gained through: starting supply (3 per player), the Rest action (2 Coins), delivery bonuses from Royal Orders (1–3 Coins by tier), and occasional Hazard Card rewards ("Oasis Rest").

### Spending Goods

Goods Cards are discarded only to fulfill Royal Orders. They are never spent on other actions.

### Spending Coins

Coins are spent to buy Goods at the market (1 Coin each), to purchase Caravan Upgrades (2–4 Coins), and occasionally lost to Hazard penalties.

### Involuntary Loss

Hazard Cards cause involuntary losses. "Bandit Raid" forces discarding goods or coins. "Dust Storm" can halt movement and waste AP.

### Hand Limit and Cargo Management

The default hand limit is 6 Goods Cards (upgradeable to 8). During Phase 3, players over the limit must discard down. There is no limit on Coins held.

### Goods Deck Recycling

When the Goods Deck is exhausted, shuffle the discard pile to form a new deck. This ensures goods are always available, though deck composition shifts as orders remove cards from circulation.

### Fixed Pricing

Goods prices at caravanserais are always 1 Coin per card. Scarcity comes from the limited display size (6 cards, refreshed once per round), not from price fluctuations.

## E) SPECIAL RULES

### Hazard Card Types

The Hazard Deck contains 24 cards of the following types:

**Bandit Raid (6 cards).** Discard 1 random Goods Card from your hand. If you have no Goods Cards, lose 1 Coin. If you have neither, no effect. The "Armed Guard" upgrade negates this entirely.

**Dust Storm (5 cards).** Your movement ends immediately. If you planned further moves this turn, you cannot move again (remaining AP may be used for non-movement actions). The "Desert Guide" upgrade lets you ignore this.

**Broken Wheel (4 cards).** Lose 1 AP immediately (reduce remaining AP by 1; if at 0, no additional effect). The "Camel Train" upgrade negates this.

**Lucky Find (5 cards).** Draw 1 Goods Card from the deck and add it to your hand.

**Oasis Rest (3 cards).** Gain 2 Coins from the supply.

**Toll Checkpoint (1 card).** Pay 2 Coins. If you cannot pay 2, pay what you have and discard 1 Goods Card of your choice. If you have neither Coins nor Goods, no effect.

### Caravan Upgrade Card Types

There are 4 types, 3 copies each (12 total):

**Camel Train (Cost: 3 Coins).** Your hand limit increases by 2 (from 6 to 8). "Broken Wheel" Hazard Cards have no effect on you.

**Armed Guard (Cost: 4 Coins).** "Bandit Raid" Hazard Cards have no effect on you. When you draw a Bandit Raid, you may treat it as a Lucky Find instead (your guards seize the bandits' goods).

**Desert Guide (Cost: 3 Coins).** "Dust Storm" Hazard Cards have no effect on you. Lawless routes cost 0 AP for you (you still draw 2 Hazard Cards, but save the AP).

**Imperial Seal (Cost: 4 Coins).** When you deliver a Royal Order, gain 1 extra Coin beyond the normal tier bonus. Once per turn when buying Goods, you may take 1 card from the Display for free (0 Coins) as part of the Buy action.

## F) END GAME

### Game End Trigger

The game ends at the conclusion of the round in which **any one** of the following occurs:

1. **The Round Marker reaches space 8.** After Round 8, the game ends.
2. **Any single player has delivered 4 or more Royal Orders.** The current round is completed (all remaining players finish their turns), then the game ends.
3. **The Royal Order Deck is empty AND all Available Order slots are empty.** The current round is completed, then the game ends.

### Scoring

Each player totals their score:

- **Prestige Points from completed Royal Orders.** Sum the PP values on all order cards on your Player Board. This is the primary scoring source.
- **Remaining Goods Cards:** 1 point per 3 cards in hand (rounded down).
- **Remaining Coins:** 1 point per 4 Coins (rounded down).
- **Caravan Upgrades:** 1 point per Upgrade Card on your Player Board.
- **Diversity Bonus:** If you completed at least 1 order from each of the three tiers (Bronze, Silver, Gold), gain 5 bonus Prestige Points.

### Tiebreaker

If tied, apply these in order:

1. More completed Royal Orders wins.
2. If still tied, more Gold-tier orders wins.
3. If still tied, more total Goods Cards in hand wins.
4. If still tied, players share the victory.

## G) EXAMPLE ROUND

This example demonstrates Round 3 of a 3-player game with Fatima, Marco, and Wei. Fatima holds the First Player Token.

**Current board state:**
- Goods Display: Silk, Spice, Jade, Incense, Silk, Spice
- Available Royal Orders: Bronze "2 Silk" (2 PP), Silver "2 Silk + 1 Spice + 1 Jade" (6 PP), Bronze "1 Jade + 1 Incense" (2 PP)
- Upgrade Market: Armed Guard (4 Coins), Desert Guide (3 Coins), Camel Train (3 Coins)

---

### Fatima's Turn

**Location:** Samarkand. **Hand:** Silk, Silk, Spice, Jade. **Coins:** 4. **Upgrades:** None.

**Phase 1 — Refresh.** Fatima resets her AP to 3.

**Phase 2 — Actions.**

*Action 1: Move from Samarkand to Merv (Guarded route, 2 AP).* Fatima pays 2 AP and moves her meeple along the green Guarded route to Merv. No Hazard draw. She has 1 AP remaining.

*Action 2: Buy Goods at the Caravanserai (1 AP + Coins).* Merv has a Market Stall. Fatima spends her last 1 AP and pays 1 Coin to buy the Jade from the Goods Display. Her hand is now: Silk, Silk, Spice, Jade, Jade. Her Coins drop to 3. The Goods Display now has 5 cards.

**Phase 3 — Cargo Check.** Fatima has 5 cards (limit 6). No discard needed.

Fatima's strategy: she is one Spice card short of completing the Silver order "2 Silk + 1 Spice + 1 Jade" for 6 PP — but wait, she already has 1 Spice and could use the Jade she just bought. She actually has the full set (2 Silk, 1 Spice, 1 Jade). She needs to reach a Royal Court next turn to deliver.

---

### Marco's Turn

**Location:** Kashgar. **Hand:** Spice, Spice, Incense. **Coins:** 5. **Upgrades:** None.

**Phase 1 — Refresh.** Marco resets his AP to 3.

**Phase 2 — Actions.**

*Action 1: Move from Kashgar to Balkh (Lawless route, 1 AP).* Marco takes the dangerous shortcut. He pays 1 AP and moves to Balkh. Because the route is Lawless, he draws 2 Hazard Cards:
- First: **Bandit Raid.** Marco must discard 1 random Goods Card. He shuffles his hand face-down, draws his Incense, and discards it. Hand: Spice, Spice.
- Second: **Lucky Find.** Marco draws 1 card from the Goods Deck: Silk. Hand: Spice, Spice, Silk.

Net result: lost Incense, gained Silk. Acceptable.

*Action 2: Move from Balkh to Merv (Open route, 1 AP).* Marco pays 1 AP and moves to Merv. He draws 1 Hazard Card: **Oasis Rest.** Marco gains 2 Coins. His Coins increase to 7. He has 1 AP remaining.

*Action 3: Purchase a Caravan Upgrade (1 AP + Coins).* Marco is on Merv, a caravanserai city. He buys the Armed Guard for 4 Coins. He places it on his Player Board. His Coins drop to 3. From now on, Bandit Raid cards have no effect on Marco, and he can treat them as Lucky Finds.

**Phase 3 — Cargo Check.** Marco has 3 cards (limit 6). No discard needed.

---

### Wei's Turn

**Location:** Tabriz. **Hand:** Silk, Incense, Jade, Spice, Spice. **Coins:** 2. **Upgrades:** Camel Train.

**Phase 1 — Refresh.** Wei resets his AP to 3.

**Phase 2 — Actions.**

*Action 1: Rest (1 AP).* Wei needs funds. He spends 1 AP and gains 2 Coins. Coins increase to 4. He has 2 AP remaining.

*Action 2: Move from Tabriz to The Khan's Court (Guarded route, 2 AP).* Wei pays 2 AP and moves to The Khan's Court. No Hazard draw.

Wei checks the Available Orders. The Bronze "1 Jade + 1 Incense" (2 PP) is available, and he has both cards. But he has 0 AP remaining, so he cannot deliver this turn. He must wait until next round. This is risky — Fatima is also heading toward a court.

**Phase 3 — Cargo Check.** Wei has 5 cards (limit 8 due to Camel Train). No discard needed.

---

### End-of-Round Phase

1. **Refill Goods Display.** The display has 5 cards. Draw 1 from the Goods Deck to refill to 6. New display: Silk, Spice, Incense, Silk, Spice, Jade.
2. **Check Available Orders.** All 3 slots occupied. No refill.
3. **Refill Upgrade Market.** The Armed Guard was purchased, leaving 2 cards. Draw 1 from the Upgrade Deck. Market now has 3 cards.
4. **Advance Round Marker.** Move from space 3 to space 4.
5. **Pass First Player Token.** Fatima passes it to Marco. Marco goes first in Round 4.

**Tensions heading into Round 4:** Fatima has the exact cards for the valuable Silver order (6 PP) and needs to reach a Royal Court — she is 2 Guarded-route moves from The Khan's Court via Tabriz. Wei is already at The Khan's Court but cannot deliver until next turn (needs 1 AP for the Deliver action). Marco is building his hand and now has hazard protection. The race for orders intensifies.
