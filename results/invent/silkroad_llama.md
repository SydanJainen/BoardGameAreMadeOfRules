# Silk Road Quest

## Theme
In "Silk Road Quest," players take on the role of ancient silk road merchants racing to fulfill royal orders. The game is set in a world where exotic spices, precious stones, and rare textiles are highly sought after by monarchs and nobles across the known world. Players must navigate treacherous trade routes, overcome rival merchants, and manage their resources carefully to emerge victorious. As players collect and trade valuable commodities, they also upgrade their caravans and establish a reputation as master traders.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: A) **Resource Management:** Players manage three types of resources: Wood, Stone, and Crystal, which are used to build and upgrade caravans, pay tribute to the royal court, and perform various actions. Resources can be gained through trade, exploration, or conquest, but must be carefully managed to avoid exhaustion.

B) **Caravan Building:** Players construct and upgrade their caravans by placing tiles on the game board, which represent different routes and trading posts. Caravans provide a safe haven for players' resources and offer bonuses for completing certain tasks.

C) **Action Point Allowance System (APAS):** Each player starts with a set of action points, which they can use to perform various actions, such as moving, trading, or building. Players must carefully manage their APAs to maximize their efficiency and minimize waste.

D) **Trade and Negotiation:** Players engage in trade and negotiation with each other, exchanging resources for rare commodities or completing tasks for exclusive rewards. However, beware of rival merchants trying to sabotage your deals! Players can also use market boards to increase their chances of acquiring rare resources.
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: Estimated play time: 30-45 minutes

## Components
- * 48 Resource Cards (12 per type: Wood
- Stone
- Crystal)
* 1 Game Board (hex grid
- 37 tiles)
* 16 Caravan Tiles (4 types: Merchant's Cart
- Camel
- Horse
- Donkey)
* 24 Action Point Tokens
* 12 Royal Order cards
* 8 Event cards
* 6 Patrol tokens
* 3 Watchtower tokens
* 2 Market boards
* 1 Player token set (4-6 players)

## Rules
**Setup:**

1. Assemble the game board by arranging the tiles in a hexagonal grid.
2. Shuffle the Resource Cards and deal 5 to each player, placing them in their resource pool.
3. Each player selects a token from the Player token set and places it on the starting tile.
4. Determine the first player randomly; this player goes first.

**Rules:**

A) **Turn Structure:**
1. Phase 1: Action Phase (APAS)
2. Phase 2: Resource Management
3. Phase 3: Trade and Negotiation
4. Phase 4: Movement
5. Phase 5: Event Phase

B) **Action Catalog:**

**Build Caravan Tile:** When can it be performed? In the Action Phase (Phase 1). Cost: 1 AP, 2 Wood resources, 1 Stone resource. Step-by-step resolution: Place a caravan tile on an empty space adjacent to your current tile. Draw 1 Market board token if you have a Watchtower token in your possession.

**Move Caravan:** When can it be performed? In the Movement Phase (Phase 4). Cost: 1 AP, 2 Stone resources. Step-by-step resolution: Move your caravan token up to 3 spaces along the hexagonal grid. You must land on an unoccupied space adjacent to your current tile.

**Trade Resource:** When can it be performed? In the Trade and Negotiation Phase (Phase 3). Cost: 1 AP, 2 Crystal resources. Step-by-step resolution: Draw a resource card from your pool that matches one of your existing resource cards. Discard the excess resource.

**Visit Royal Court:** When can it be performed? During your Action Phase (Phase 1) or as an optional action in your Trade and Negotiation Phase. Cost: 2 AP, 3 Wood resources, 1 Stone resource. Step-by-step resolution: Pay tribute to the royal court by discarding your specified resources.

**Block Enemy Caravan:** When can it be performed? In the Action Phase (Phase 1). Cost: 1 AP, 2 Crystal resources. Step-by-step resolution: If an enemy caravan token is adjacent to you, place a Patrol token on that space to block their movement for one turn.

C) **Player Interaction:**
Players interact through trade and negotiation with each other. However, rival merchants can sabotage deals by placing Watchtower tokens on the hexagonal grid, blocking your movement or stealing resources from you. Players must also be mindful of their APAs, as excessive use can lead to exhaustion.

D) **Resource Flow:**
Resources are gained through trade, exploration, or conquest. Resources are lost when they exceed a player's resource pool limit (5 cards per type). Resources refresh after the Action Phase (Phase 1).

E) **Special Rules:**

* **Market Boards:** Players can use market boards to increase their chances of acquiring rare resources.
* **Event Cards:** These cards represent natural disasters, bandits, or royal decrees that affect all players.
* **Watchtower Tokens:** These tokens provide bonuses for completing tasks and protecting your caravan.

F) **End Game:**
The game ends when one player has fulfilled three Royal Orders. Players score points based on the completed orders:
* 2 points for each completed order
* -1 point for every Watchtower token placed on the board (max -3)

Tiebreaker rule: If two or more players are tied, the player with the most resources of any type wins.

G) **Example Round:**

Turn 1:

Alice goes first. She spends 2 Wood and 1 Stone to build a Caravan Tile on hex A1. Then, she moves her caravan token up 2 spaces along the grid, reaching hex B3.

Bob's turn:
Bob decides to trade 2 Crystal resources for 1 Wood resource from Alice. He discards his excess resources.

Charlie's turn:
Charlie spends 1 AP and 2 Stone resources to build a Watchtower on hex C4. She places a Watchtower token on the board, blocking Bob's movement.

The game continues with each player taking their turns in sequence, using the actions described above to manage their caravans, trade resources, and navigate the challenges of the Silk Road.
