# Wildfire Horizon

## Theme
In "Wildfire Horizon," players take on the roles of firefighters battling a devastating wildfire that threatens to consume a national park. The game is set in a fictionalized version of Yellowstone National Park, where two rival fire departments, the Red Oak Fire Department and the Blackwood Fire Department, must put aside their differences to work together against a common enemy. As players coordinate water drops, evacuations, and firebreaks, they must navigate the challenges of working under pressure and making tough decisions that impact the success of their mission.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: A) **Resource Management:** Players must manage three types of resources: Water (used for water drops and firefighting), Stone (used for constructing firebreaks and barriers), and Crystal (used to activate special abilities and equipment). Resources can be gained through various means, such as collecting Resource Cards or completing actions that generate resources. Players must carefully manage their resource hand size and storage capacity to ensure they have the necessary resources to complete their objectives.

B) **Action Points:** Each player starts with a set of Action Points (AP), which are used to perform various actions during their turn. C) **Wind Cards:** The game features a deck of Wind Cards, which represent the unpredictable nature of wildfires. When a player draws a Wind Card, they must resolve its effects immediately, which can either help or hinder their progress. Wind Cards can also affect other players, creating opportunities for cooperation and competition.

T) **Market or Economy:** There is no market or economy in the game; players must manage their resources carefully to succeed.

W) **Environmental Effects:** The game features environmental effects, such as changing weather conditions, which can affect gameplay.

X) **Event Resolution:** Event Token cards have unique effects that players must resolve immediately.

Y) **Situational Rules:** When a player uses an Equipment Token, they may choose to activate its special ability or discard it. This decision affects the game state and future resource generation.

Z) **END GAME:**

A) The game ends when the fire reaches 3 towns (hex tiles with player tokens). If this condition is met, all players lose.

B) In the event of a tie, the player with the most Water resources wins. If there is still a tie, the player with the most AP wins.
