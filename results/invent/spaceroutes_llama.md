# Galactic Conquest

## Theme
In Galactic Conquest, players represent rival factions vying for control of the galaxy. As they connect space stations across a vast expanse, they must manage fuel, navigate asteroid fields, and uncover secret mission cards that grant them an advantage over their opponents. The game's theme is driven by the tension between exploration and exploitation, as players balance their desire to expand their territory with the need to maintain the delicate balance of resources in the galaxy.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: A) **Route Building:** Players deploy shuttle fleets to connect space stations, creating a network of routes across the galaxy. Route building is done through the use of Resource Cards, which are used to construct roads, habitats, and other infrastructure.

- Resources are gained by completing missions, trading with other players, or discovering new resources on asteroid fields.
- Routes can be extended by spending resources, but there is a limit to how many routes a player can build before they must return to the starting space station to replenish their resources.
- Route building creates opportunities for players to block and control key intersections in the galaxy, limiting their opponents' movement.

B) **Fuel Management:** Players must manage their fuel carefully, as it depletes over time. Fuel is used to move shuttle fleets between space stations, construct new infrastructure, and resolve certain actions.

- Fuel can be replenished by returning to the starting space station or by completing specific missions.
- When a player runs out of fuel, they are unable to take any actions until their fuel is restored.
- Players must carefully balance their fuel usage with their need to complete missions and expand their territory.

C) **Asteroid Fields:** Asteroid fields pose a risk to players who venture too close, causing damage to their shuttle fleets or disrupting their operations.

- When a player lands on an asteroid field, they must draw a Mission Card, which may contain rewards or penalties.
- Players can mitigate the risks of asteroid fields by using specific resources or technologies, but there is no guarantee of success.
- Asteroid fields create opportunities for players to discover new resources and gain an advantage over their opponents.
- **Players**: 2-5 players.
- **Duration**: 45-75 minutes.

## Components
- * 48 Resource Cards (12 per type: Wood
- Stone
- Iron
- Crystal)
* 1 Game Board (hex grid
- 37 tiles)
* 5 Space Station Tiles (3 starting stations
- 2 expansion tiles)
* 10 Mission Cards (5 rewards
- 5 penalties)
* 20 Shuttle Fleet Tokens (10 each for players and asteroid fields)
* 10 Fuel Tokens
* 5 Asteroid Field Tiles (1 each for 4 asteroid fields)
* 1 Rulebook
* 5 Player Boards

## Rules
**SETUP:**

1. Assemble the game board by arranging the hex tiles in a random configuration.
2. Shuffle the Mission Cards and deal 2 to each player. These cards are kept secret from other players.
3. Each player chooses a starting space station tile and places it on the board, ensuring that all edges of the board are adjacent to at least one edge.
4. Determine the starting player randomly, or by agreement among players.
5. Each player receives 2 Fuel Tokens and 1 Resource Card (Wood).
6. Players place their shuttle fleet tokens across the starting space station, connecting it to adjacent hex tiles.

**RULES:**

A) **TURN STRUCTURE:**
Each turn consists of three phases:

1. **Resource Management:** Players collect resources from the board or use resources to complete actions.
2. **Action Phase:** Players take one action (listed in the Action Catalog), which may include building a route, resolving a mission card, moving their shuttle fleet, or using an asteroid field.
3. **Event Phase:** The top Mission Card is drawn and resolved according to its effects.

B) **ACTION CATALOG:**

* **Build Route:** Spend 2 resources (Wood, Stone, Iron) to build a road between two adjacent hex tiles. Cost: 2 resources. Step-by-step resolution: Place a Road Token on the board, connecting the two hex tiles.
* **Move Shuttle Fleet:** Move your shuttle fleet from one space station to another using Fuel Tokens. Cost: 1 Fuel Token. Step-by-step resolution: Draw a new Resource Card and discard an old one.
* **Use Asteroid Field:** Resolve the effects of an asteroid field by spending 1 resource (Wood, Stone, Iron) and drawing a Mission Card. Cost: 1 resource. Step-by-step resolution: Draw a new Mission Card or resolve its effects immediately.
* **Complete Mission:** Spend resources to complete a mission card. Cost: varies depending on the mission card. Step-by-step resolution: Resolve the mission card's effects immediately.

Restrictions and limits:

* Players can only hold 10 Resource Cards in their hand at any time.
* Players must discard an old Resource Card when drawing a new one.

Example: Alice spends 2 Wood and 1 Stone to build a Watchtower on hex B3. She places a Watchtower token and draws 1 Patrol card.

C) **PLAYER INTERACTION:**
Players can interact with each other in the following ways:

* Trading: Players can trade Resource Cards with each other, but there is no formal trading mechanism.
* Blocking and Area Control: Players can block and control key intersections in the galaxy by building routes or using asteroid fields. Other players can disrupt these efforts by landing on the same hex tile or completing a mission card that negates their effects.
* Alliances or Negotiations: Players can form temporary alliances or negotiate with each other to achieve common goals, but there is no formal mechanism for doing so.

D) **RESOURCE FLOW:**
Resources are gained in the following ways:

* Completing missions
* Trading with other players
* Discovering new resources on asteroid fields

Resources are spent by spending resources to complete actions or build routes. Resources can be replenished by returning to the starting space station or by completing specific missions.

E) **SPECIAL RULES:**

* Card effects: Mission Cards have rewards and penalties that affect gameplay.
* Environmental effects: Asteroid fields pose a risk to players who venture too close, causing damage to their shuttle fleets or disrupting their operations.
* Event resolution: The top Mission Card is drawn and resolved according to its effects.

F) **END GAME:**
The game ends when all players have completed a set number of missions (5 for 2-player games, 7 for 3-4 player games). The player with the most points wins. Points are awarded as follows:

* Routes: 1 point per route built
* Mission Cards: rewards and penalties listed on the mission card
* Asteroid Fields: -1 point per asteroid field landed on

In case of a tie, the player with the most routes built wins. If there is still a tie, the player with the highest score from their mission cards wins.

G) **EXAMPLE ROUND:**

Alice (Player 1): Starting space station B1.

* Turn 1:
 + Resource Management: Alice collects 2 Wood and 1 Stone.
 + Action Phase: Alice spends 2 Wood to build a road between hexes A1 and A2.
 + Event Phase: The top Mission Card is drawn and resolved. Alice draws a new Patrol card.
* Turn 2 (Bob's turn):
 + Resource Management: Bob collects 2 Iron and 1 Crystal.
 + Action Phase: Bob spends 1 Fuel Token to move his shuttle fleet from hex B3 to hex C4.
 + Event Phase: The top Mission Card is drawn and resolved. Bob draws a new Asteroid Field card.
