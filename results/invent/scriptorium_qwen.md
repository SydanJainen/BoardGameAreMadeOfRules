# **Scriptoria Scriptorium**

## Theme
In *Scriptoria Scriptorium*, players take on the role of medieval scribes and illuminators, vying to produce the most magnificent illuminated manuscripts for patrons. The conflict lies in the scarcity of resources and the ever-present threat of rival scribes attempting to sabotage your work. The compelling setting is a rich tapestry of medieval Europe, where each manuscript you complete not only earns you prestige but also improves your ability to produce future works.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Resource Management**: Players must manage various resources such as rare pigments and skilled monks, allocating them wisely for the best outcomes.
2. **Card Drafting and Action Selection**: Each turn, players select from a pool of cards representing different actions, each offering unique benefits but requiring specific costs.
3. **Building and Upgrading Structures**: Building structures like workshops and storage facilities enhances your production capabilities and provides strategic advantages.
4. **Trading and Negotiation**: Players can trade resources with one another or negotiate exclusive deals to gain an edge.
5. **Patron Management**: Satisfying patrons' requests for specific manuscripts earns points, but completing them requires the right combination of resources and specialized monks.
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: 40-60 minutes

3. **Building and Upgrading Structures**: Building structures like workshops and storage facilities enhances your production capabilities and provides strategic advantages.

### Components
1. **48 Resource Cards (12 per type: Wood, Stone, Iron, Crystal)**
2. **30 Monk Recruitment Cards (6 types with unique abilities)**
3. **24 Manuscript Cards (various designs for different patrons)**
4. **12 Watchtower Tokens**
5. **1 Game Board (hex grid, 37 tiles)**
6. **1 Set of 4 Player Mats (one per player)**
7. **1 Rulebook**

### Players
2-4 players

### Duration
40-60 minutes

### Setup
1. **Board Assembly**: Place the game board on a flat surface and arrange all hex tiles to form a single large hexagon.
2. **Deck Preparation**: Shuffle the Resource Cards, Monk Recruitment Cards, and Manuscript Cards separately and place them aside.
3. **Initial Resource Distribution**: Each player starts with 5 Wood, 4 Stone, 3 Iron, and 2 Crystal cards.
4. **Starting Positions**: Place each player's mat on one of the hex tiles adjacent to the center tile.
5. **First Player Selection**: The youngest player goes first; rotate clockwise for subsequent turns.

### Rules

#### A) Turn Structure
1. **Recruitment Phase** (Mandatory): Each player may recruit up to 2 monks from their available options, using the Monk Recruitment Cards.
2. **Action Phase** (Optional): Players take turns performing actions based on cards drawn at the start of each turn. Actions can include building structures, trading resources, or completing manuscripts.
3. **Production Phase** (Mandatory): Use the collected resources to produce a manuscript according to its requirements.
4. **End Phase** (Mandatory): Discard excess cards and draw new ones to maintain hand size.

#### B) Action Catalog
1. **Recruit Monk**
   - When: Recruitment Phase
   - Cost: 2 Wood, 1 Stone
   - Resolution: Place the recruited monk on your player mat and discard the corresponding card.
   - Example: Alice spends 2 Wood and 1 Stone to recruit a scribe with "Pigment Expert" ability.

2. **Build Structure**
   - When: Action Phase (requires adjacent hex)
   - Cost: Varies by structure
   - Resolution: Place a Watchtower token on an unoccupied hex tile, gaining the corresponding resource bonus.
   - Example: Bob builds a Library for 3 Iron and gains +1 Manuscript card per turn.

3. **Trade Resources**
   - When: Action Phase (requires adjacent player)
   - Cost: None
   - Resolution: Propose a trade; if accepted, exchange resources with the other player.
   - Example: Carol trades 2 Crystal for 1 Wood with Dave.

4. **Complete Manuscript**
   - When: Production Phase
   - Cost: Varies by manuscript (resources and monks)
   - Resolution: Pay the required resources and monk abilities to complete a manuscript, gaining points and increasing future production.
   - Example: Eve completes a "Liturgy" manuscript for 5 Wood, 3 Stone, and 1 Monk with "Pigment Expert."

#### C) Player Interaction
- **Trading**: Players can propose trades with adjacent opponents. Trades must be mutually agreed upon.
- **Blocking**: Structures like Watchtowers provide defensive bonuses but restrict movement through hexes.
- **Negotiation**: Alliances or negotiations are encouraged to secure better deals and resources.

#### D) Resource Flow
- **Gained**: Through successful actions, trading, or completing manuscripts.
- **Spent**: On actions, completing manuscripts, and upgrading structures.
- **Lost**: Discarded cards at the end of each turn. Hand size is limited to 8 cards per player.
- **Refresh**: At the start of each turn.

#### E) Special Rules
1. **Card Effects**:
   - **Resource Cards**: Provide specific resources.
   - **Monk Recruitment Cards**: Offer unique abilities and resource bonuses.
   - **Manuscript Cards**: Require a combination of resources, monks, and can be upgraded for more points.
2. **Environmental Effects**:
   - Structures like Libraries and Armories provide ongoing benefits that affect all players.
3. **Event Resolution**:
   - Random events (e.g., "Pestilence" card) may disrupt production or grant bonuses.

#### F) End Game
- The game ends when a player completes 5 manuscripts or after the last turn if no one has completed 5 manuscripts.
- Victory is determined by the total number of points from completed manuscripts. Each manuscript type provides different point values:
   - Common Manuscript: 10 points
   - Rare Manuscript: 20 points
   - Epic Manuscript: 30 points

#### G) Example Round
**Example with Alice, Bob, and Carol**

- **Alice's Turn**
  1. Recruitment Phase: Recruit a "Pigment Expert" for 2 Wood, 1 Stone.
  2. Action Phase: Build a Library for 3 Iron (gains +1 Manuscript card per turn).
  3. Production Phase: Complete a Common Manuscript (5 Wood, 3 Stone, and 1 Monk with "Pigment Expert").
  4. End Phase: Discard excess cards.

- **Bob's Turn**
  1. Recruitment Phase: Trade 2 Crystal for 1 Wood with Carol.
  2. Action Phase: Build a Watchtower on hex B3 to gain +1 Resource.
  3. Production Phase: Complete a Rare Manuscript (8 Wood, 5 Stone, and 2 Monks).
  4. End Phase: Discard excess cards.

- **Carol's Turn**
  1. Recruitment Phase: Recruit a "Binding Specialist" for 2 Iron, 1 Crystal.
  2. Action Phase: Trade 3 Wood with Alice for 2 Stone.
  3. Production Phase: Complete an Epic Manuscript (10 Wood, 8 Stone, and 4 Monks).
  4. End Phase: Discard excess cards.

This example demonstrates the flow of turns, interactions, and key actions in *Scriptoria Scriptorium*.
