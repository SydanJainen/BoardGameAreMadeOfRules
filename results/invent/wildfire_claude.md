# Wildfire

## Theme

In Wildfire, players are elite wildfire response operatives deployed to Pinecrest National Park, where a catastrophic fire has broken out across the forest canopy. The park is home to ancient sequoia groves, alpine meadows, wildlife corridors, and remote ranger stations --- all under immediate threat. Players must coordinate firefighting crews, deploy water bombers, construct firebreaks, and staff watchtowers to halt the advancing blaze before the entire park is consumed. But fire is a living, shifting adversary: wind cards drive the flames in unpredictable directions, embers leap across cleared terrain, and drought conditions can turn a controlled situation into a firestorm in a single round. Victory demands both strategic planning and moment-to-moment adaptation. This is a cooperative game where all players win or lose together.

## Mechanics

- **Cooperative Action Point Allocation**: Each player has a pool of Action Points (AP) per turn to spend on movement, firefighting, construction, and scouting. Players must coordinate their AP expenditure to cover the most critical threats.
- **Fire Spread with Wind Cards**: At the end of each round, a Wind Card is drawn that determines the direction and intensity of fire spread. Wind can shift suddenly, cause ember showers that ignite distant tiles, or die down to give players breathing room.
- **Tile-Based Map with Modular Setup**: The park is represented by a hex grid of terrain tiles (forest, meadow, river, ridge, town) assembled randomly each game for high replayability.
- **Firebreak Construction**: Players spend AP and resources to place firebreak tiles that block fire spread along specific edges. Placement is permanent and strategic.
- **Watchtower Network**: Watchtower tokens grant line-of-sight bonuses, allowing players to preview upcoming Wind Cards or spot new fire ignitions before they grow out of control.
- **Resource Management**: Water and supply tokens are finite. Players must manage their reserves and resupply at ranger stations.
- **Role Asymmetry**: Each player takes on a specialist role (Hotshot, Spotter, Engineer, Medic) with a unique ability that encourages division of labor.

## Components

- 1 Rulebook
- 37 Hex Terrain Tiles (10 Forest, 6 Meadow, 5 River, 5 Ridge, 4 Town, 4 Ranger Station, 3 Sequoia Grove)
- 4 Player Role Boards (Hotshot, Spotter, Engineer, Medic)
- 4 Firefighter Meeples (1 per player color: red, orange, yellow, white)
- 20 Fire Tokens (wooden flame-shaped, red/orange)
- 12 Ember Tokens (small orange discs)
- 18 Firebreak Tiles (rectangular barrier pieces)
- 8 Watchtower Tokens (tall wooden cylinders)
- 30 Wind Cards
- 15 Event Cards
- 24 Water Tokens (blue droplet-shaped)
- 16 Supply Tokens (green cubes)
- 4 Action Point Trackers (numbered dials, 1 per player)
- 1 Fire Intensity Track (cardboard strip with slider)
- 1 Round Marker and Round Track (10 rounds)
- 1 Wind Direction Compass (6-sided cardboard spinner or die)
- 12 Wildlife Tokens (animals to rescue for bonus objectives)
- 4 Reference Cards (1 per player, summarizing actions and costs)

## Players

2--4 players

## Duration

45--75 minutes (approximately 60 minutes for a standard game)

## Setup

1. **Build the Park**: Shuffle all 37 hex terrain tiles face-down. Draw and arrange them into a large hexagonal map (concentric rings: 1 center tile, 6 inner ring, 12 middle ring, 18 outer ring). Flip all tiles face-up. Ensure at least 2 Ranger Station tiles are in the inner or middle ring; swap if necessary.

2. **Place Initial Fires**: Draw 3 Wind Cards (discard after use). For each card, place 1 Fire Token on the terrain tile indicated by the card's ignition coordinates. If a card names a River tile, skip it and draw again. Return these 3 Wind Cards to the box.

3. **Shuffle Decks**: Shuffle the remaining Wind Cards into a face-down Wind Deck. Shuffle the Event Cards into a separate face-down Event Deck.

4. **Choose Roles**: Each player selects a Role Board (or deal randomly). Take the matching Firefighter Meeple and Action Point Tracker. Set the AP Tracker to 4 (starting AP).

5. **Distribute Starting Resources**: Each player receives 4 Water Tokens and 3 Supply Tokens placed on their Role Board.

6. **Deploy Firefighters**: Each player places their meeple on any Ranger Station tile. Multiple players may start on the same station.

7. **Place the Round Marker**: Set it to Round 1 on the Round Track. Set the Fire Intensity Track to Level 1.

8. **Prepare the Reserve**: Place remaining Water Tokens, Supply Tokens, Fire Tokens, Ember Tokens, Firebreak Tiles, Watchtower Tokens, and Wildlife Tokens in a general supply within reach of all players.

9. **Place Wildlife**: Randomly place 1 Wildlife Token on each Sequoia Grove tile and on 3 randomly selected Forest tiles.

## Rules

### A) Turn Structure

Each round consists of three phases played in order:

**Phase 1 -- Player Turns** (taken in clockwise order, one player at a time):
Each player has 4 Action Points (AP) to spend on any combination of actions from the Action Catalog (see Section B). Unspent AP do not carry over. Players may freely discuss strategy at any time (open cooperation).

**Phase 2 -- Fire Spreads**:
After all players have taken their turns, resolve fire spread:
1. Draw the top Wind Card from the Wind Deck and read it aloud.
2. The Wind Card specifies a **direction** (one of six hex directions) and an **intensity** (1, 2, or 3).
3. For each tile currently containing a Fire Token, spread fire to adjacent tiles in the indicated direction, up to the number of tiles equal to the intensity. Place a new Fire Token on each newly ignited tile.
4. **Blocked spread**: Fire does not spread across a Firebreak Tile edge, onto a River tile, or onto a tile already containing a Fire Token.
5. **Ember jump**: If the Wind Card has an ember icon, also place 1 Ember Token on a random tile within 2 hexes of any existing fire (roll the compass die for direction, count 2 tiles out). At the start of the next round, each Ember Token becomes a full Fire Token unless a player is on that tile or an adjacent tile with a Watchtower.

**Phase 3 -- Upkeep**:
1. Advance the Round Marker by 1.
2. If any tile now has 2 or more Fire Tokens on it, it becomes a **Firestorm** tile: remove the extra tokens but advance the Fire Intensity Track by 1. When the track reaches Level 4, the fire is out of control (see Section G for end-game conditions).
3. Draw 1 Event Card and resolve it immediately (see Section F).
4. Each player at a Ranger Station tile may resupply: take 2 Water Tokens and 1 Supply Token from the general supply.
5. Reset all players' AP Trackers to 4.

---

### B) Action Catalog

All actions are performed during Phase 1 of a player's turn. A player may perform any combination in any order, as long as they have sufficient AP and resources.

| Action | AP Cost | Resource Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Move** | 1 AP | None | Move your meeple to an adjacent hex tile. Moving onto a Ridge tile costs 2 AP instead of 1. |
| **Fight Fire** | 2 AP | 1 Water Token | Remove 1 Fire Token from your current tile or an adjacent tile. |
| **Build Firebreak** | 2 AP | 1 Supply Token | Place a Firebreak Tile on one edge of your current hex. Fire cannot spread across this edge. Maximum 2 firebreaks per hex tile. |
| **Erect Watchtower** | 2 AP | 2 Supply Tokens | Place a Watchtower Token on your current tile (only on Forest, Meadow, or Ridge tiles; max 1 per tile). Grants scouting ability (see Section D). |
| **Scout** | 1 AP | None | If on or adjacent to a Watchtower, look at the top 2 Wind Cards; return them in any order. |
| **Rescue Wildlife** | 1 AP | None | If on a tile with a Wildlife Token and no Fire Token, take the Wildlife Token and place it on your Role Board. |
| **Resupply** | 1 AP | None | Only available on a Ranger Station tile. Take 1 Water Token or 1 Supply Token from the general supply. |
| **Pass Equipment** | 0 AP | None | Give any number of Water or Supply Tokens to another player on the same tile. |

---

### C) Movement and Positioning

1. **Basic movement** costs 1 AP per hex. Players may move multiple hexes in a single turn by spending 1 AP per hex.
2. **Ridge tiles** are difficult terrain: entering a Ridge tile costs 2 AP.
3. **River tiles** are impassable on foot. Players cannot enter River tiles. Rivers act as natural firebreaks.
4. **Fire tiles**: A player may enter a tile with a Fire Token, but if they end their turn on a burning tile, they lose 1 Water Token (heat exposure). If they have no Water, they must retreat to an adjacent non-burning tile (free move) and lose their next turn's first AP.
5. **Multiple meeples**: Any number of players may occupy the same tile simultaneously, enabling the Pass Equipment action.

---

### D) Watchtowers and Scouting

1. A Watchtower Token on a tile provides **line of sight** over all tiles within 2 hexes of that watchtower.
2. Any player on or adjacent to a watchtower tile may use the **Scout** action (1 AP) to look at the top 2 cards of the Wind Deck and rearrange them in any order.
3. Watchtowers **suppress embers**: During Phase 2, any Ember Token that lands on a tile within line-of-sight of a watchtower is immediately removed (the crew spots and stamps it out).
4. If a Watchtower tile catches fire (receives a Fire Token), the Watchtower Token is destroyed and returned to the supply.
5. Maximum of 8 Watchtowers in play at any time (limited by component count).

---

### E) Firebreaks

1. A Firebreak Tile is placed on a **single edge** of a hex tile. It blocks fire spread across that specific edge.
2. Each hex tile may have a maximum of 2 Firebreak Tiles on its edges.
3. Firebreaks are permanent once placed and cannot be moved or removed.
4. Firebreaks do not block player movement; players may freely cross firebreak edges.
5. **Strategic placement**: Since each firebreak only covers one edge, players must anticipate wind direction to position them effectively. A firebreak on the wrong edge provides no protection.
6. If a tile with firebreaks becomes a Firestorm tile (see Phase 3 upkeep), all firebreaks on that tile are destroyed.

---

### F) Event Cards and Wind Cards

**Wind Cards (drawn every round in Phase 2):**
Each Wind Card shows:
- A compass direction (one of 6 hex directions)
- An intensity value (1, 2, or 3)
- Optionally, an ember icon (triggers ember jump) or a special effect

Special Wind Card effects (resolve instead of or in addition to normal spread):
- **Calm Winds**: No fire spreads this round. Players may each recover 1 Water Token.
- **Firestorm Gust**: Intensity 3 spread, plus all existing Ember Tokens immediately ignite.
- **Shifting Winds**: Draw a second Wind Card; fire spreads in both directions this round.
- **Backdraft**: Fire spreads in the opposite direction of the arrow at intensity 2.

**Event Cards (drawn every round in Phase 3):**
- **Water Bomber Flyover**: Remove 1 Fire Token from any tile on the map.
- **Supply Drop**: Place 2 Water Tokens and 1 Supply Token on any Ranger Station.
- **Dry Lightning**: Place 1 new Fire Token on a random Meadow tile.
- **Volunteer Crew**: Each player gains +1 AP on their next turn.
- **Equipment Breakdown**: Choose 1 player; that player gets only 3 AP next turn.
- **Wildlife Spotted**: Place 1 Wildlife Token on a random Forest tile without fire.
- **Drought**: No resupply is available at Ranger Stations this round.
- **Favorable Forecast**: Look at the top 3 Wind Cards; discard 1 and return the other 2 in any order.
- **Reinforced Line**: Place 1 free Firebreak Tile on any edge adjacent to a player's current tile.

---

### G) Victory and Defeat

**Defeat -- All players lose if any of the following occur:**
1. The Fire Intensity Track reaches Level 4 (too many firestorms; the blaze is uncontrollable).
2. Fire Tokens are present on 3 or more Town tiles simultaneously (civilian areas overrun).
3. All 3 Sequoia Grove tiles have Fire Tokens (irreplaceable ancient forest lost).
4. The Wind Deck runs out (10 rounds pass) and any of the above partial-loss conditions are one step away from triggering (at the game master's discretion in competitive variants, or simply: the Wind Deck running out while any Town tile is burning counts as a loss).

**Victory -- All players win if:**
At the end of Round 10 (when the Wind Deck is exhausted), none of the defeat conditions are met. The fire has been contained.

**Scoring (for competitive ranking across sessions):**
After a victory, calculate a team score:
- +5 points for each Town tile with no Fire Token
- +3 points for each Sequoia Grove tile with no Fire Token
- +2 points for each Wildlife Token rescued
- +1 point for each Water Token remaining in players' supply
- -1 point for each Fire Token still on the map

Record the score to compare across games and difficulty levels.

---

### Role Abilities

Each specialist role has a unique passive or activated ability:

- **Hotshot (red meeple)**: Fight Fire costs only 1 AP instead of 2. The Hotshot is the frontline firefighter.
- **Spotter (orange meeple)**: Scout is free (0 AP) when on a Watchtower tile. May scout from up to 2 tiles away from a Watchtower. The Spotter reads the wind.
- **Engineer (yellow meeple)**: Build Firebreak costs only 1 AP instead of 2. May place a third Firebreak on a single hex tile. The Engineer shapes the terrain.
- **Medic (white meeple)**: When on the same tile as another player, may spend 1 AP to restore 2 Water Tokens to that player from the supply. Immune to heat exposure penalty when ending a turn on a burning tile.

---

### Difficulty Variants

- **Recruit (Easy)**: Start with 5 Water and 4 Supply each. Begin with only 2 initial fires. Fire Intensity loss threshold is Level 5 instead of 4.
- **Veteran (Standard)**: Use the default setup as written above.
- **Inferno (Hard)**: Start with 3 Water and 2 Supply each. Begin with 4 initial fires. Shuffle 2 extra "Firestorm Gust" cards into the Wind Deck.

---

### Example Round

**Round 3, Veteran difficulty, 3 players (Hotshot, Spotter, Engineer):**

*Phase 1 -- Player Turns:*

The Hotshot is on tile F4 (Forest, burning). She spends 2 AP and 1 Water to fight fire on F4, removing the Fire Token. She moves 1 hex to F5 (Meadow) for 1 AP, then uses her last AP to resupply (not on a station, so she cannot; she instead moves to F6, a Ranger Station, spending her last AP). She resupplies during upkeep.

The Spotter is on tile D3 (adjacent to a Watchtower on D2). He scouts for free (role ability), peeks at the top 2 Wind Cards, and sees a Shifting Winds card on top. He warns the team. He then spends 2 AP to move to tile E4 and 2 AP to erect a Watchtower on E4 (Meadow, valid placement) using 2 Supply Tokens.

The Engineer is on tile C5 (Forest). She spends 1 AP (role discount) and 1 Supply to build a firebreak on the north edge of C5, anticipating the shifting wind. She spends another 1 AP and 1 Supply for a second firebreak on the northeast edge. With her remaining 2 AP, she moves to C4 and builds a third firebreak there (role ability allows a third on C4).

*Phase 2 -- Fire Spreads:*

The Shifting Winds card is drawn as the Spotter predicted. A second Wind Card is drawn: direction northeast, intensity 2. Fire on tile D6 spreads northeast to E6 and E7. The second wind direction is southeast, intensity 1. Fire on D6 also spreads to D7. However, the firebreak on C5's north edge blocks spread toward C5. The Watchtower on E4 catches an Ember Token that would have landed on E5, removing it.

*Phase 3 -- Upkeep:*

Round marker advances to 4. No firestorms occurred. An Event Card is drawn: "Supply Drop" -- 2 Water and 1 Supply placed on the nearest Ranger Station (F6, where the Hotshot is). The Hotshot resupplies, taking 2 Water and 1 Supply. All AP reset to 4.

The team has contained the eastern front thanks to the Engineer's firebreaks and the Spotter's early warning. But the southern fire line is growing, and the Wind Deck still holds surprises.
