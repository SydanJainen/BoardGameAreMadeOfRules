# **Wildfire Frontiers**

## Theme
In Wildfire Frontiers, players take on the roles of dedicated firefighters battling an encroaching wildfire through a scenic national park. The game simulates the intense and dynamic environment where every decision can mean the difference between saving lives and losing the battle against nature's fury. Players must work together to deploy water drops, evacuate towns, and create firebreaks before the flames consume everything in their path. The setting is both beautiful and perilous, making every move count as you fight not just the fire but also the unpredictable forces of nature.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Turn Structure**: Each player takes a turn in clockwise order, with specific phases for decision-making.
2. **Resource Management**: Players manage a limited set of resources to perform actions and maintain their position against the encroaching flames.
3. **Environmental Cards**: Wind cards dictate the spread of fire each round, adding an element of surprise and strategy.
4. **Team Coordination**: Players must coordinate their actions with others to maximize efficiency and effectiveness in battling the fire.
5. **Dynamic Fire Spread**: The fire's movement is not fixed but changes based on wind conditions, requiring players to adapt quickly.
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: 45-60 minutes

## Rules
### GAME NAME
**Wildfire Frontiers**

### THEME
In Wildfire Frontiers, players take on the roles of dedicated firefighters battling an encroaching wildfire through a scenic national park. The game simulates the intense and dynamic environment where every decision can mean the difference between saving lives and losing the battle against nature's fury. Players must work together to deploy water drops, evacuate towns, and create firebreaks before the flames consume everything in their path. The setting is both beautiful and perilous, making every move count as you fight not just the fire but also the unpredictable forces of nature.

### MECHANICS
1. **Turn Structure**: Each player takes a turn in clockwise order, with specific phases for decision-making.
2. **Resource Management**: Players manage a limited set of resources to perform actions and maintain their position against the encroaching flames.
3. **Environmental Cards**: Wind cards dictate the spread of fire each round, adding an element of surprise and strategy.
4. **Team Coordination**: Players must coordinate their actions with others to maximize efficiency and effectiveness in battling the fire.
5. **Dynamic Fire Spread**: The fire's movement is not fixed but changes based on wind conditions, requiring players to adapt quickly.

### COMPONENTS
1. **Game Board (hex grid, 37 tiles)** - A hexagonal board representing the national park with towns and wilderness areas.
2. **Wind Cards (40 cards)** - Each card represents a different wind direction and strength, affecting fire spread.
3. **Fire Tokens (50 tokens)** - Representing the wildfire's progression across the map.
4. **Resource Cards (160 total: 40 per type: Water Drops, Evacuation Points, Firebreaks)** - Used for various actions.
5. **Player Aid Sheets** - For tracking player positions and resource counts.
6. **Towns Tokens (36 tokens)** - Placed at the start on key locations.
7. **Fire Station Tokens (4 tokens)** - Starting positions of each player.

### PLAYERS
2-4 players

### DURATION
45-60 minutes

### SETUP
1. **Board Assembly**: Place the game board flat and ensure all hex tiles are properly connected to form a continuous map.
2. **Deck Preparation**: Shuffle the Wind Cards and set aside for later use.
3. **Initial Resource Distribution**: Each player receives 10 Water Drops, 5 Evacuation Points, and 5 Firebreaks at the start.
4. **Starting Positions**: Place one Fire Station Token on each of the four corners of the board. The players choose their starting Fire Station token and place it in front of them.
5. **First Player Selection**: The player with the most recent birthday goes first.

### RULES

#### A) TURN STRUCTURE
1. **Draw Wind Card Phase** (Mandatory): Each round, a new wind card is drawn to determine the fire spread for that turn.
2. **Fire Spread Phase** (Mandatory): Based on the wind direction and strength, the fire spreads across the board according to specific rules.
3. **Action Phase** (Optional): Players can perform any of their available actions as long as they have the necessary resources or meet any prerequisites.
4. **End Turn Phase** (Mandatory): End the turn by discarding excess resources if needed and drawing new ones.

#### B) ACTION CATALOG
1. **Deploy Water Drop**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: 2 Water Drops
   - Resolution: Place a Water Drop token on any hex adjacent to an unoccupied fire token.
   - Example: Alice spends 2 Water Drops and places a Water Drop token next to the fire on B3.

2. **Evacuate Town**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: 1 Evacuation Point, 1 Firebreak
   - Resolution: Remove the town token from an adjacent hex and discard both cards.
   - Example: Bob uses his Evacuation Point and Firebreak to save the town on C4.

3. **Build Firebreak**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: 2 Firebreaks, 1 Water Drop
   - Resolution: Place a Firebreak token in an adjacent hex.
   - Example: Eve spends 2 Firebreaks and 1 Water Drop to build a Firebreak on A5.

4. **Patrol**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: 1 Patrol Card (drawn at the start of each round)
   - Resolution: Move one token per card drawn, but not diagonally.
   - Example: Frank uses his Patrol Card to move from B2 to B3.

5. **Build Watchtower**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: 1 Wood and 1 Stone
   - Resolution: Place a Watchtower token on an unoccupied hex adjacent to the Fire Station token.
   - Example: Gina spends 1 Wood and 1 Stone to build a Watchtower on C3.

6. **Combine Actions**
   - When: Action Phase
   - Cost: Varies (depends on actions combined)
   - Resolution: Perform multiple actions in sequence, but each has its own cost.
   - Example: Dave uses his Water Drop token and Evacuation Point to both save a town and extinguish the adjacent fire.

#### C) PLAYER INTERACTION
- **Trading Rules**: Players can trade resources with each other, but must do so face-to-face or through a designated neutral mediator.
- **Combat/Conflict Resolution**: There is no direct combat. Players must use strategic placement of resources to prevent the fire from reaching certain areas.
- **Blocking and Area Control**: Players can block each other's actions by placing tokens in adjacent hexes, but this requires coordination and planning.

#### D) RESOURCE FLOW
1. **Gaining Resources**:
   - Each round, players draw 2 Resource Cards at the start of their turn to replenish resources.
2. **Spending Resources**: Used for performing actions as described above.
3. **Lost Resources**:
   - Fire tokens adjacent to a player’s token cost them 1 resource per fire token when they choose to extinguish it.

#### E) SPECIAL RULES
- **Wind Cards**: Each card has unique effects, such as causing the fire to spread in multiple directions or stopping at certain hexes.
- **Event Resolution**: Certain events on Wind Cards can trigger special actions that affect all players simultaneously.
- **Environmental Effects**: Natural obstacles and resources scattered across the board provide strategic advantages.

#### F) END GAME
The game ends when:
1. The fire reaches 3 towns, resulting in a loss for all players.
2. All towns are saved and the fire is contained within a designated boundary, leading to a win for all players.

Scoring:
- Each town saved = 5 points.
- Each Water Drop used = -1 point (to discourage hoarding).
- Additional points for strategic use of Firebreaks and Watchtowers.

Tiebreaker Rules: In the event of a tie in total score, the player with the most Fire Stations still operational wins.

#### G) EXAMPLE ROUND
**Player Setup**: Alice starts at B2, Bob at C4, Carol at D3.
- **Turn 1 (Alice)**:
  - Draw Wind Card: East wind, fire spreads to A3 and B3.
  - Action Phase: Deploy Water Drop on B3 (-2 Water Drops), Build Firebreak on A5 (-2 Firebreaks, -1 Water Drop).
- **Turn 2 (Bob)**:
  - Draw Wind Card: North wind, fire spreads to C4 and D4.
  - Action Phase: Evacuate Town at D3 (-1 Evacuation Point, -1 Firebreak), Build Watchtower on B5 (-1 Wood, -1 Stone).
- **Turn 3 (Carol)**:
  - Draw Wind Card: South wind, fire spreads to C2 and D2.
  - Action Phase: Combine actions: Deploy Water Drop on C4 (-2 Water Drops), Evacuate Town at C4 (-1 Evacuation Point, -1 Firebreak).

This example demonstrates the dynamic nature of the game, showing resource management, strategic decisions, and coordination among players.
