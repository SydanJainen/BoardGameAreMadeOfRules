# Scriptoria

## Theme
In Scriptoria, players take on the role of medieval scriptorium owners, competing to produce the most valuable illuminated manuscripts for patrons. As they collect rare pigments and recruit skilled monks, each completed manuscript improves their production chain, unlocking new opportunities and increasing competition among players. By navigating the challenges of a medieval scriptorium, players experience a rich narrative world that simulates the intricate dance of artistry, commerce, and innovation.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**: 1. **Resource Management:** Players manage three types of resources: Wood, Stone, and Crystal, which are used to construct buildings, recruit monks, and produce manuscripts. Resources can be gained through various means, such as building resource-generating structures or completing patron requests.
2. **Monk Recruitment:** Monks are recruited using resources and provide unique abilities that enhance the player's production chain. Each monk type has a specific ability, which is triggered when a manuscript of their corresponding rarity is produced.
3. **Manuscript Production:** Manuscripts are produced by combining resources with monastery buildings. The quality of the manuscript depends on the type of pigment used and the skill level of the monks involved. Patrons offer rewards for completed manuscripts, which can be redeemed for resources or used to recruit additional monks.
- **Players**: 2-4 players
- **Duration**: Estimated play time: 40-60 minutes

* 1 Game Board (hex grid

- each with a unique ability) - 5 of each type

- each requiring a different combination of resources and monks)
* 12 Patron Cards (3 types
- each offering rewards for completed manuscripts)
* 1 Rulebook

- featuring individual resource tracks and monk slots)

- each representing a unique challenge or opportunity)

## Rules
**SETUP:**

1. Assemble the game board by arranging the tiles in a hexagonal pattern.
2. Shuffle the Resource Card deck and deal 5 cards to each player, placing them on their respective resource tracks.
3. Each player selects a starting monk from the available pool (one per type).
4. Place the Pigment Card deck within reach of all players.
5. Determine the starting player randomly.

**RULES:**

A) **TURN STRUCTURE:** A turn consists of three phases:

1. **Action Phase:** During this phase, players can take one of the following actions:
	* Recruit a Monk (see Action Catalog for details)
	* Produce a Manuscript (see Action Catalog for details)
	* Collect Resources (automatic gain of resources based on monastery buildings and patron requests)
2. **Resolution Phase:** In this phase, players resolve any active effects or events from the previous turn.
3. **Cleanup Phase:** Players discard any used resources, cards, or event tokens.

B) **ACTION CATALOG:**

1. **Recruit a Monk:**
	* When a player has enough resources (Wood and Crystal), they can recruit a new monk using the resources and action points listed on their Player Board.
	* The player must also discard one Pigment Card of any rarity to fuel the recruitment process.
	* Example: Alice recruits a Scribe Monk, discarding 1 Iron Pigment Card.
2. **Produce a Manuscript:**
	* To produce a manuscript, a player must have the required resources (Wood, Stone, and Crystal) and meet the skill level requirement of their monks.
	* The player draws a Pigment Card and uses it to construct a manuscript template.
	* Example: Alice produces an Illuminated Gospel using 2 Wood, 1 Stone, and 1 Crystal. She draws a Gold Pigment Card and constructs a manuscript template.
3. **Collect Resources:**
	* When a player has completed a patron request or built a resource-generating structure, they collect the corresponding resources.
	* Example: Alice collects 1 Wood Resource Card after completing a patron request.

C) **PLAYER INTERACTION:**

1. **Trading:** Players can trade Pigment Cards with each other using the following rules:
	* Each player has a set of available Pigment Cards (listed on their Player Board).
	* When a player wants to trade, they must offer one or more Pigment Cards from their hand and receive an equal or lesser number of Pigment Cards from another player.
2. **Combat/Conflict Resolution:** There is no direct combat mechanism in Scriptoria. However, players can compete for resources by building structures that block other players' access to the same resource. The following rules apply:
	* When a player attempts to collect resources blocked by another player's structure, they must engage in a "resource duel."
	* Both players roll dice (1d6) and add their relevant resource values.
	* The player with the higher total wins the duel and collects the resources; the other player loses one of their Pigment Cards.
3. **Alliances and Negotiations:** Players can form temporary alliances or negotiate trades by agreeing to share resources, Pigment Cards, or monk abilities.

D) **RESOURCE FLOW:**

1. **Gaining Resources:** Players gain resources through:
	* Building resource-generating structures (Monastery Buildings).
	* Completing patron requests.
	* Recruiting monks with specific abilities.
2. **Spending Resources:** Players spend resources to construct buildings, produce manuscripts, and recruit new monks.
3. **Loss of Resources:** Resources are lost when a player's Pigment Card hand is depleted or when they discard a resource-generating structure.

E) **SPECIAL RULES:**

1. **Card Effects:** Pigment Cards have specific effects:
	* Rare Pigments (Gold, Silver): increase the quality of manuscripts produced.
	* Common Pigments (Red, Blue): decrease the cost of constructing buildings or recruiting monks.
2. **Environmental Effects:** Weather and events can affect gameplay:
	* A "Drought" event forces all players to discard one resource type.
	* A "Market Boom" event increases the reward for completing manuscripts.

F) **END GAME:**

1. **Victory Conditions:** Players earn victory points for completed manuscripts, Pigment Cards, and patron requests.
2. **Scoring:** Each completed manuscript is worth 10-20 points (depending on its rarity), each Pigment Card is worth 5 points, and each completed patron request earns a bonus of 5-10 points.

Tiebreaker rules: In case of a tie, the player with the most completed manuscripts wins. If still tied, the player with the highest total score from their Pigment Cards wins.

**EXAMPLE ROUND:** Here's an example round with two players:

Alice (Player 1):

* Action Phase:
	+ Recruits a Scribe Monk using 1 Wood and 1 Iron.
	+ Produces an Illuminated Gospel manuscript using 2 Wood, 1 Stone, and 1 Crystal.
	+ Discards 1 Red Pigment Card to fuel recruitment.
* Resolution Phase: None
* Cleanup Phase: Discards 1 resource card.

Bob (Player 2):

* Action Phase:
	+ Collects 1 Gold Resource Card after completing a patron request.
	+ Recruits an Apprentice Monk using 1 Wood and 1 Iron.
	+ Discards 1 Blue Pigment Card to fuel recruitment.
* Resolution Phase: None
* Cleanup Phase: Discards 1 resource card.

In this example round, Alice produces a completed manuscript (10 points) and collects resources. Bob completes a patron request (5 points) and recruits a new monk. The game continues with each player taking turns, competing for resources, Pigment Cards, and the pursuit of patronage.
