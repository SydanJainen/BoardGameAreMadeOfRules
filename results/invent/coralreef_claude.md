# Coralreef

## Theme
In Coralreef, players are marine biologists competing to cultivate the most thriving coral reef ecosystem on a shared ocean floor. Each player manages a personal research station while placing coral formations, seagrass beds, and rocky substrates onto a communal hexagonal grid that represents a stretch of tropical seabed. The conflict emerges from the tension between cooperation and competition: a healthy shared reef attracts rarer species that benefit everyone, yet each biologist is ultimately judged on the biodiversity flourishing within their own claimed territories. Ocean currents shift nutrients unpredictably, bleaching events threaten fragile corals, and migratory species follow their own logic, rewarding players who read the ecosystem rather than force it. The setting is compelling because the reef is alive, constantly reacting to every tile placed and every species introduced, creating an emergent puzzle where short-term exploitation risks long-term collapse.

## Game Details
- **Mechanics**:
  1. **Hex Tile Placement**: Players draft and place hexagonal habitat tiles (coral, seagrass, rock, sand, kelp, deep water) onto the shared ocean grid. Each tile type supports different species and interacts with adjacent tiles to form biomes. Placement decisions create spatial puzzles: grouping corals builds reefs that attract reef-dwelling fish, but monocultures are vulnerable to bleaching events.
  2. **Species Attraction via Matching**: Each species card lists a habitat pattern it requires (e.g., "2 adjacent coral + 1 seagrass"). When a player completes a pattern on the board with a tile they just placed, they may claim a matching species from the attraction pool. This creates a satisfying engine where building the right habitats pulls in the right creatures.
  3. **Biodiversity Set Collection**: Scoring is driven by the diversity of species a player has attracted. Species belong to five taxonomic groups (Coral, Fish, Invertebrate, Mammal, Algae). Collecting one of each group forms a "balanced ecosystem" worth bonus points. Duplicate species within a group yield diminishing returns, pushing players toward breadth over depth.
  4. **Current and Nutrient Flow**: A rotating current marker moves each round, designating which edge of the board receives nutrient tokens. Tiles adjacent to nutrient-rich hexes produce bonus attraction opportunities. Players must anticipate current shifts to position their habitats optimally.
  5. **Ecological Event Resolution**: An event deck introduces bleaching, algal blooms, predator migrations, and conservation grants. Events force reactive play and risk management, ensuring no single strategy dominates unchallenged.
- **Players**: 2-4
- **Duration**: 45-75 minutes

## Components
- 1 Ocean Board (modular hex grid frame, accommodates up to 61 hex spaces in concentric rings)
- 90 Habitat Tiles (15 each of 6 types: Coral, Seagrass, Rock, Sand, Kelp, Deep Water; hexagonal, double-sided with standard/premium habitat)
- 60 Species Cards (12 each across 5 taxonomic groups: Coral organisms, Fish, Invertebrates, Mammals, Algae)
- 30 Event Cards (6 Bleaching, 6 Algal Bloom, 6 Predator Migration, 6 Conservation Grant, 6 Seasonal Shift)
- 40 Nutrient Tokens (blue wooden cubes)
- 20 Research Tokens (white wooden discs, 5 per player)
- 4 Player Boards (dual-layer, with tracks for species collected, nutrients stored, and research progress)
- 4 Research Station Markers (colored wooden huts, 1 per player)
- 16 Territory Claim Markers (colored wooden rings, 4 per player)
- 1 Current Marker (wooden arrow on a rotating base)
- 1 Round Tracker (wooden fish on a 12-space track)
- 1 First Player Token (wooden seahorse)
- 1 Cloth Tile Bag
- 4 Player Reference Cards (summarizing actions, species requirements, and scoring)
- 1 Scorepad

## Rules

### A) TURN STRUCTURE

Each round consists of four phases performed in order. All players complete each phase before moving to the next.

**Phase 1 -- Current Phase (Mandatory, simultaneous)**
Move the Current Marker one position clockwise around the board edge. Place 1 Nutrient Token on each empty hex adjacent to the edge the current now points toward. If a hex already has a habitat tile, place the Nutrient Token on that tile. Maximum 2 Nutrient Tokens per hex.

**Phase 2 -- Draft Phase (Mandatory, simultaneous)**
Draw 4 Habitat Tiles from the bag and place them face-up in the center as the "Tidal Pool." Beginning with the first player and proceeding clockwise, each player drafts 1 tile from the Tidal Pool. Then, in reverse order, each player drafts a second tile. Remaining tiles are discarded to the box. Each player now holds exactly 2 newly drafted tiles plus any tiles held from previous rounds (hand limit: 4 tiles).

**Phase 3 -- Action Phase (Mandatory, sequential)**
Beginning with the first player and proceeding clockwise, each player takes a full turn consisting of exactly 2 actions chosen from the Action Catalog (section B). A player may take the same action twice or two different actions. After all players have completed their actions, proceed to Phase 4.

**Phase 4 -- Event Phase (Mandatory, simultaneous)**
Draw 1 Event Card from the event deck and resolve it immediately according to its text (see section E). Then advance the Round Tracker by 1 space. Pass the First Player Token clockwise.

### B) ACTION CATALOG

**1. Place Habitat**
- **When**: Action Phase (costs 1 of your 2 actions).
- **Cost**: Discard 1 Habitat Tile from your hand.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Choose 1 tile from your hand.
  2. Place it on any empty hex on the board. It must be adjacent to at least one already-placed tile (exception: if the board is empty, place it on the center hex).
  3. If the placed tile is adjacent to a hex containing Nutrient Tokens, take 1 Nutrient Token from that hex and place it on your Player Board in your nutrient reserve (maximum reserve: 6).
  4. Check whether the newly placed tile completes any species habitat pattern (see "Attract Species" action). You do not automatically attract; you must spend a separate action to do so.
- **Restrictions**: You must always have at least one tile in hand after the Draft Phase. You may not place a tile on a hex occupied by another tile or a Research Station.
- **Example**: Bob places a Coral tile on hex D4, adjacent to a Seagrass tile on D3 that holds 1 Nutrient Token. Bob takes that Nutrient Token onto his board. The coral-seagrass adjacency now partially matches the Clownfish card requirement (2 Coral + 1 Seagrass), but Bob needs one more Coral adjacent to complete the pattern.

**2. Attract Species**
- **When**: Action Phase (costs 1 of your 2 actions).
- **Cost**: 1 Nutrient Token from your reserve, returned to the general supply.
- **Prerequisites**: There must be a species card in the Attraction Pool whose habitat pattern is fully satisfied on the board, and at least one tile in that pattern must have been placed by you (marked with your Territory Claim Marker or placed this turn).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Identify a species card whose habitat pattern matches a contiguous group of tiles on the board.
  2. Pay 1 Nutrient Token.
  3. Take the species card and place it in the matching taxonomic group slot on your Player Board.
  4. Place 1 of your Territory Claim Markers on one tile in the matched pattern that does not already have a claim marker. This marks your ecological influence. (If all tiles in the pattern already have claim markers, you still take the species but place no new marker.)
  5. Refill the Attraction Pool to 5 cards from the Species Deck.
- **Restrictions**: Each specific habitat pattern on the board can only be used to attract 1 species per round. You cannot attract a species if you have no Nutrient Tokens. You may attract at most 2 species per round.
- **Example**: Alice has placed a second Coral tile on hex D5, adjacent to her earlier Coral on D4 and the Seagrass on D3. This completes the Clownfish pattern (2 Coral + 1 Seagrass in a contiguous group). She spends 1 Nutrient Token, takes the Clownfish card (Fish group), places it on her board, and puts a Territory Claim Marker on hex D5.

**3. Conduct Research**
- **When**: Action Phase (costs 1 of your 2 actions).
- **Cost**: 2 Nutrient Tokens from your reserve, returned to the general supply.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pay 2 Nutrient Tokens.
  2. Place 1 Research Token from your supply onto any habitat tile on the board (including tiles with other players' claim markers).
  3. Draw 2 Species Cards from the Species Deck, look at them, keep 1, and place the other on the bottom of the deck.
  4. The kept card goes into your hand as a "Studied Species" -- it does not go onto your Player Board yet. On a future Attract Species action, you may play a Studied Species from your hand instead of taking from the Attraction Pool, but you must still satisfy its habitat pattern and pay the Nutrient cost.
- **Restrictions**: Maximum 5 Research Tokens per player across the entire game. A hex may hold at most 1 Research Token regardless of owner.
- **Example**: Charlie pays 2 Nutrient Tokens, places a Research Token on hex E2 (a Kelp tile), draws 2 species cards -- Sea Otter (Mammal) and Hermit Crab (Invertebrate) -- keeps the Sea Otter, and returns the Hermit Crab to the bottom of the deck.

**4. Flip Habitat (Upgrade)**
- **When**: Action Phase (costs 1 of your 2 actions).
- **Cost**: 1 Nutrient Token from your reserve.
- **Prerequisites**: Target hex must contain a habitat tile with your Territory Claim Marker on it.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pay 1 Nutrient Token.
  2. Flip the habitat tile from its standard side to its premium side. Premium habitats count as 2 tiles of that type for the purpose of species pattern matching. (For example, a premium Coral counts as 2 Coral hexes.)
  3. Any Nutrient Tokens or Research Tokens on the tile remain.
- **Restrictions**: Each tile may only be flipped once. Once premium, it cannot revert. You may only flip tiles with your own claim marker.
- **Example**: Alice pays 1 Nutrient Token and flips her Coral tile on hex D4 to its premium side. Now D4 alone satisfies "2 Coral" in future pattern checks, making it much easier to attract coral-dependent species.

**5. Harvest Nutrients**
- **When**: Action Phase (costs 1 of your 2 actions).
- **Cost**: None.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Choose up to 3 habitat tiles on the board that have Nutrient Tokens on them. At least 1 of the chosen tiles must have your Territory Claim Marker on it.
  2. Take 1 Nutrient Token from each chosen tile and add them to your reserve (respecting the 6-token reserve limit; excess tokens stay on the board).
- **Restrictions**: Once per turn (you cannot use both of your actions on Harvest Nutrients in the same turn).
- **Example**: Bob has claim markers on hexes D4 and F1. Hex D4 has 2 Nutrient Tokens, hex F1 has 1, and hex E3 (no claim marker) has 1 Nutrient Token. Bob takes 1 from D4, 1 from F1, and because he has at least one claimed tile, he may also take 1 from E3. He adds 3 Nutrient Tokens to his reserve.

**6. Relocate Habitat**
- **When**: Action Phase (costs 1 of your 2 actions).
- **Cost**: 1 Nutrient Token from your reserve.
- **Prerequisites**: Target hex must contain a habitat tile with no Territory Claim Markers from any player.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pay 1 Nutrient Token.
  2. Pick up 1 unclaimed habitat tile from the board and add it to your hand (respecting the 4-tile hand limit).
  3. Any Nutrient Tokens on that tile return to the general supply.
- **Restrictions**: You cannot relocate a tile that has any claim marker or Research Token on it. You cannot relocate a tile if removing it would split the board into disconnected groups of tiles.
- **Example**: Charlie pays 1 Nutrient Token and picks up an unclaimed Sand tile from hex A1, adding it to his hand for future placement in a more strategic location.

### C) PLAYER INTERACTION

**Shared Board / Area Control**: All players place tiles on the same ocean grid. Placing tiles in key positions can block opponents from completing species patterns or claim nutrient-rich zones. Territory Claim Markers create soft ownership: you need at least one claimed tile in a pattern to attract its species, so claiming tiles in contested areas is strategic.

**Nutrient Competition**: Nutrient Tokens are a shared, finite resource on the board. The Harvest Nutrients action allows you to take from any tile as long as you have at least one claimed tile among your selections, meaning players compete for the same nutrient deposits.

**Pattern Blocking**: Because each habitat pattern on the board can only be used for 1 attraction per round, placing a tile that completes a pattern an opponent was building toward -- and then attracting that species first -- is a powerful competitive move.

**No Direct Trading**: Players do not trade cards, tokens, or tiles with each other. All interaction is spatial and resource-competitive.

**Research Token Placement**: Research Tokens can be placed on any tile, including opponents' claimed tiles. While this does not displace the claim marker, it occupies the research slot, preventing the opponent from placing their own Research Token there later.

**Indirect Cooperation**: Because a richer, more diverse board benefits species attraction for everyone, there is an incentive to contribute constructively to the shared reef. However, the scoring system rewards individual collection, creating a natural tension.

### D) RESOURCE FLOW

**Nutrient Tokens (Primary Resource)**
- **Gained**: During the Current Phase, tokens are placed on board hexes near the current edge. Players collect them via the Harvest Nutrients action or by placing tiles adjacent to nutrient-bearing hexes.
- **Spent**: Attract Species (1 token), Conduct Research (2 tokens), Flip Habitat (1 token), Relocate Habitat (1 token).
- **Storage Limit**: Each player may hold at most 6 Nutrient Tokens in their reserve. Excess must remain on the board.
- **Refresh Cycle**: New tokens enter every round via the Current Phase. The current rotates, so different board edges receive nutrients on different rounds, preventing any single area from permanent abundance.

**Habitat Tiles (Building Resource)**
- **Gained**: 2 tiles per round via the Draft Phase, plus any recovered via Relocate Habitat.
- **Spent**: 1 tile per Place Habitat action.
- **Storage Limit**: Hand limit of 4 tiles. Excess must be discarded.
- **Exhaustion**: When the tile bag is empty, the Draft Phase draws whatever remains (potentially fewer than 4 tiles). If no tiles remain, skip the Draft Phase.

**Species Cards (Victory Points)**
- **Gained**: Via Attract Species (from the Attraction Pool) or Conduct Research (from the deck).
- **Spent**: Never spent; once collected, species remain on your Player Board permanently.
- **Attraction Pool**: Always maintained at 5 face-up cards. Refilled immediately after any card is taken.

**Research Tokens (Limited-Use Asset)**
- **Gained**: Each player starts with 5. They are never replenished.
- **Spent**: 1 per Conduct Research action, placed permanently on the board.

### E) SPECIAL RULES

**Event Cards (drawn one per round in Phase 4):**

- **Bleaching (6 cards)**: Target a specific habitat type (named on the card). All standard (non-premium) tiles of that type that have no Nutrient Tokens on them are flipped face-down and become "Bleached." Bleached tiles count as barren for species pattern matching. A bleached tile can be restored by placing 1 Nutrient Token on it during a future Harvest action (flip it back face-up). Premium tiles of the named type are immune.

- **Algal Bloom (6 cards)**: Place 1 Algal Bloom Token (use a spare nutrient token turned sideways) on each hex at the current-facing edge. Hexes with Algal Bloom tokens cannot have species attracted from patterns that include them until the bloom is cleared. A bloom clears automatically at the start of the next Current Phase.

- **Predator Migration (6 cards)**: A predator species arrives. Each player who has 3 or more Fish-group species must discard 1 Fish-group species card of their choice (returned to the bottom of the Species Deck). Players with fewer than 3 Fish are unaffected.

- **Conservation Grant (6 cards)**: Each player receives 2 Nutrient Tokens from the general supply (respecting the 6-token reserve limit).

- **Seasonal Shift (6 cards)**: Move the Current Marker 2 additional positions clockwise (instead of the normal 1 at the start of next round). This effectively skips an edge, disrupting nutrient expectations.

**Premium Habitat Bonus**: When scoring at game end, each premium-side tile with your claim marker on it is worth 1 bonus point.

**Biodiversity Milestone**: The first player to collect at least 1 species from each of the 5 taxonomic groups immediately gains 3 bonus points (tracked on the scorepad). Only the first player to achieve this receives the bonus.

---

### F) END GAME

**Trigger**: The game ends at the conclusion of the round in which the Round Tracker reaches space 12 (i.e., after 12 complete rounds). Alternatively, if the Species Deck and Attraction Pool are both completely empty, the game ends at the conclusion of the current round.

**Scoring**:

1. **Species Diversity (primary)**: For each taxonomic group, score points based on how many unique species you collected:
   - 1 species in a group = 1 point
   - 2 species in a group = 3 points
   - 3 species in a group = 6 points
   - 4+ species in a group = 10 points

2. **Balanced Ecosystem Bonus**: For each complete set of 5 species (one from each taxonomic group), score 5 bonus points. A single species card can only count toward one set.

3. **Territory Bonus**: Score 1 point for each of your Territory Claim Markers on the board.

4. **Premium Habitat Bonus**: Score 1 point for each premium-side tile bearing your Territory Claim Marker.

5. **Biodiversity Milestone**: 3 points if you were the first to collect from all 5 groups (already tracked during play).

6. **Remaining Nutrients**: Score 1 point per 3 Nutrient Tokens remaining in your reserve (rounded down).

**Total**: Sum all categories. Highest total wins.

**Tiebreaker**: In case of a tie, the tied player with more total species cards wins. If still tied, the player with more Territory Claim Markers on the board wins. If still tied, the players share the victory.

### G) EXAMPLE ROUND

**Setup context**: Round 5 of a 3-player game (Alice, Bob, Charlie). The board has approximately 20 tiles placed. The current marker points to the north edge.

**Phase 1 -- Current Phase**
The Current Marker advances one position clockwise to the northeast edge. Nutrient Tokens are placed: hexes B1, B2, and C1 (adjacent to the northeast edge) each receive 1 Nutrient Token. Hex B2 already has 1 token, so it now has 2 (the maximum).

**Phase 2 -- Draft Phase**
Four tiles are drawn from the bag: Coral, Kelp, Rock, Sand. Draft order (Alice is first player):
- Alice drafts Coral.
- Bob drafts Kelp.
- Charlie drafts Rock.
- (Reverse) Charlie also takes Sand.
- Bob receives nothing further (only 4 tiles drawn for 3 players; the 2-per-player rule applies: Alice gets 1, Bob gets 1, Charlie gets 2 because reverse order gives Charlie the pick).

Correction for clarity with standard draft: 4 tiles, 3 players, each takes 1 forward then 1 reverse until tiles run out:
- Forward: Alice takes Coral, Bob takes Kelp, Charlie takes Rock.
- Reverse: Charlie takes Sand.
- Alice and Bob each have 1 new tile; Charlie has 2. All within hand limits.

**Phase 3 -- Action Phase**

**Alice's Turn (2 actions):**
- **Action 1: Place Habitat.** Alice places her Coral tile on hex C3, adjacent to her existing Coral on C2 and a Seagrass tile on D3. Hex D3 has 1 Nutrient Token; Alice takes it (reserve now: 3 tokens). She notices the pattern for Clownfish (2 Coral + 1 Seagrass) is now complete across C2-C3-D3.
- **Action 2: Attract Species.** Alice pays 1 Nutrient Token (reserve: 2). She claims the Clownfish (Fish group) from the Attraction Pool and places it on her Player Board. She puts a Territory Claim Marker on hex C3. The Attraction Pool refills to 5 cards.

**Bob's Turn (2 actions):**
- **Action 1: Place Habitat.** Bob places his Kelp tile on hex E2, adjacent to his claimed Rock tile on E1. Hex E1 has no nutrients, so Bob gains nothing extra.
- **Action 2: Harvest Nutrients.** Bob selects hex B2 (2 Nutrient Tokens, has his claim marker), hex B1 (1 Nutrient Token, no claim marker), and hex C1 (1 Nutrient Token, no claim marker). Since B2 has his claim marker, the selection is valid. He takes 1 from each of the 3 hexes, gaining 3 tokens (reserve now: 4). Hex B2 retains 1 token.

**Charlie's Turn (2 actions):**
- **Action 1: Conduct Research.** Charlie pays 2 Nutrient Tokens (reserve: 1). He places a Research Token on hex D3 (the Seagrass tile -- Alice has a nearby claim but not on D3 itself). He draws 2 Species Cards: Giant Clam (Invertebrate) and Manta Ray (Fish). He keeps Giant Clam as a Studied Species in hand and returns Manta Ray to the bottom of the deck.
- **Action 2: Place Habitat.** Charlie places his Rock tile on hex F3, adjacent to existing tiles on F2. No nutrients nearby. He now holds 1 remaining tile (the Sand) for a future turn.

**Phase 4 -- Event Phase**
An Event Card is drawn: **Conservation Grant**. Each player receives 2 Nutrient Tokens from the supply. Alice now has 4, Bob has 6 (at the cap), Charlie has 3. The Round Tracker advances to space 6. The First Player Token passes to Bob.

## Setup

1. **Assemble the Board**: Place the modular ocean board frame in the center of the table. All 61 hex spaces begin empty.
2. **Prepare Tiles**: Place all 90 Habitat Tiles in the cloth bag and shuffle thoroughly.
3. **Prepare Species Deck**: Shuffle all 60 Species Cards. Deal 5 face-up next to the board to form the Attraction Pool. Place the remaining deck face-down nearby.
4. **Prepare Event Deck**: Shuffle all 30 Event Cards face-down next to the board.
5. **Distribute Player Materials**: Each player takes 1 Player Board, 1 Research Station Marker (of their chosen color), 4 Territory Claim Markers (matching color), 5 Research Tokens, and 1 Player Reference Card.
6. **Starting Nutrients**: Each player receives 3 Nutrient Tokens from the general supply and places them on their Player Board reserve.
7. **Seed the Reef**: Draw 3 Habitat Tiles from the bag. Place them on the 3 center hexes of the board (the center hex and 2 adjacent hexes of the active player's choice). These starting tiles have no claim markers.
8. **Set Current Marker**: Place the Current Marker pointing toward the north edge of the board.
9. **Round Tracker**: Place the wooden fish on space 1 of the 12-space round track.
10. **First Player**: The player who most recently visited the ocean takes the seahorse token and becomes first player. If no one qualifies, determine randomly.
