# **Silk Routes**

## Theme
In "Silk Routes," players take on the roles of ambitious merchants competing to fulfill royal orders and amass wealth along the ancient Silk Road. The game takes place in a richly detailed world where caravans traverse treacherous landscapes, encounter rival traders, and navigate through the shifting demands of empires. The conflict arises from the scarcity of resources and the need to outmaneuver competitors to secure lucrative trade routes. This setting is compelling because it captures the essence of exploration, negotiation, and risk-taking that defined the early days of global commerce.

### Setup
1. **Assemble the Board**: Lay out the hexagonal tiles in a pattern that forms a path resembling the Silk Road, ensuring there is enough space for all players.
2. **Prepare Resource Cards**: Shuffle each type of resource card separately and place them face down near the board.
3. **Initial Resource Distribution**: Each player receives 10 resource cards (4 Silk, 3 Spices, 2 Gold, 1 Ivory).
4. **Starting Positions**: Place a Caravan Token on the starting tile for each player.
5. **First Player Selection**: The youngest player starts; if there is a tie, use rock-paper-scissors.

### Rules

#### A) Turn Structure
Each turn consists of the following phases in order:
1. **Action Phase** (mandatory): Players can perform one action per phase.
2. **Trading Phase** (optional): Players may trade with other players or the market.
3. **Event Phase** (mandatory): Draw an Event Card and resolve it.

#### B) Action Catalog
- **Move Caravan**: Spend 1 Silk to move your caravan to an adjacent hex. If moving into a new tile, draw 2 resource cards from that tile’s supply.
- **Build Watchtower**: Spend 3 Spices and place a Watchtower token on any unoccupied tile within range of your caravan. On your next turn, you can perform a Watchtower Action (see below).
- **Watchtower Action**: If you have a Watchtower in an adjacent hex, spend 1 Silk to gain 2 resources from that hex.
- **Upgrade Caravan**: Spend 5 Gold and choose one upgrade: +2 movement speed or +1 storage capacity. Upgrades last until the next turn.
- **Trade**: Spend 1 Silk to trade with another player or the market (see below).

**Trading Resolution**:
   - To trade, a player must declare their offer (e.g., "I will give you 3 Spices for your Silk.")
   - The other party can accept or counteroffer. If no agreement is reached, both players discard 1 card each.
   
#### C) Player Interaction
- **Trading**: Players can trade goods with one another at any time during the game. There are no restrictions on how many times a player can trade in a single turn.
- **Watchtowers**: Placing a Watchtower blocks movement and resource collection for adjacent hexes.

#### D) Resource Flow
- **Resource Gain**: Gained by moving onto tiles with resources or trading. Hand size is limited to 10 cards at any time.
- **Resource Spend**: Used in actions like building, upgrading caravans, and trading.
- **Resource Loss**: Occurs when trading with the market or losing a card during an event.

#### E) Special Rules
- **Event Cards**:
   - Type A: Resource Bonuses (e.g., +2 Silk)
   - Type B: Movement Restrictions (e.g., cannot move for 1 turn)
   - Type C: Trade Disruptions (e.g., no trading with a specific player for the next turn)

#### F) End Game
The game ends when one player has fulfilled all of their royal orders, as indicated on their order card. Victory points are calculated based on:
- Royal Orders Fulfilled (1 point per order)
- Total Resource Cards in Hand at End of Turn (3 points per card)
- Caravan Upgrades (2 points for each upgrade level)

Tiebreakers: 
1. Highest number of royal orders fulfilled.
2. Most resource cards in hand.

#### G) Example Round
**Example with 3 Players: Alice, Bob, and Carol**

**Alice's Turn**:
- Moves caravan to adjacent tile containing Silk (Action Phase).
- Trades 2 Spices for 1 Gold from Bob (Trading Phase).
- Draws 2 Event Cards: Type A (gain +1 Silk) and Type C (cannot trade with Carol).

**Bob's Turn**:
- Moves caravan to a new hex with Ivory (Action Phase).
- Builds a Watchtower on adjacent unoccupied tile (costs 3 Spices, Action Phase).
- Draws 2 Event Cards: Type B (cannot move for next turn) and Type A (+1 Silk).

**Carol's Turn**:
- Moves caravan to an adjacent hex containing Spices (Action Phase).
- Upgrades Caravan with +1 Storage Capacity (Upgrade Caravan, cost 5 Gold, Action Phase).
- Draws 2 Event Cards: Type C (cannot trade with Alice) and Type B (cannot move for next turn).

This example demonstrates the game flow and shows how different actions can be taken in a single round.

### YOUR COMPLETE GAME DESIGN
In "Silk Routes," players compete to fulfill royal orders by managing their caravans, trading resources, and navigating the Silk Road. The strategic depth lies in balancing resource management, upgrading capabilities, and making tactical decisions during turns and interactions with other players. By carefully planning each turn and leveraging the dynamic environment, players can achieve success and claim victory through a combination of skillful trade, shrewd negotiation, and clever use of events to their advantage.
