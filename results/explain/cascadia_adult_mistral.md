# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

**Cascadia: A Game of Balance and Nature**

**Objective:** Create the most biodiverse habitat by strategically placing wildlife and habitats while managing nature tokens and actions to maximize your score at the end of the game.

**Components:**
- 27 Habitat Tiles (5 each of Mountains, Wetlands, Prairies, Rivers; 3 Forests)
- 10 Wildlife Tokens (2 each of Bears, Elk, Salmon, Hawks, Foxes)
- 8 Nature Tokens
- 5 Wildlife Scoring Cards
- 5 Achievement Hiking Tracks
- 1 Game Board (map of Cascadia)

**Setup:**
1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Shuffle the habitat tiles and place them face down on their corresponding spaces on the board.
3. Shuffle the wildlife tokens and place them face up on the board, mixing them together randomly.
4. Give each player 8 nature tokens, a Wildlife Scoring Card (chosen at random), and their first Habitat Tile from the top of the pile.
5. Choose who goes first by playing a round of "Simon Says".

**Gameplay:**

On your turn, perform these phases in order:

1. **Take a Nature Token Action:** If you have 2 or more nature tokens, you may choose to spend them to gain one of the following:
   - Draw and place an additional habitat tile.
   - Place two wildlife tokens on any habitats already in play.
   - Discard your current Wildlife Scoring Card and draw a new one.

2. **Place Your Habitat Tile:** Place your tile on any edge of another tile that is already on the board, following these rules:
   - Tiles must be placed orthogonally (only horizontally or vertically).
   - No two habitats of the same type can touch each other.
   - At least one wildlife token must be present in an adjacent habitat to place your tile.

3. **Place Your Wildlife Tokens:** If you have any, place them on habitats that meet their placement requirements:
   - Each wildlife type has its own unique placement rules (e.g., Bears cannot touch Salmon).
   - If there is no valid spot for a wildlife token, it remains with the player until their next turn.

4. **Check for Overpopulation:** If any habitat contains 3 or more of the same wildlife type, those wildlife tokens are removed from play and returned to the supply.

5. **Draw Habitat and Wildlife Tokens:** Draw one habitat tile from the top of the pile and place it face down on its corresponding space on the board. Then draw two wildlife tokens (one at a time) and place them face up in their supply spaces.

6. **Score Points:** If you completed all phases without discarding any nature tokens, gain 1 point. Also, score points based on the achievements marked on your Wildlife Scoring Card for this turn.

**Endgame:**
The game ends when either:
- All habitat tiles have been placed.
- One player reaches a total of 20 points or more (including bonus points from achievements).

Players tally their final scores and compare them to determine the winner(s) based on these criteria:

1. Most total points from biodiverse habitats, nature token actions, and achievements.
2. If tied, the player who placed the most unique wildlife tokens wins.
3. In case of a continued tie, both players share the victory.

**Solo Mode:**
Cascadia can be played solo using scenarios or achievements to challenge yourself. Follow the instructions provided for each mode to track your progress on the Achievement Hiking Tracks.
