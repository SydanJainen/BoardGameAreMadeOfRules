# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

**Welcome to Pandemic!**

**Objective:**
The goal of Pandemic is to cure all four global diseases before they overwhelm humanity. You'll work together with your teammates as a disease-fighting team, traveling the world, treating infections, and discovering cures.

**Game Components:**

1. **Game Board:** A world map with cities connected by lines.
2. **Player Pawns (6):** Representing you and your teammates. Each pawn has a unique color.
3. **Disease Cubes (4 colors, 40 cubes each):** These represent the four diseases spreading across the globe.
4. **Epidemic Cards (24):** Special event cards that can be played at any time during the game.
5. **Player Cards (108):** These are used to move around the map and perform actions like treating diseases, discovering cures, etc.
6. **Infection Discard Pile:** A place for discarded infection cards.
7. **Infection Draw Pile:** A pile of infection cards that will be drawn each turn to represent disease spreading.
8. **Player Discard Pile:** Where player cards go after they've been used or discarded.
9. **Player Draw Pile:** Where player cards are taken from when players draw new cards at the end of their turns.

**Setup:**

1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Shuffle the disease cubes and place them on the matching cities according to their color.
3. Shuffle the infection cards and place them face down in the infection discard pile, then shuffle again and place them face up in the infection draw pile.
4. Choose a player to go first. That player chooses one of the six roles (each role has unique abilities) and takes the corresponding pawn.
5. The other players choose their roles and take their pawns. Each role gets 3-4 cards according to their role's ability. The remaining cards are shuffled and placed face down in the player draw pile.

**Gameplay:**

**Each player's turn consists of four phases:**

1. **Draw Cards:** Draw two player cards from the player draw pile. If you draw an epidemic card, follow its instructions (increase infection rate, intensify infection, infect city).
2. **Take Actions:** Spend your action points to move around the board using player cards or perform actions like treating diseases, discovering cures at research stations, building research stations, sharing knowledge with teammates.
3. **Infect Cities:** Draw infection cards from the infection draw pile equal to the current infection rate (tracked on the game board). Add cubes to the matching cities according to the drawn card's color.
4. **Clean Up:** Discard used player and infection cards into their respective discard piles.

**Winning & Losing:**

- The team wins instantly once all four diseases have been cured, regardless of the number of infected cities.
- Players lose if any of these conditions occur:
  - 8 outbreaks (outbreaks marker reaches skull)
  - A disease has no more cubes left when a player needs to infect a city
  - Not enough cards in the player draw pile when a player must draw cards

**Roles & Actions:**

Each role has unique abilities and starts with certain cards. Here's a brief overview:

1. **Medic:** Can treat diseases anywhere on the board, not just adjacent cities.
2. **Researcher:** Discards fewer cards to discover cures due to their extra card at setup.
3. **Scientist:** Needs only 4 cards of one color to discover cure instead of usual 5.
4. **Operations Expert:** Builds research stations using only one action point.
5. **Dispatcher:** Uses chartered flight cards to move others' pawns and vice versa.
6. **Quarantine Specialist:** Prevents outbreaks in cities connected by a quarantine card.

Each player gets four actions per turn, with some roles allowing more actions or special abilities on certain turns.

**Special Event Cards:**
Play these at any time during your turn for their effects:
- Airship: Move anywhere once
- Government Grant: Draw 2 extra cards from player draw pile
- One Quiet Night in a Hotel: Skip infecting cities phase this turn
- Forecast: Prevent outbreaks in one city next turn
- Emergency Shipment: Take cure marker of your choice and place it vial-side up

**Discuss & Plan:**
Players may openly discuss strategies during the game, but like the real world, they don't immediately know everything each other does. Players must convey what cards they have.

Now that you understand Pandemic's rules, get ready to save the world from diseases! Good luck!
