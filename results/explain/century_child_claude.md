# Century: Spice Road - Rules Explanation (For Kids!)

## What Is This Game About?

Imagine you are a spice trader traveling along ancient roads, collecting colorful spices and trading them to earn points. The player with the most points at the end wins!

Spices are represented by little colored cubes:
- **Yellow** (Turmeric) - the most common spice
- **Red** (Saffron) - a bit more valuable
- **Green** (Cardamom) - even more valuable
- **Brown** (Cinnamon) - the rarest and most valuable

Think of it like a ladder: yellow is at the bottom, and brown is at the top.

## Setting Up the Game

1. Shuffle the **Point cards** (the ones with orange backs) and lay 5 face-up in a row, with the rest as a deck nearby.
2. Put gold coins above the first point card and silver coins above the second one. Use twice as many coins as there are players.
3. Every player gets two starter cards: a **Create 2** card and an **Upgrade 2** card. These go in your hand.
4. Shuffle the **Merchant cards** (purple backs) and lay 6 face-up in a row, with the rest as a deck nearby.
5. Put all the colored cubes in the bowls so everyone can reach them.
6. Each player gets a **Caravan card** (like a little tray for your cubes). The player whose card has a special symbol goes first.
7. The first player starts with 3 yellow cubes. The second and third players get 4 yellow cubes. The fourth and fifth players get 3 yellow cubes plus 1 red cube.

## What Do You Do on Your Turn?

On your turn, you pick **one** of these four things to do:

### 1. Play a Card
Take a card from your hand and put it face-up in front of you. Then do what the card says:
- **Spice Cards** let you grab cubes from the bowls and put them on your caravan.
- **Upgrade Cards** let you swap a cube for one that is one step better (yellow becomes red, red becomes green, green becomes brown). An "Upgrade 2" card lets you do this twice!
- **Trade Cards** let you swap some cubes from your caravan for different cubes from the bowls. You can repeat the same trade as many times as you want, as long as you have enough cubes.

### 2. Acquire a Merchant Card
Pick a face-up Merchant card from the row and add it to your hand. But there is a cost! You have to put one cube from your caravan on each card that sits to the LEFT of the one you want. The very first card in the row is free! If the card you pick up has cubes on it from other players, you get to keep those cubes too.

After you take a card, slide the remaining cards to the left and flip a new one from the deck.

### 3. Rest
Pick up ALL the cards you have played in front of you and put them back in your hand. Now you can use them again!

### 4. Claim a Point Card
If you have the exact cubes shown on a Point card, you can return those cubes to the bowls and take the Point card. That card is worth points at the end! If you claim one of the first two Point cards in the row, you also get a gold or silver coin (bonus points!). Then slide cards left and draw a new one.

## Important Rules to Remember

- Your caravan can hold a **maximum of 10 cubes**. If you ever have more than 10, you must put some back.
- You can only do **one action** per turn.
- Gold coins are worth **3 points** and silver coins are worth **1 point** at the end.
- Each non-yellow cube left on your caravan at the end is also worth **1 point**.

## How Does the Game End?

The game ends when someone collects their **5th Point card** (or 6th if there are only 2 or 3 players). Finish the round so everyone gets the same number of turns. Then add up all your points from Point cards, coins, and leftover non-yellow cubes. The player with the most points wins!

If there is a tie, the person who took their turn last wins.

## Quick Tips for New Players

- Try to get Merchant cards that work well together.
- Do not forget to rest so you can reuse your cards!
- Pay attention to what Point cards are available and collect the cubes you need for them.
- Gold and silver coins are a nice bonus, so try to claim the first or second Point card when you can.

Have fun trading spices!
