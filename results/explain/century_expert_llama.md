3. **Rest**: A player can rest, taking all previously played cards back into their hand. This allows players to rebuild their Merchant card deck and gain an advantage.
4. **Claim**: To claim a Point card, a player must have all the cubes shown on the point card in their Caravan. They return those cubes to the bowls, take the Point card, and place it face down in front of them.

**Merchant Card Effects**

There are three types of Merchant cards: Spice Cards, Upgrade Cards, and Trade Cards.

**Strategic Considerations**

The 'Trade Card' allows players to gain additional cubes by trading away excess cubes from their Caravan. This can be a powerful strategy, especially when combined with the 'Spice Cards'.

**Edge Cases and Rule Interactions**

* When acquiring a Merchant card, any cubes that were on that card are placed on the player's Caravan. This creates an empty slot to the left of the merchant deck, which is filled by drawing a new card from the deck.
* If a player claims a Point card and has all the required cubes in their Caravan, they take the corresponding gold or silver token.

**Game End**

The game ends when a player takes their fifth Point card (or sixth in a 2-3 player game). The player with the most points wins. In case of a tie, the last player to take a turn wins.
