# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

**Ticket to Ride: North America**

**Components:**
- Map of North American train routes
- Colored Train Cars (Blue, Red, Green, Yellow, Black; 45 each)
- Scoring Markers (for each player color)
- Train Car cards (12 each of 8 types, plus Locomotives)
- Destination Ticket cards (30)
- Longest Path Bonus card
- Rules booklet

**Setup:**
1. Place the board map in the center.
2. Each player takes a set of colored Train Cars and matching Scoring Marker, placing it on 'Start' on the scoring track.
3. Shuffle Train Car cards, deal 4 to each player, and place the remaining deck face down with five cards face up.
4. Place the Longest Path Bonus card face up near the board.
5. Shuffle Destination Ticket cards, deal 3 to each player; they keep at least two.
6. Players keep their Destination Tickets secret until the end of the game.

**Objective:**
Score the most points by claiming routes, completing Destination Tickets, and having the longest continuous path of routes.

**Gameplay:**
The game is played in turns, clockwise. On your turn, perform one of these actions:

1. **Draw Train Car Cards:** Draw 2 cards (one face-up or blind draw), replace any taken face-up card.
   - Locomotive cards act as wildcards but follow specific drawing rules.

2. **Claim a Route:** Play a set of matching-colored cards equal to the route's length, place your colored trains on the route, and score points by moving your Scoring Marker along the scoring track (see Route Scoring Table).

3. **Draw Destination Tickets:** Draw 3 new tickets; keep at least one.

**Route Scoring:**
| Route Length | Points Scored |
| --- | --- |
| 1-3 spaces | 1 point |
| 4-6 spaces | 2 points |
| 7+ spaces | 4 points |

**Game End and Winning:**
When a player runs out of 1 or 2 trains, each player gets one final turn. The game ends, and players calculate their final scores as follows:
- Points earned from routes
- Plus/minus Destination Ticket points (based on completion)
- Longest Continuous Path bonus (10 points, ties split)

The player with the most points wins; in case of a tie, the player who completed the most Destination Tickets wins. If still tied, the player with the longest continuous path wins.

**Special Rules:**
- Gray routes can be claimed using any color set.
- Double-Routes: Only one route can be used in 2 or 3-player games; once a double-route is claimed, it's closed to other players.
