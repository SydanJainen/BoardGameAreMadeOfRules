# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

Welcome to Wingspan, a competitive bird-collecting engine-building game designed by Elizabeth Hargrave. In this game, players take on the role of bird enthusiasts, researching, watching, and collecting birds to create the most impressive aviary.

**Setup**

Each player receives:

* 1 player mat
* 8 action cubes of one color
* 2 random bonus cards
* 5 random bird cards
* 5 food tokens (one of each type: , , , , )

Players keep up to 5 bird cards and discard the others. For each bird card kept, they must discard 1 food token. Players choose 1 bonus card to keep and discard the other.

**Gameplay**

A game of Wingspan consists of 4 rounds. Each round is divided into a turn structure:

1. **Play a Bird**: Place an action cube on the "Play a Bird" spot above where you will play the bird. Pay the bird's food and egg cost, then place the bird on your mat.
2. **Gain Food and Activate Forest Bird Powers**: Gain food by placing an action cube in the leftmost exposed slot of the "gain food" row. Draw dice from the birdfeeder and gain the corresponding amount of food tokens. Discard a die to refill the birdfeeder.
3. **Lay Eggs and Activate Grassland Bird Powers**: Lay eggs by placing an action cube in the leftmost exposed slot of the "lay eggs" row. Gain an egg token from the supply and place it on a bird card with space for it.
4. **Draw Bird Cards and Activate Wetland Bird Powers**: Draw bird cards by placing an action cube in the leftmost exposed slot of the "draw cards" row. If the slot shows an egg-to-card bonus conversion, discard at most 1 egg to draw an additional card.

**End-of-Round Goals**

At the end of each round, players score points for their birds and eggs based on the following goals:

* **Green**: Majority of the targeted item (e.g., most birds in a habitat)
	+ Score points by counting your quantity of the targeted item. Compare with other players to determine rank order.
* **Blue**: One point per targeted item
	+ Score 1 point for each item, up to a maximum of 5 points.

**Bonus Cards**

Bonus cards are scored at the end of the game. Each player starts with 1 bonus card and can gain additional ones by playing specific bird cards.

**Winning Conditions**

The winner is the player with the most points accumulated from birds, bonus cards, end-of-round goals, eggs, cached food, and tucked birds. In case of a tie, the player with the most unused food tokens wins. If players are still tied, they share the victory.

**Key Concepts**

* **Food Tokens**: Used to play bird cards, gain food, or cache food.
* **Egg Tokens**: Represented on bird cards; used to lay eggs or score points for end-of-round goals.
* **Bird Powers**: Activated by playing birds; can provide benefits or penalties.
* **Cache**: A food token stored on a bird card for later use.

By following these rules, players will engage in a strategic game of bird collecting, power management, and resource optimization. Will you build the most impressive aviary and emerge as the winner?
