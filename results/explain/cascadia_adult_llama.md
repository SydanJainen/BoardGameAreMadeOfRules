# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

Welcome to Cascadia, a wildlife-themed board game designed by Flatout Games CoLab. In this game, players take on the role of Cascadian biologists tasked with restoring balance to the ecosystem in the Pacific Northwest region of North America.

**Objective:**
The objective of the game is to score points by placing tiles representing different habitats (mountains, wetlands, forests, and prairies) and matching them with corresponding wildlife species. The player with the highest total score at the end of the game wins.

**Gameplay Overview:**

1. Each player starts with a set of random habitat tiles and a hand of five Wildlife Scoring Cards.
2. On each turn, players draw a tile from their deck and can choose to place it in one of the four habitats (mountains, wetlands, forests, or prairies).
3. When placing a tile, players must match it with corresponding wildlife species on adjacent tiles.
4. Each wildlife species has its own Scoring Card, which indicates the points awarded for each habitat type and any special conditions (e.g., "adjacent to another hawk").
5. Players can also use Nature Tokens to move around the board, place tiles, or gain bonus points.
6. The game ends when all tiles have been placed and scored.

**Key Rules:**

* **Habitat Placement:** When placing a tile, players must match it with corresponding wildlife species on adjacent tiles. For example, a mountain tile can be matched with a Roosevelt Elk tile if the elk is in a habitat that allows for its placement (e.g., prairies).
* **Wildlife Scoring:** Players earn points by matching wildlife species with their corresponding habitats. The points awarded vary depending on the Scoring Card and any special conditions.
* **Nature Tokens:** Nature Tokens can be used to move around the board, place tiles, or gain bonus points. However, players must have at least two Nature Tokens to take a Nature Token Action.
* **Keystone Habitats:** Keystone habitats are designated as "Only" on their Scoring Cards, meaning that only one of each habitat type can be placed in that location.

**Game Modes:**
Cascadia offers three game modes:

1. **Normal Game:** Players compete to earn the highest total score by placing tiles and matching wildlife species.
2. **Scenarios:** Players complete specific objectives or challenges, such as "Score at least 80 points" or "Place all tiles in a single habitat type."
3. **Rule Restrictions:** Players follow modified rules, such as limiting the number of Nature Tokens that can be used or requiring players to use only one Scoring Card.

**Components:**

* Game board featuring the Pacific Northwest region
* Habitat tiles (mountains, wetlands, forests, and prairies)
* Wildlife species cards (Roosevelt Elk, Chinook Salmon, Red-Tailed Hawk, etc.)
* Nature Tokens
* Scoring Cards for each wildlife species
* Rulebook

**Number of Players:**
2-4 players can play Cascadia.

**Recommended Age:** 12+ years old.

I hope this explanation provides a clear and accurate overview of the rules of Cascadia. Happy gaming!
