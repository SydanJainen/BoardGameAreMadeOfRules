# Rule Explanation for Expert Audience

Cascadia is a tile-laying game designed for 2-5 players, where players aim to create a thriving ecosystem by placing tiles representing different habitats (mountains, wetlands, forests, prairies, and rivers) and wildlife species. The game's objective is to score points by creating diverse ecosystems, while also adhering to the rules of each habitat type.

**Setup**

The game board consists of 21 hexagonal tiles, arranged in a circular pattern. Each tile has a unique ability and point value associated with it. Players start with a hand of five Wildlife Scoring Cards, which determine how points are awarded for specific wildlife species.

**Gameplay**

On each player's turn, they draw a tile from the deck and place it on the board, adjacent to an existing habitat or river tile. The tile must be placed according to its ability, such as adding a new habitat type or connecting two habitats with a river. Players can also choose to place tiles in a way that blocks other players' potential placements.

**Habitat Types**

Each habitat type has its own rules and point values:

* Mountains: Provide points for each bear species present on the board.
* Wetlands: Clean up phosphorus from the water, allowing fish to spawn and providing points for salmon.
* Forests: Support carbon sequestration and provide points for certain bird species.
* Prairies: Support a variety of wildlife species, including foxes, and provide points for each pair of hawks present on the board.
* Rivers: Connect habitats and provide points for fish populations.

**Wildlife Species**

Players can place tiles representing different wildlife species, such as bears, salmon, birds, and foxes. The Wildlife Scoring Cards determine how points are awarded for specific species:

* Bears (Grizzly Bear): Provide points for each habitat type present on the board.
* Salmon: Spawn in wetlands and provide points for each fish present.
* Birds (Red-Tailed Hawk): Score points for each hawk present, with bonus points for connecting hawks to other birds.
* Foxes: Support a variety of habitats and provide points for each pair present.

**Nature Tokens**

Players can use Nature Tokens to perform various actions, such as adding new wildlife species or blocking opponents' placements. The game includes 16 different types of Nature Tokens, which are used strategically throughout the game.

**Endgame**

The game ends when all tiles have been placed or a designated number of rounds has passed (e.g., seven rounds). Players then score points based on their completed ecosystems, wildlife populations, and habitat connections.

**Scoring**

Points are awarded for:

* Completed habitats: Each tile provides a point value based on its ability.
* Wildlife species: Points are awarded for each species present on the board, with bonus points for specific combinations (e.g., hawks connecting to other birds).
* Nature Tokens: Players earn points for using certain tokens strategically.

**Achievements**

Players can earn Achievements by completing specific objectives, such as scoring a minimum number of points in multiple habitats or achieving a high diversity score. These achievements are tracked on the Achievement Hiking Map and provide additional bonuses and rewards.

Overall, Cascadia is a game that requires strategic planning, adaptability, and a deep understanding of ecosystem dynamics. Players must balance their individual goals with the need to create a thriving ecosystem, making it a challenging and engaging experience for expert players.
