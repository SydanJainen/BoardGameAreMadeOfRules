# Century: Spice Road - Rules Explanation

## Overview

Century: Spice Road is an engine-building card game for 2-5 players. Players take on the role of spice traders, acquiring merchant cards to build an efficient trading engine, converting spices (represented by cubes) up the value chain, and fulfilling point cards to score victory points. The game uses a simple action economy: one action per turn, chosen from four options.

## Components and Spice Hierarchy

Spices are represented by colored cubes with a fixed value hierarchy:

**Yellow (Turmeric) < Red (Saffron) < Green (Cardamom) < Brown (Cinnamon)**

This hierarchy matters for upgrade actions and end-game scoring.

## Setup

1. **Point cards**: Shuffle the deck (orange backs), deal 5 face-up in a market row with the deck to the right.
2. **Bonus tokens**: Place gold tokens (2 per player) above the 1st point card; silver tokens (2 per player) above the 2nd point card.
3. **Starting hands**: Each player receives two starting merchant cards: "Create 2" and "Upgrade 2."
4. **Merchant cards**: Shuffle the remaining merchant deck (purple backs), deal 6 face-up in a market row.
5. **Cube supply**: Organize cubes by color into the bowls.
6. **Caravan cards**: Deal one caravan card per player. The player with the first-player symbol goes first.
7. **Starting cubes**: 1st player: 3 yellow. 2nd/3rd: 4 yellow. 4th/5th: 3 yellow + 1 red.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform exactly **one** of four actions:

### 1. Play
Play a merchant card from your hand face-up in front of you, then resolve its effect:

- **Spice cards**: Gain the depicted cubes from the supply and place them on your caravan.
- **Upgrade cards**: Upgrade cubes on your caravan by one level each (yellow to red, red to green, green to brown). An "Upgrade 2" card grants 2 upgrade steps, which can be applied to one or two cubes. You may use fewer upgrades than allowed.
- **Trade cards**: Return the cubes shown above the arrow from your caravan to the supply, then take the cubes shown below the arrow. The trade may be executed **multiple times** in a single play, provided you have the required cubes each time.

### 2. Acquire
Take one face-up merchant card from the market row into your hand. To pay, place one cube (any color) from your caravan onto each merchant card to the **left** of the chosen card. The leftmost card is free.

When you pick up a card, any cubes on it are added to your caravan. After acquiring, slide remaining cards left and refill from the deck.

### 3. Rest
Take **all** previously played cards (face-up in front of you) back into your hand. This is your only means of recovering played cards.

### 4. Claim
If your caravan contains all the cubes depicted on a face-up point card, return those cubes to the supply and take the point card (place it face-down in front of you).

- If you claim the **1st or 2nd** point card in the row, also take one gold or silver token respectively from the pile above it.
- If the gold tokens run out, slide the silver pile to the 1st position.
- Slide remaining point cards left and refill from the deck.

## Caravan Limit

Your caravan holds a maximum of **10 cubes**. At the end of your turn, if you exceed this limit, discard cubes of your choice back to the supply until you have 10.

## Cube Supply

The cube supply is considered unlimited. If a color runs out, use substitute materials.

## Game End and Scoring

The endgame triggers when any player claims their **5th point card** (or **6th** in a 2-3 player game). Complete the current round so all players have equal turns.

**Final scoring:**
- Points printed on claimed point cards
- Gold tokens: **3 points** each
- Silver tokens: **1 point** each
- Non-yellow cubes remaining on caravan: **1 point** each

**Tiebreaker**: The player who took their turn later in the final round wins.

## Key Reminders

- You must perform exactly one action per turn; you cannot pass.
- Trade cards can be repeated multiple times in a single play.
- Upgrade steps from a single card can target the same or different cubes.
- Acquiring cards costs cubes placed on cards to the left, not returned to supply. Those cubes become available to whoever later acquires those cards.
- Yellow cubes on your caravan at game end are worth nothing.
