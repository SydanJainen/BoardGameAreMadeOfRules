# Ticket to Ride - Explanation for Adults

## Overview

Ticket to Ride is a route-building board game for 2 to 5 players, designed by Alan R. Moon. Players collect colored Train Car cards and use them to claim railway routes between cities on a map of North America. The game blends set collection with network building, and a typical session lasts 30 to 60 minutes. The goal is to accumulate the most points through claiming routes, completing secret Destination Tickets, and building the longest continuous path.

## Components

- 1 board depicting a map of North American cities connected by colored railway routes
- 240 plastic train pieces (45 per player in blue, red, green, yellow, and black)
- 110 Train Car cards: 12 each in 8 colors (matching the route colors on the board) plus 14 Locomotive wild cards
- 30 Destination Ticket cards, each showing two cities and a point value
- 1 Longest Continuous Path bonus card (worth 10 points)
- 5 wooden scoring markers
- 1 summary card

## Setup

1. Place the board centrally. Each player selects a color, takes the corresponding 45 trains and scoring marker, and places the marker on the Start position of the scoring track.
2. Shuffle the Train Car deck and deal 4 cards to each player. Place the remaining deck near the board and reveal 5 face-up cards beside it.
3. Place the Longest Continuous Path bonus card face-up near the board.
4. Shuffle the Destination Ticket deck and deal 3 to each player. Each player must keep at least 2 (may keep all 3). Return any unwanted tickets to the bottom of the deck. Destination Tickets are kept secret from opponents throughout the game.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player performs exactly one of three possible actions:

### 1. Draw Train Car Cards

Draw 2 cards, chosen from the 5 face-up cards or blind-drawn from the top of the deck, in any combination. When a face-up card is taken, it is immediately replaced from the deck.

**Locomotive exception:** If a player takes a face-up Locomotive (wild card), that counts as the entire turn -- no second card is drawn. Locomotives drawn blind from the deck carry no penalty and the player still draws a second card. Additionally, if the replacement card revealed after a first draw is a Locomotive, the player may not take it as the second card. If at any point 3 of the 5 face-up cards are Locomotives, all 5 are discarded and replaced with 5 new cards.

There is no hand size limit.

### 2. Claim a Route

To claim a route, play a set of Train Car cards matching the route's color and length, then place one plastic train on each space of that route. Locomotive cards may substitute for any color within a set. Gray routes may be claimed with a set of any single color.

Points are scored immediately based on route length:

| Route Length | Points |
|---|---|
| 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 7 |
| 5 | 10 |
| 6 | 15 |

Key rules for claiming:
- Only one route may be claimed per turn.
- There is no requirement to connect to previously claimed routes.
- A single player cannot claim both routes of a Double-Route (parallel routes between the same two cities).
- In 2- or 3-player games, once one player claims one route of a Double-Route, the other route is closed entirely.

### 3. Draw Destination Tickets

Draw 3 Destination Tickets from the top of the deck. Keep at least 1; return any unwanted cards to the bottom of the deck. Each ticket names two cities and a point value. If fewer than 3 tickets remain, draw only what is available.

## Game End

The end-game is triggered when any player finishes a turn with 0, 1, or 2 trains remaining. Every player, including the one who triggered the end, gets one final turn. Then scoring takes place.

## Final Scoring

1. **Route points** should already be recorded on the scoring track. It is recommended to verify by recounting.
2. **Destination Tickets:** Reveal all tickets. For each completed ticket (an unbroken path of the player's trains connects the two named cities), add the ticket's point value. For each incomplete ticket, subtract the point value.
3. **Longest Continuous Path:** The player with the longest unbroken chain of connected trains receives the 10-point bonus. The path may loop and pass through the same city multiple times, but each individual train piece can only be counted once. In case of a tie, all tied players receive the bonus.

The player with the highest total wins. Tiebreaker: most completed Destination Tickets. Second tiebreaker: holder of the Longest Continuous Path bonus.

## Strategic Considerations

- Longer routes offer disproportionately more points per card spent, making them efficient targets.
- Balancing Destination Ticket ambition against risk is central: failed tickets cost double their potential (loss instead of gain).
- Monitoring opponents' route claims can reveal their likely destinations and inform blocking decisions.
- Locomotive cards are powerful but drawing them from the face-up display costs an entire turn's draw action.
- The end-game trigger based on train supply means keeping track of all players' remaining trains is important for timing.
