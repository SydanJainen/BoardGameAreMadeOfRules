# Rule Explanation for Expert Audience

**Cascadia: A Game of Natural Beauty**

**Objective:** Create the most biodiverse landscape by attracting various wildlife to your habitats while managing overpopulation and habitat loss.

**Components:**
- Habitat Tiles (Mountains, Wetlands, Forests, Rivers, Prairies)
- Wildlife Tokens (Bears, Elk, Salmon, Hawks, Foxes)
- Nature Tokens
- Scoring Cards for each Wildlife type
- Starter Habitat Tile for each player

**Setup:**
1. Shuffle Habitat Tiles and place them face down in a draw pile.
2. Draw five pairs of Habitat-Token combos from the pile and place them in the display.
3. Each player takes a Starter Habitat Tile and places it in their personal play area.

**Gameplay:**

**A. Place a Habitat Token:** On your turn, take one Wildlife Token from any pair in the display. Then, place your chosen Wildlife token on a matching habitat tile of yours (if available), following these rules:
   - **Adjacency:** Wildlife tokens must be placed adjacent to another wildlife token already present.
   - **Habitat Matching:** Wildlife tokens may only be placed on habitats that match their preferred environment.

**B. Activate Nature Tokens:** When a habitat reaches its capacity (as indicated by the scoring cards), players must activate Nature Tokens:
   - Remove one Wildlife Token from the full habitat.
   - Gain one Nature Token for each remaining Wildlife Token in the habitat.

**C. Play Nature Token Actions:**
   1. **Take Action:** Pay one Nature Token to take an action from a list of three options (e.g., remove overpopulation, connect habitats).
   2. **Score Points:** Spend your Nature Tokens at any point to gain points on your scoring card(s).

**D. End the Game:**
When either player's scoring card is fully scored or there are no more Habitat-Token pairs in the display, the game ends immediately.

**Scoring:**
Players tally their scores based on Wildlife attracted and habitats created according to each Wildlife's scoring card. The player with the highest total score wins!

**Additional Rules:**
- **Overpopulation:** If a habitat cannot accommodate another wildlife token, it goes into 'overpopulation.' Players must activate Nature Tokens.
- **Keystone Habitats:** Some habitats provide bonus points or actions when certain wildlife are present.
- **Wildlife Interactions:** Certain combinations of wildlife attractors, while others repel them. Refer to the scoring cards for details.

**Advanced Rules:**
- Explore optional advanced rules such as rule restrictions, scenario challenges, and solo mode gameplay variants for added replayability.

**Art and Theme:**
Cascadia features stunning original art by Beth Sobel that captures the natural beauty of the Paciﬁc Northwest, emphasizing biodiversity and conservation efforts.
