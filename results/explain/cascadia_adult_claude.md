# Cascadia - Rules Explanation

## Overview

Cascadia is a tile-laying and token-drafting game for 1-4 players, designed by Randy Flynn. Players compete to build the most harmonious Pacific Northwest ecosystem by drafting hexagonal Habitat Tiles and Wildlife Tokens over exactly 20 turns. Points are scored through wildlife pattern-matching (governed by variable scoring cards) and by forming the largest contiguous habitat corridors. A typical game lasts 30-45 minutes.

## Components

- **85 Habitat Tiles** (including 25 Keystone Tiles) depicting five terrain types: Mountains, Forests, Prairies, Wetlands, and Rivers. Each tile has one or two terrain types and displays 1-3 wildlife icons indicating which animals may be placed there.
- **100 Wildlife Tokens** (20 each of Bear, Elk, Salmon, Hawk, Fox), stored in a cloth bag.
- **5 Starter Habitat Tiles** -- larger multi-hex tiles, one distributed to each player.
- **21 Wildlife Scoring Cards** (4 per animal species labeled A-D, plus 1 Family/Intermediate card).
- **25 Nature Tokens** (pinecone-shaped) -- serve as a flexible action currency and are worth 1 point each if unspent.
- **1 Scorepad.**

## Setup

1. Place all 100 Wildlife Tokens in the cloth bag and mix thoroughly.
2. Determine the Habitat Tile count by player count: 2 players = 43 tiles; 3 players = 63 tiles; 4 players = 83 tiles (formula: 20 per player + 3). Randomly select the required number without looking; return the rest to the box.
3. Shuffle the selected tiles and form face-down stacks accessible to all players.
4. Randomly select one Wildlife Scoring Card (A, B, C, or D) for each of the five wildlife species. Place these five cards in the center. For a first game, use the "A" cards. Return unused scoring cards to the box.
5. Randomly distribute one Starter Habitat Tile to each player, placed face-up. Return extras to the box.
6. Reveal 4 Habitat Tiles from the stacks, placing them face-up in the center.
7. Draw 4 Wildlife Tokens from the bag, pairing each (in order) with one of the 4 revealed Habitat Tiles to form 4 tile-token combinations.
8. Place Nature Tokens in a shared supply accessible to all.
9. The player who most recently saw one of the five wildlife species in real life goes first. Play proceeds clockwise.

## Turn Structure

Each turn consists of two phases:

### Phase 1: Select a Tile-Token Combination

Four combinations of one Habitat Tile and one Wildlife Token are available. Before selecting, check for overpopulation:

- **4 identical tokens:** Automatically wipe all 4. Return them to the bag, then draw 4 new tokens, pairing each with a tile in order. This can trigger repeatedly.
- **3 identical tokens:** The active player may optionally wipe those 3. Return them to the bag, draw 3 replacements. This may only be done once per turn.

After resolving overpopulation, select one combination. You must take a paired combination unless you spend a Nature Token for one of two special actions:

**Nature Token actions (optional, before selecting):**
1. Take any one of the four Habitat Tiles and any one of the four Wildlife Tokens (ignoring pairings).
2. Wipe any number of Wildlife Tokens and replace them from the bag.

Multiple Nature Tokens may be spent on a single turn. Spent tokens return to the shared supply.

### Phase 2: Place the Tile and Token

**Habitat Tile placement rules:**
- Must be placed adjacent to at least one existing tile in your environment (sharing a hex edge).
- Cannot be placed on top of another tile. Existing tiles cannot be moved.
- Terrain matching is not required for placement but affects end-game scoring.

**Wildlife Token placement rules:**
- Must be placed on a Habitat Tile that does not already have a Wildlife Token.
- The tile must display the matching wildlife icon (tiles show 1-3 possible wildlife).
- The token may be placed on the tile just drafted or any other eligible tile in your environment.
- If no legal placement exists or you choose not to place the token, return it to the bag.
- **Keystone Tile bonus:** Placing a Wildlife Token on a Keystone Tile (single-wildlife tile) earns you a Nature Token from the supply.

**Replenishment:** After placement, replace the taken Habitat Tile from the top of any face-down stack and draw a new Wildlife Token from the bag, filling the empty positions without rearranging existing combinations.

Play passes clockwise.

## End Game Trigger

The game ends immediately when a player takes a Habitat Tile and no face-down tiles remain to replace it. Each player will have played exactly 20 turns.

## Scoring

Points are tallied in four categories:

### 1. Wildlife Scoring Cards

Score each wildlife species according to the scoring card selected during setup. Each species has its own pattern requirements:

- **Bears:** Score for groups of specific sizes (depending on card). Bear groups cannot be adjacent to one another. Each group must contain the exact number shown.
- **Elk:** Score for groups in specific formations (lines, shapes, contiguous groups, or circles depending on card). Elk groups may be adjacent, but each elk scores only once.
- **Salmon:** Score for runs -- chains where each salmon is adjacent to no more than two others. Runs cannot have additional salmon adjacent. Points scale with run length.
- **Hawks:** Score for spatial distribution -- isolated hawks, lines of sight between hawks, or pairs with unique wildlife between them (depending on card).
- **Foxes:** Score based on adjacencies to other wildlife. Points increase with the number of unique adjacent animal types, pairs, or matching species (depending on card).

### 2. Habitat Tile Corridors

For each of the 5 habitat types, score 1 point per tile in your largest contiguous corridor (connected group of matching terrain). Tiles are contiguous if they share at least one matching terrain edge.

### 3. Habitat Corridor Majorities

Bonus points for having the largest corridor in each habitat type, varying by player count:

- **Solo:** 2-point bonus per habitat type with a corridor of 7+ tiles.
- **2 players:** 2 points for the largest corridor in each habitat. Ties award 1 point each. No second-place bonus.
- **3-4 players:** 3 points for largest, 1 point for second largest. Two-way tie for largest: 2 points each, no second-place bonus. Three or four-way tie for largest: 1 point each, no second-place bonus. Ties for second largest: 0 points each.

### 4. Nature Tokens

Each unspent Nature Token is worth 1 point.

## Determining the Winner

Sum all four scoring categories. The player with the highest total wins. In case of a tie, the player with more Nature Tokens wins. If still tied, those players share the victory.

## Variants

- **Family Variant:** Uses only the Family Variant Wildlife Scoring Card, which awards points for groups of the same wildlife by size (any shape). Habitat corridor majority scoring may be skipped.
- **Intermediate Variant:** Uses the Intermediate Variant Scoring Card with different point values per group size.
- **Solo Mode:** Uses 2-player tile count (43). After placing your tile and token, discard the combination furthest from the draw stacks, slide remaining pairs, then draw two new tiles and tokens. Score against a rating chart (60+ through 110+).
