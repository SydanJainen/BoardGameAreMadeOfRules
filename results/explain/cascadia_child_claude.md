# Cascadia - How to Play!

## What Is This Game About?

Cascadia is a game where you build your own nature world! You get to place land tiles (like mountains, forests, rivers, wetlands, and prairies) and put animals on them (bears, elk, salmon, hawks, and foxes). The player who builds the best nature scene wins!

It is for 1 to 4 players, and each game takes about 20 turns per person.

## What Is in the Box?

- **Habitat Tiles** -- these are hexagon-shaped pieces that show different types of land (mountains, forests, prairies, wetlands, rivers). Some tiles show two types of land at once.
- **Wildlife Tokens** -- round tokens with animals on them: Bear, Elk, Salmon, Hawk, and Fox. There are 20 of each.
- **Starter Habitat Tiles** -- special bigger tiles you begin the game with. Each player gets one.
- **Wildlife Scoring Cards** -- these cards tell you HOW to arrange your animals to get points. You pick one card per animal at the start.
- **Nature Tokens** -- little pinecone tokens. These are like special power-ups you can use during the game.
- **A Cloth Bag** -- you put all the animal tokens in here and draw them randomly.
- **A Scorepad** -- for writing down points at the end.

## How to Set Up

1. Put all the animal tokens in the cloth bag and shake it up.
2. Make a stack of land tiles (how many depends on how many players -- about 20 per player, plus 3 extra).
3. Pick one scoring card for each of the five animals and put them where everyone can see.
4. Each player gets one Starter Habitat Tile and puts it in front of them.
5. Flip over 4 land tiles from the stack and put them in the middle.
6. Pull 4 animal tokens from the bag and put one next to each land tile. Now you have 4 pairs to choose from!
7. Put the Nature Tokens (pinecones) where everyone can reach them.
8. Whoever saw a real bear, elk, salmon, hawk, or fox most recently goes first!

## How to Play Your Turn

Every turn, you do two simple things:

### Step 1: Pick a Pair

Look at the 4 pairs in the middle (each pair is one land tile + one animal token). Pick the pair you want!

**But wait -- check for "overpopulation" first!**
- If all 4 animal tokens are the same type, throw them all back in the bag and draw 4 new ones. This happens automatically.
- If 3 of the 4 are the same type, you CAN choose to throw those 3 back and draw 3 new ones. But you do not have to.

**Using a Nature Token (pinecone power-up):**
If you have a Nature Token, you can spend it to do something special:
- Pick ANY land tile and ANY animal token (they do not have to be a pair), OR
- Throw back any animal tokens you do not like and draw new ones.

### Step 2: Place Your Tile and Animal

- **Place the land tile** next to any tile you already have. It has to touch at least one side of a tile you already placed. You cannot stack tiles on top of each other.
- **Place the animal token** on any tile in your area that (a) does not already have an animal on it, and (b) shows that animal's picture. Each tile shows 1 to 3 animal types it can hold.
- If you cannot place the animal anywhere (or do not want to), put it back in the bag.
- If you put an animal on a special **Keystone Tile** (a tile with only one animal option), you get a free Nature Token!

After you are done, a new tile is flipped from the stack and a new token is drawn from the bag to replace the ones you took. Then the next player goes.

## When Does the Game End?

The game ends when there are no more land tiles left in the stack to replace one that was taken. Every player gets exactly 20 turns.

## How Do You Score Points?

You get points from four things:

1. **Animal Scoring Cards** -- Look at the five scoring cards you picked at the start. Each one tells you how that animal scores. For example, bears might score for being in pairs, salmon might score for being in a line, and so on.

2. **Biggest Land Groups** -- For each of the 5 land types, find your biggest connected group of that land. You get 1 point for each tile in that group. For example, if your biggest forest group has 6 tiles touching each other, that is 6 points!

3. **Bonus for Biggest Groups** -- The player who has the very biggest group of each land type gets bonus points (2 or 3 extra points depending on how many people are playing). If players tie, they split the bonus.

4. **Leftover Nature Tokens** -- Each pinecone you did not use is worth 1 extra point!

Add up all your points. The player with the most points wins! If there is a tie, the player with more Nature Tokens wins.

## Tips for Having Fun

- Try to make big connected areas of the same land type -- that is an easy way to get lots of points.
- Pay attention to the animal scoring cards. They tell you exactly how to arrange your animals for the best score.
- Do not forget about your Nature Tokens -- they are really helpful when you need a specific tile or animal!
- You do not have to match land types when placing tiles, but connecting the same types earns you more points.

Have fun building your nature world!
