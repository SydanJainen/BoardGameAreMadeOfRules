# Rule Explanation for Child Audience

Here's an explanation of the game Cascadia that's easy to understand for kids:

**What is Cascadia?**
Cascadia is a fun board game where you get to build a beautiful landscape with different types of habitats like mountains, wetlands, forests, and rivers. You also get to collect and protect different animals that live in these habitats.

**Objective:**
Your goal is to create the most beautiful and diverse landscape by placing different tiles and collecting points for each animal you protect.

**How to Play:**

1. First, we shuffle the tiles and draw a certain number of them to start our game.
2. Then, we take turns placing these tiles on the board to build our landscape. Each tile has a special type of habitat or an animal that lives there.
3. As we place each tile, we have to think about what animals will be happy living in that habitat. Some animals need specific types of habitats to survive, so we have to make sure we have those tiles available.
4. If we draw an animal card, we get to put one of its friends from our collection into the corresponding habitat on the board. We earn points for each animal we collect and protect!
5. The game ends when all the tiles are placed or a certain number of rounds have passed. Then, we count up all our points to see who has the most beautiful and diverse landscape!

**Important Rules:**

* We can't put two tiles with different habitats next to each other.
* Some animals need special conditions to survive, like having a specific type of tile in their habitat. If they don't get that, they might disappear!
* Nature Tokens are special cards that let us do fun things on the board, but we have to use them carefully.

**Why is it Fun?**
Cascadia is all about creativity and problem-solving. We have to think ahead and plan our moves carefully to create a beautiful landscape with happy animals. It's like building with blocks, but instead of blocks, we're using tiles and cards!

So, are you ready to build your own Cascadian landscape and become a wildlife expert? Let's get started!
