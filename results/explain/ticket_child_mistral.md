# Rule Explanation for Child Audience

**Welcome to Ticket to Ride!**

Hello there, young explorer! Let's learn how to play Ticket to Ride together. It's a fun game where you'll connect cities with trains, just like Phileas Fogg did in his adventures!

**What do you need?**
- A big board map of North America.
- Colored train cars (blue, red, green, yellow, black) and some extra ones.
- Wooden scoring markers for each player.
- Illustrated cards: Train Car cards and Destination Ticket cards.

**Setting up the game:**
1. Put the board map in the middle of the table.
2. Each player takes a set of colored train cars and matching wooden marker.
3. Place your marker on "Start" on the Scoring Track along the map's border.
4. Shuffle Train Car cards, deal 4 to each player, and place the rest near the board with 5 face-up cards.
5. Shuffle Destination Ticket cards, deal 3 to each player, and keep at least two. Place returns on the bottom of the deck.

**How to play:**
Your turn has three choices:

1. **Draw Train Car Cards:** You can take one face-up card or draw from the top of the deck (blind). Then take another card from either place.
   - *Locomotive cards* are special, they can be used as any color and only one can be drawn in a turn.

2. **Claim a Route:** Use your Train Car cards to connect two cities on the map. You need the same number of cards as spaces between those cities. Place your colored trains on that route and move your scoring marker.
   - *Gray routes* can use any color card set, but you'll need specific colors for other routes (like Blue Passenger cars for blue routes).
   - Some cities have two routes! Only one player can claim both in a 2 or 3-player game.

3. **Draw Destination Tickets:** Draw 3 cards from the top of the deck and keep at least one. These secret tickets show two cities you need to connect with trains.

**Winning the game:**
- The first player to run out of train cars gets everyone one final turn, then it's time to calculate scores!
- You score points for claiming routes, completing Destination Tickets (if you connected those cities), and having the longest continuous path of routes.
- The player with the most points wins! If there's a tie, the one who completed the most tickets or has the longest path wins.

**Let's go on an adventure together!**
You'll love traveling across North America, connecting cities, and collecting points as you play Ticket to Ride. So grab your train cars, and let's embark on this exciting journey! Good luck!
