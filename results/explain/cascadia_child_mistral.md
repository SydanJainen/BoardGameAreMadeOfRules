# Rule Explanation for Child Audience

**Welcome to Cascadia, the board game about exploring and protecting our beautiful region's habitats and wildlife! Here are the simple steps to play:**

1. **Gather Your Team**: You'll need at least 2 players (or you can play solo) and some tokens - we have bears, elk, salmon, hawks, foxes, mountains, wetlands, forests, rivers, and prairies.

2. **Set Up The Board**: Each player starts with two habitat tiles and one wildlife token. The rest of the tiles go face down in a pile, called the "draw stack".

3. **Take Turns**: On your turn, you'll do three things:
   - **Draw a Tile**: Take one tile from the draw stack and add it to your environment.
   - **Place a Wildlife Token**: Choose one of your wildlife tokens (except for bears) and place it on any habitat tile in your environment. You can't place bears next to other bears though!
   - **Take a Nature Action** (optional): If you have nature tokens, you can use them to do special things like move tiles around or swap wildlife tokens.

4. **Score Points**: At the end of each round (when all players have taken their turns), it's time to score points! Each player scores based on how many and which types of habitats and wildlife they have in their environment. There are also bonus points for completing certain challenges, like having a big habitat corridor or lots of a single type of wildlife.

5. **Repeat Until The End**: Keep taking turns and scoring points until all the tiles from the draw stack have been taken (that's when you'll hear "the call of the wild" and it's time to end the game). The player with the most points at the end is the winner!

**But remember, kids, we're playing this game because we care about our environment. So let's learn about Cascadia while having fun!**

*For the solo mode or special scenarios, follow the extra instructions given!*
