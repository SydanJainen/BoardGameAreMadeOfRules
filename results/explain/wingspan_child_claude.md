# How to Play Wingspan

## What Is This Game About?

Wingspan is a fun game where you collect beautiful birds! You are a bird lover trying to build the best bird sanctuary. You will find cool birds, feed them, and help them lay eggs. The player who collects the most points from their birds wins!

You can play with 1 to 5 players, and the game lasts 4 rounds.

## What You Get

- A personal player mat with three nature areas (forest, grassland, wetland)
- 8 little colored cubes (these are your action markers)
- Bird cards with pictures of real birds
- Tiny colorful egg pieces
- Food tokens (worms, seeds, fish, berries, and mice)
- A cool dice tower shaped like a birdfeeder

## Setting Up

1. Each player gets a player mat and 8 cubes of one color.
2. You get 5 random bird cards and 5 food tokens (one of each kind).
3. You also get 2 bonus cards (pick your favorite one to keep).
4. Now here is the tricky part: you can keep up to 5 bird cards, but for every bird you keep, you have to give back one food token. So if you keep 3 birds, you give back 3 food and keep 2 food.

## How to Play

On your turn, you do ONE of these four things:

### 1. Play a Bird
Pick a bird from your hand and put it on your player mat. You have to pay for it using food tokens. Some spots also cost eggs. Birds go in one of three rows:
- **Forest** birds (top row)
- **Grassland** birds (middle row)
- **Wetland** birds (bottom row)

### 2. Get Food
Take dice from the birdfeeder tower. Each die shows a type of food. Pick the food you want! You need food to play birds later. After you grab food, you can also use the special powers of birds you already have in your forest.

### 3. Lay Eggs
Put egg tokens on your bird cards. Each bird can only hold so many eggs (the card tells you). Eggs are worth points at the end, and you also need them to play birds in certain spots. After laying eggs, your grassland birds can use their powers.

### 4. Draw Bird Cards
Pick new bird cards from the face-up cards on the table or from the top of the deck. More birds means more choices for later! After drawing, your wetland birds can use their powers.

## Bird Powers

Some birds have special abilities:
- **When Played** powers happen right when you put the bird down.
- **Brown powers** work every time you use that row.
- **Pink powers** can happen on other players' turns too.

## Rounds and Scoring

The game has 4 rounds. Here is how many turns you get:
- Round 1: 8 turns
- Round 2: 7 turns
- Round 3: 6 turns
- Round 4: 5 turns

You lose one turn each round because one of your cubes goes on the scoreboard to track a special goal.

## How to Win

At the end of 4 rounds, add up your points:
- Points printed on your bird cards
- Points from your bonus card
- Points from round goals
- 1 point for each egg on your birds
- 1 point for each food saved on your birds
- 1 point for each card tucked under your birds

The player with the most points wins! If there is a tie, whoever has the most leftover food tokens wins.

## Tips for New Players

- At the start, focus on getting food and playing birds. You need birds on your mat before the other actions become really powerful.
- Look for birds with brown powers because they help you every time you use that row.
- Do not worry about memorizing everything right away. The game gets easier each time you play!
