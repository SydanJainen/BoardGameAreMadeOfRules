# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

Welcome to Pandemic, a cooperative board game designed by Matt Leacock. In this game, players work together as a team of disease-fighting specialists to save the world from four deadly diseases. The goal is to find cures for these diseases before they spread and take over the globe.

**Number of Players:** 2-4

**Objective:**

* Each player has a unique role with different abilities and strengths.
* Work together as a team to find cures for all four diseases (Blue, Yellow, Black, and Red).
* Develop resources and travel across the globe to stem the tide of infection.

**Game Components:**

* Game board featuring a map of the world
* 48 Infection cards, which represent outbreaks and epidemics
* 24 Player cards, each representing a different role with unique abilities (White, Green, Blue, Red, Yellow)
* 4 Reference cards, used to track player hand limits and other game information
* 5 Role cards, which provide additional abilities and strengths for each player

**Gameplay:**

1. **Setup:** Each player chooses a role card and places their pawn on the corresponding starting location on the board.
2. **Turn Structure:** A turn consists of three phases:
	* **Action Phase:** Players take individual turns performing one of four actions:
		+ Treat Disease (remove an outbreak from a city)
		+ Move (travel to a new city or research station)
		+ Share Knowledge (exchange information with other players)
		+ Discover Cure (find a cure for a disease)
	* **Draw Phase:** Players draw two cards from the Infection Draw Pile, which represents the current infection rate.
	* **Resolution Phase:** Resolve any ongoing outbreaks and epidemics, taking into account the new infections drawn during the draw phase.
3. **Infection Rate:** The Infection Rate is determined by the number of Infection cards in the Infection Draw Pile. As players draw more Infection cards, the infection rate increases, making it harder to find cures.
4. **Outbreaks and Epidemics:** When a city reaches three cubes of the same color (e.g., three Black cubes), an outbreak occurs, and additional outbreaks are triggered in adjacent cities. Outbreaks can be devastating if not managed properly.
5. **Finding Cures:** Players must work together to find cures for each disease. The number of cards required to find a cure varies depending on the player's role:
	* White (Scientist): 4 Black cards
	* Green (Operations Expert): 5 Yellow cards
	* Blue (Medic): 3 Blue cards
	* Red (Researcher): 2 Red cards
6. **Game End:** The game ends when all four diseases are cured, or if the infection rate becomes too high and outbreaks occur eight times.

**Unique Features:**

* Players must work together to overcome challenges, making it a true cooperative experience.
* Each role has unique abilities and strengths, requiring players to communicate effectively and coordinate their efforts.
* The game features a modular board, allowing for different maps and scenarios to be created.

By working together and utilizing each player's strengths, you can save the world from these deadly diseases. Will you rise to the challenge?
