# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

Welcome to Century, a strategic board game where players navigate the spice trade, collecting gold, silver tokens, and valuable spices while building their merchant cards hand. Let's break down the game rules into clear and concise steps.

**Setup**

1. Shuffle the Point cards (orange back) to form deck A.
2. Draw 5 cards and place them face up in a row to the left of deck B.
3. Place two piles: gold tokens equal to the number of players x2 above point card C, and silver tokens equal to the number of players x2 above point card D.
4. Among the Merchant cards (purple back), each player gets one 'Create 2' and one 'Upgrade 2' card as part of their starting hand.
5. Shuffle the remaining Merchant cards to form deck E and draw 6 cards face up in a row to the left of deck F.
6. Separate spices into bowls, ensuring the order matches the given color sequence: Yellow (Turmeric), Red (Saffron), Green (Cardamom), and Brown (Cinnamon).
7. Deal one Caravan card to each player, shuffling them and placing the first player's card in front of them.
8. Assign yellow and red cubes according to the table, adding 3 yellow cubes for the 1st player, 4 yellow cubes for the 2nd-3rd players, and 3 yellow cubes with 1 red cube for the 4th-5th players.

**Gameplay**

A game of Century consists of rounds, with each player taking one turn per round. On a player's turn:

1. **Play**: Play a card from your hand by placing it face up in front of you and executing its effect.
2. **Acquire**: Acquire a Merchant card by paying the required cubes from your caravan. If acquiring the leftmost Merchant card, no cubes are placed.
3. **Rest**: Take all played cards back to your hand, allowing players to build their merchant cards hand through deck-building.
4. **Claim**: Claim a Point card by having all its cubes in your caravan; return the cubes to the bowls and take the Point card.

**Merchant Card Effects**

* **Spice Cards**: Take the number and color of cube(s) shown on the card from the bowls.
* **Upgrade 2 Cards**: Upgrade one cube level, then upgrade another cube level (optional).
* **Trade Cards**: Return cubes to the bowls and add new ones from the supply to your caravan.

**Caravan Limit**

At the end of a player's turn, if their caravan exceeds its limit (10 cubes), they must return excess cubes to the bowls.

**Game End and Winning Conditions**

A game ends when a player takes their 5th Point card (or 6th in a 2-3 player game). Players count points from:

* **Point cards**: Each gold token is worth 3 points, each silver token is worth 1 point, and each non-yellow cube is worth 1 point.
* **Gold and silver tokens**: The player with the most points wins. In case of a tie, the last player to take a turn wins.

**Additional Rules**

* Cube supply is not limited; use substitute materials if necessary (e.g., players run out of yellow cubes).
* If a supply is exhausted, players can continue using substitute materials.
* The game designer and credits are listed at the end of the rulebook.

By following these rules, you'll navigate the spice trade, collect valuable resources, and build your merchant cards hand to become the ultimate caravan leader.
