# Rule Explanation for Expert Audience

Wingspan, designed by Elizabeth Hargrave, is a highly strategic bird-collecting game for 1-5 players that combines engine-building, resource management, and area control. The game's objective is to attract birds to your aviary, earning points through various means.

**Setup**

Each player receives a set of components, including:

* 1 player mat
* 8 action cubes of one color
* 2 random bonus cards
* 5 random bird cards
* 5 food tokens (one of each type)
* 1 scorepad

Players keep their hand of cards private or public throughout the game. Each player chooses to keep up to 5 bird cards and discards the rest. For each bird card kept, they must discard a corresponding number of food tokens.

**Turn Structure**

On each turn, players take one of four actions:

1. **Play a Bird**: Select a bird card in hand to play and place an action cube at the top of the column where you'll play the bird. Pay the corresponding egg cost (if any) by discarding eggs from any birds on your player mat.
2. **Gain Food and Activate Forest Bird Powers**: Place an action cube in the leftmost exposed slot in the "gain food" row and gain the amount of food shown on the dice you select from the birdfeeder. Remove a die from the birdfeeder and place it on the table, gaining a food token matching the icon on the die.
3. **Lay Eggs and Activate Grassland Bird Powers**: Place an action cube in the leftmost exposed slot in the "lay eggs" row and lay the number of eggs. Gain an egg token from the supply and place it on a bird card that has space for it, according to its egg limit.
4. **Draw Bird Cards and Activate Wetland Bird Powers**: Place an action cube in the leftmost exposed slot in the "draw cards" row and draw the number of cards there from either the face-up cards on the bird tray or the bird deck.

**Bird Powers**

Bird powers are categorized into three types:

* **WHEN ACTIVATED (brown)**: These powers can be activated from right to left whenever you use the corresponding habitat.
* **ONCE BETWEEN TURNS (pink)**: These powers can only be triggered on opponents' turns. Players must inform their opponents what power is being used and what activates it.
* **CACHE**: This refers to putting a food token on a bird, which is worth 1 VP at end of game.

**Bird Feeder Management**

The birdfeeder has a tray to hold the 5 food dice. When a player gains food, they remove a die from the birdfeeder and place it on the table. If all dice show the same face and the player is about to gain food for any reason, they can throw all 5 dice back into the birdfeeder.

**Egg Management**

There is no limit to the egg supply. In the unlikely event that no eggs remain in the supply, players must use a temporary substitute.

**End-of-Round Goals**

The game consists of four rounds. At the end of each round:

1. Remove all action cubes from your player mat.
2. Score the end-of-round goal for the round you just completed.
3. Discard all face-up bird cards on the bird tray and replenish them with 3 new bird cards.
4. Rotate the first player token clockwise to the next player.

**Scoring**

The game ends at the conclusion of Round 4. Players score points by adding together:

* Points for each bird card (printed on the cards)
* Points for each bonus card (printed on the cards)
* Points for end-of-round goals
* 1 point for each:
 + egg on a bird card
 + food token cached on a bird card
 + card tucked under a bird card

In case of a tie, players who tied are awarded the next highest place value.

**Most Valuable Player**

The player with the most points wins. In the event of a tie, the player with the most unused food tokens wins. If there is still a tie, the victory is shared among the tied players.

This explanation provides a comprehensive overview of the game's rules, mechanics, and strategic considerations. Players must carefully manage their resources, activate bird powers strategically, and adapt to changing circumstances to emerge victorious in Wingspan.
