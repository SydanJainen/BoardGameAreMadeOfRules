# Rule Explanation for Adult Audience

### Ticket to Ride - A Cross-Country Train Adventure Overview

**Game Setup:**
- **Number of Players:** 2-5 players.
- **Age Range:** Ages 8 and above, though it is a popular game among adults.
- **Duration:** Approximately 30-60 minutes.

**Components:**
- Board map of North American train routes
- Colored Train Cars (45 each in Blue, Red, Green, Yellow, and Black)
- Illustrated cards:
  - Train Car cards (8 types including Locomotives)
  - Destination Ticket cards
  - Longest Path Bonus card
- Wooden Scoring Markers (1 for each player in different colors)
- Rules booklet
- Days of Wonder Online access number

**Objective:**
The primary goal is to score the highest points by connecting cities with train routes and successfully completing destination tickets.

### Game Setup Steps:
1. **Place the Board:** Center it on the table.
2. **Distribute Train Cars & Markers:** Each player gets 45 colored train cars and a matching scoring marker, placing them at the "Start" position on the Scoring Track.
3. **Deal Cards:**
   - Shuffle the Train Car cards and deal each player 4 cards initially.
   - Turn five face-up cards from the deck to start.
   - Place the Longest Path Bonus card face up next to the board.
   - Shuffle and deal three Destination Ticket cards to each player, keeping them secret.

### Gameplay:
- **Turn Order:** The most experienced traveler starts; play proceeds clockwise.
- **Actions per Turn (One of the Following):**
  1. **Draw Train Car Cards:**
     - Draw up to two cards from the face-up or deck.
     - If drawing a face-up card, immediately replace it with a new one.
  2. **Claim a Route:**
     - Use matching colored train cars to claim routes between cities (see "Route Scoring Table" for point values).
     - Place your trains on these spaces and move your scoring marker accordingly.
  3. **Draw Destination Tickets:**
     - Draw up to three new cards from the deck, keeping at least one.

### Train Car Cards:
- There are eight types of regular Train Car cards (Purple, Blue, Orange, White, Green, Yellow, Black, Red) and Locomotives.
- Locomotives act as wildcards but can only be used in a single set per turn if drawn from the face-up cards.

### Claiming Routes:
- Each route requires a specific number of train cars to claim it (see "Route Scoring Table").
- You cannot connect more than two adjacent cities with one move.
- Double-routes are exclusive; only one player can use them in a 2 or 3-player game.

### Route Scoring Table:
- Points are awarded based on the length of the route claimed.

### Drawing Destination Tickets:
- At any point, you may draw up to three new destination tickets and keep at least one.
- Keep your destination tickets secret until the end of the game.

### Game End & Scoring:
1. **End Condition:** When a player has 0, 1, or 2 train cars left after their turn.
2. **Final Turn:** All players get one last turn before scoring.
3. **Scoring:**
   - Add points for completed routes and destination tickets.
   - Subtract points if you fail to connect the cities on any destination ticket.
   - The player with the longest continuous path receives a 10-point bonus.

### Winning:
- The player with the highest total score wins.
- In case of ties, the player who has completed the most destination tickets wins. If still tied, the player with the Longest Continuous Path card wins.

This setup ensures an engaging and competitive experience where strategic planning and luck play key roles in your journey to victory!
