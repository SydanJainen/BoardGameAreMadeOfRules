# Ticket to Ride - Explanation for Children

## What Is This Game?

Ticket to Ride is a fun train game where you and your friends race to build train routes across a big map of North America! You collect colorful cards and use them to place your little train pieces on the board. The person who earns the most points wins!

## What's in the Box?

- A big board with a map showing cities and train routes connecting them
- Plastic train pieces in 5 colors (blue, red, green, yellow, and black) -- each player picks one color and gets 45 tiny trains
- Train Car cards in 8 different colors, plus special rainbow Locomotive cards
- Destination Ticket cards that show two cities you need to connect
- Scoring markers to keep track of your points on the board

## How Do You Set Up?

1. Put the map board in the middle of the table.
2. Pick your favorite color and take all the train pieces and the matching scoring marker for that color.
3. Put your scoring marker on the "Start" space on the score track that goes around the edge of the board.
4. Get 4 Train Car cards dealt to you -- this is your starting hand.
5. Flip 5 Train Car cards face-up next to the deck so everyone can see them.
6. Get 3 Destination Ticket cards. Look at them secretly! You must keep at least 2, but you can keep all 3 if you want. Put any you don't want back on the bottom of the pile.

## What Do You Do on Your Turn?

On your turn, you pick ONE of these three things to do:

### 1. Pick Up Train Car Cards
Take 2 cards. You can grab from the 5 face-up cards or draw from the top of the deck (a surprise draw!). When you take a face-up card, a new one flips up to replace it.

**Special rule about Locomotive cards:** Locomotive cards are wild -- they can be any color! But if you take a face-up Locomotive, that is the only card you get this turn (just 1 instead of 2). If you draw a Locomotive from the deck by surprise, you still get to draw your second card.

### 2. Claim a Route
Look at the routes on the board -- they are the colored spaces between two cities. To claim one, play cards from your hand that match the color and number of spaces on that route. Then place your little trains on those spaces!

- A blue route with 3 spaces? Play 3 blue cards.
- A gray route? Use any single color you want!
- Locomotives can fill in for any color you need.

You score points right away when you claim a route! Longer routes are worth more points:
- 1 space = 1 point
- 2 spaces = 2 points
- 3 spaces = 4 points
- 4 spaces = 7 points
- 5 spaces = 10 points
- 6 spaces = 15 points

### 3. Draw Destination Tickets
Take 3 new Destination Ticket cards. Keep at least 1 (you can keep 2 or all 3). These give you a secret mission -- connect the two cities shown on the card!

## How Does the Game End?

When any player has only 0, 1, or 2 train pieces left, everyone (including that player) gets one last turn. Then the game is over!

## Who Wins?

Now add up all the points:
- Points you already scored from claiming routes (already on the score track).
- **Add** points for each Destination Ticket where you successfully connected both cities with a path of your trains.
- **Subtract** points for each Destination Ticket where you did NOT connect the cities -- so be careful which tickets you keep!
- The player with the longest connected chain of trains gets a 10-point bonus!

The player with the most points wins! If there is a tie, the player who completed more Destination Tickets wins.

## Tips for New Players

- Start by looking at your Destination Tickets and finding the cities on the map.
- Try to collect cards of the colors you need for the routes between those cities.
- Longer routes give you a lot more points, so try to claim them when you can.
- Keep an eye on what other players are doing -- if they take a route you need, you might have to find another way!
- Don't grab too many Destination Tickets unless you are sure you can finish them, because unfinished tickets lose you points.
