# Rule Explanation for Expert Audience

### Detailed Analysis of Wingspan Rules

**Game Overview**
Wingspan is a competitive bird-collection, engine-building board game for 1-5 players, designed by Elizabeth Hargrave. The objective is to accumulate points from birds, bonus cards, end-of-round goals, eggs, cached food, and tucked birds.

### Setup
1. **Bird Cards**: Shuffle the deck and place it next to the bird tray; populate the tray with 3 face-up bird cards.
2. **Supply**: Place all food and egg tokens in a supply accessible to all players.
3. **Birdfeeder**: Toss custom dice into the birdfeeder dice tower.
4. **Goal Board**: Choose between Green (more direct competition) or Blue (less direct competition).
5. **Goal Tiles**: Shuffle, place 1 random side up on each of four spaces, and return extras to the box.
6. **Bonus Cards**: Shuffle and place them on the table.

7. **Player Mats**:
   - Each player receives a mat with 8 action cubes in one color, 2 bonus cards, 5 bird cards, and 5 food tokens (1 of each type).
   - Players keep up to 5 bird cards by discarding others proportionately.
   - Choose 1 bonus card to keep.

8. **First Player Token**: Randomly select the first player and give them the token.

### Turn Structure
Each turn consists of one action from four options:
1. **Play a Bird**
2. **Gain Food and Activate Forest Bird Powers**
3. **Lay Eggs and Activate Grassland Bird Powers**
4. **Draw Bird Cards and Activate Wetland Bird Powers**

#### Action 1: Play a Bird
- Place an action cube at the top of the column in which you will play.
- Pay egg cost (if any) by discarding eggs from your player mat.
- Pay food cost by discarding tokens next to your mat. 
- Place the bird card on the leftmost exposed slot and move the action cube to the "Play a Bird" row.
- Use "WHEN PLAYED" power if applicable.

#### Action 2: Gain Food
- Place an action cube in the "gain food" row.
- Select dice from the birdfeeder, gain corresponding tokens.
- Optionally discard 1 bird card for additional food.
- Activate any brown powers on forest birds.

#### Action 3: Lay Eggs
- Place an action cube in the "lay eggs" row.
- Lay the number of eggs required by discarding tokens to match the egg limit.
- Optionally pay 1 food token for extra eggs.
- Activate any brown powers on grassland birds.

#### Action 4: Draw Bird Cards
- Place an action cube in the "draw cards" row.
- Draw from face-up cards or deck, optionally discard eggs for additional draws.
- Activate any brown powers on wetland birds.

### Managing Components
- **Birdfeeder**: Dice are removed and placed outside when gaining food. If all dice show one type, throw them back into the feeder.
- **Food Tokens**: No limit; use temporary substitutes if tokens run out.
- **Egg Tokens**: Unlimited supply; use temporary substitutes as needed.

### Habitat Management
Each player mat has 5 slots per habitat (forest, grassland, wetland). Birds can be played in any available slot. The "WHEN PLAYED" power of a bird can cache food for later or create flocks by tucking other birds under it.

### End-of-Round Goals
- **Green Method**: Award points based on the majority count of items (1st, 2nd, and 3rd place).
- **Blue Method**: Award 1 point per item up to a maximum of 5.
- Players must always mark their score even if they scored zero.

### Scoring End-of-Round Goals
- Use action cubes to indicate scores on the goal board.
- For tied places, divide points among players who tied and round down (4th place gets 0 points).

### Game End
1. **Round 1: 8 turns per player**
2. **Round 2: 7 turns per player**
3. **Round 3: 6 turns per player**
4. **Round 4: 5 turns per player**

At the end of Round 4, total points from birds, bonus cards, eggs, cached food, and tucked birds. The player with the most points wins; in a tie, the player with the most unused food tokens wins.

### Strategic Considerations
1. **Early Game Focus**: Prioritize playing birds to get early access to habitats.
2. **Resource Management**: Balance bird play, egg laying, and card drawing based on available actions.
3. **Power Utilization**: Use "WHEN ACTIVATED" powers strategically for food gain or other benefits.
4. **Goal Strategy**: Plan your moves to maximize points from end-of-round goals.

### Edge Cases
1. **Empty Birdfeeder**: Re-roll all dice when gaining food if the tray is empty.
2. **No Eggs/food Tokens**: Use temporary substitutes as needed during gameplay.
3. **Tied Goals**: Follow specific rules for splitting points in tied scenarios.

By carefully managing resources and leveraging bird powers, players can build a strong aviary to dominate the game.
