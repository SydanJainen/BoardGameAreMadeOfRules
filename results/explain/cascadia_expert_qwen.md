# Rule Explanation for Expert Audience

Cascadia™ is a strategic tile-laying and token-drafting board game that challenges players to create diverse and harmonious ecosystems in the Pacific Northwest. The game involves complex interactions between habitat placement, wildlife management, and scoring mechanisms, making it an engaging challenge for experienced board gamers.

### Setup

1. **Habitat Tiles**: Place 43 Habitat Tiles (for 2-3 players) or 63 (for 4 players) in a stack face down. Shuffle the Wildlife Tokens (20 of each type: Bear, Elk, Salmon, Hawk, Fox).
2. **Scoring Cards**: Randomly select one card for each wildlife species and place them centrally.
3. **Starter Tiles**: Each player receives one Starter Habitat Tile placed face-up in front of them. Remaining tiles are returned to the box.
4. **Initial Display**: Reveal 4 Habitat Tiles from the stack, pair them with Wildlife Tokens drawn from the bag (20 per game), and place these combinations on the table.

### Gameplay

1. **Turn Order**: Players take turns clockwise. Each turn involves drafting a Habitat Tile and Wildlife Token combination.
2. **Tile Placement**:
   - The Habitat Tile must be placed adjacent to an existing tile or Starter Tile.
   - No habitat can overlap; they must touch at least one side.
3. **Token Placement**:
   - Place the Wildlife Token on a Habitat Tile if it matches (1-3 options per tile).
   - You may spend Nature Tokens to take any tile/token pair, or wipe overpopulated tokens and replace them.
4. **End of Turn**: Replace used tiles/cards with new ones from the stack/bag.

### End Game

The game ends when no Habitat Tiles remain for replacement (after 20 turns per player). Scoring then occurs based on:
1. **Wildlife Scoring Cards**:
   - Points are awarded for creating patterns like pairs, runs, and lines of sight.
2. **Habitat Corridors**:
   - Score points for the largest contiguous groups in each habitat type (Mountains, Forests, Prairies, Wetlands, Rivers).
3. **Nature Tokens**: Each unused Nature Token is worth 1 point.

### Strategic Considerations

- **Balancing Habitats and Wildlife**: Carefully choose which Habitat Tiles to place next to others to maximize wildlife placements while considering future scoring opportunities.
- **Overpopulation Handling**: Be prepared to wipe tokens if necessary, as this can open up new placement options or reduce competition for valuable tiles/tokens.
- **Nature Tokens**: Use these strategically; they allow you to take any tile/token pair or wipe overpopulated tokens. They are especially useful early in the game when fewer scoring opportunities are available.

### Edge Cases and Rule Interactions

- **Overpopulation Resolution**: If 3 of the same Wildlife Tokens appear, you can choose to wipe them and replace with new ones. This is a critical moment for strategic token management.
- **Wildlife Scoring Variants**:
   - **Family Variant**: Focus on creating groups of bears, runs of salmon, etc., with specific scoring rules.
   - **Intermediate Variant**: Similar to Family but uses different wildlife scoring cards.

### Conclusion

Cascadia™ requires a deep understanding of habitat and wildlife interactions to create the most advantageous ecosystem. The game's complexity lies in balancing immediate placement decisions with long-term strategic goals related to habitat corridors, wildlife distribution, and token management. Mastering these elements will significantly enhance your gameplay experience.
