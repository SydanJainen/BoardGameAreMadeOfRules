# How to Play Wingspan

## Overview

Wingspan is a competitive engine-building board game for 1-5 players designed by Elizabeth Hargrave. Players take on the role of bird enthusiasts seeking to attract the best birds to their wildlife preserves. Over 4 rounds, you play bird cards into three distinct habitats on your player mat, each of which strengthens a different action. The player who accumulates the most points from birds, bonus cards, end-of-round goals, eggs, cached food, and tucked cards wins the game.

## Setup

**Shared components:** Shuffle the 170 bird cards and place 3 face-up on the bird tray. Place food tokens and eggs in a shared supply. Roll all 5 food dice into the birdfeeder tower. Place the goal board (green side for competitive scoring, blue side for individual scoring) and randomly assign one double-sided goal tile to each of the 4 round slots. Shuffle the bonus card deck.

**Per player:** Each player receives a player mat, 8 action cubes, 5 random bird cards, 5 food tokens (one of each type), and 2 random bonus cards. Players must then make a critical opening decision: you may keep up to 5 bird cards, but you must discard 1 food token for each bird you keep. Then choose 1 of your 2 bonus cards and discard the other. A first player is randomly selected.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, you perform exactly one of four possible actions:

### 1. Play a Bird

Select a bird from your hand and place it into the leftmost open slot of its matching habitat row. Pay the bird's food cost (shown top-left of the card) by discarding food tokens. If placing a bird in column 2-3, also discard 1 egg; columns 4-5 cost 2 eggs. If the bird has a "When Played" power, resolve it immediately. Note: you may spend any 2 food tokens as a wild to substitute for one food of any type.

### 2. Gain Food (Forest Row)

Place an action cube on the leftmost exposed slot in the forest row. Gain the indicated number of food tokens by selecting dice from the birdfeeder (1 die per food token). The die showing two food types lets you choose one type, not both. If only one face value remains in the birdfeeder (or it is empty), you may reroll all 5 dice. Some slots offer a card-to-food conversion: optionally discard 1 bird card from hand to gain 1 additional food. Then activate brown powers on forest birds from right to left.

### 3. Lay Eggs (Grassland Row)

Place an action cube on the leftmost exposed slot in the grassland row. Lay the indicated number of eggs on any of your birds, respecting each bird's egg limit. Some slots offer a food-to-egg conversion: optionally discard 1 food to lay 1 additional egg. Then activate brown powers on grassland birds from right to left.

### 4. Draw Bird Cards (Wetland Row)

Place an action cube on the leftmost exposed slot in the wetland row. Draw the indicated number of cards from the face-up tray or the top of the deck. Some slots offer an egg-to-card conversion: optionally discard 1 egg to draw 1 additional card. Then activate brown powers on wetland birds from right to left. Face-up cards are refilled only at the end of your turn.

## Bird Powers

- **When Played (white):** Triggers once, immediately upon placing the bird.
- **When Activated (brown):** Triggers each time you take an action in that bird's habitat row, resolved right to left.
- **Once Between Turns (pink):** May trigger once during another player's turn when the stated condition is met. Monitor opponents' actions for these.

Some birds are predators (they roll dice to attempt to catch prey by tucking cards from the deck). Some birds create flocks (tucking cards behind them for points). Cached food tokens on birds score 1 point each and cannot be spent as regular food.

## Round Structure

Each round, players alternate taking turns until everyone has placed all available action cubes. Then:

1. Remove all action cubes from player mats.
2. Score the end-of-round goal for the completed round. Each player places one action cube on the goal board to mark their score.
3. Discard face-up bird cards and refill the tray.
4. Pass the first player token clockwise.

Because one cube is consumed per round for goal tracking, available turns decrease: 8, 7, 6, then 5 turns per round.

**End-of-round goal scoring** depends on the board side chosen during setup:
- **Green (competitive):** Players rank against each other. First place scores the most, with ties splitting combined rewards and rounding down.
- **Blue (individual):** Each player scores 1 point per qualifying item, up to 5 points maximum.

## End-Game Scoring

After Round 4, total your score across six categories:

| Category | Scoring |
|---|---|
| Bird cards | Face value printed on each card |
| Bonus cards | Variable, based on card conditions |
| End-of-round goals | As marked on the goal board |
| Eggs | 1 point per egg on your birds |
| Cached food | 1 point per food token on your birds |
| Tucked cards | 1 point per card tucked behind your birds |

The highest total wins. Tiebreaker: most unused food tokens. If still tied, players share the victory.

## Strategic Considerations

- Early rounds should focus on food generation and playing birds so your engine is productive for later rounds.
- Brown-powered birds compound in value the earlier they are played, as they activate every time you use that row.
- Balance your habitats: overinvesting in one row gives strong actions there but leaves you weak elsewhere.
- Monitor end-of-round goals and plan your bird placements to score well without overcommitting.
- The egg economy is crucial in mid-to-late game since eggs are both a placement cost and a scoring source.
