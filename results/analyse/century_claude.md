MECHANICS: Hand Management, Engine Building, Resource Conversion, Set Collection, Card Drafting, Open Market/Card Row
COMPLEXITY: 1.8
PLAYERS: 2-5
DURATION: 30-45

## Detailed Analysis

### Hand Management
The core action loop revolves around managing a personal hand of Merchant cards. Players choose when to Play cards from hand for their effects, when to Acquire new cards, and critically when to Rest to recover all previously played cards back into their hand. The timing of resting versus continuing to play is a central tactical decision since played cards are unavailable until a full rest action is taken.

### Engine Building
Players progressively improve their capabilities by acquiring increasingly powerful Merchant cards from the market row. Starting with only a basic "Create 2" (gain 2 turmeric) and "Upgrade 2" card, players build a personal engine of Trade cards, Spice cards, and Upgrade cards that allows them to generate and convert spices more efficiently over the course of the game. The rest mechanic (described in the rules as "deck-building") enables repeated cycling through an ever-improving set of cards.

### Resource Conversion
Three types of Merchant cards facilitate resource transformation. Spice cards generate cubes from the supply. Upgrade cards move cubes up the value hierarchy (yellow to red to green to brown, each one level at a time). Trade cards exchange specific cube combinations for other cubes, and can be executed multiple times in a single play as long as the player has sufficient cubes. This layered conversion system creates a spice production chain.

### Set Collection
Victory is achieved by collecting Point cards, each of which demands a specific combination of colored spice cubes. Players must accumulate the right assortment of cubes on their caravan (limited to 10 cubes) and then spend them to claim these cards. Gold and silver bonus tokens reward players who claim the leftmost or second-leftmost Point cards, adding a positional incentive to the collection strategy.

### Card Drafting / Open Market Row
Both Merchant cards and Point cards are displayed in a row of face-up options (6 Merchant cards, 5 Point cards). When acquiring a Merchant card, the player must pay an escalating cost: one cube of any type placed on each card to the left of the desired card. The leftmost card is always free. Cards on which cubes have been placed become more attractive to future acquirers since those cubes transfer to the acquiring player's caravan. This mechanism creates an indirect interaction and cost-benefit tension.

### Game Flow and Endgame
The game ends when a player claims their 5th Point card (or 6th in a 2-3 player game), after which the current round is completed so all players have equal turns. Final scoring sums Point card values, gold tokens (3 points each), silver tokens (1 point each), and non-yellow cubes remaining on the caravan (1 point each). Tiebreaker goes to the last player in turn order.

### Complexity Assessment
Century: Spice Road is a gateway-level game with straightforward rules but meaningful strategic depth. The four possible actions per turn are simple to grasp, the iconography on cards is clear, and the spice upgrade hierarchy is intuitive. The complexity arises from optimizing the engine-building sequence and timing rest actions efficiently. Rated 1.8 out of 5.0, reflecting an accessible game suitable for new and casual players while offering enough depth for experienced gamers.
