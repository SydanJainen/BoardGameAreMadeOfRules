## Detailed Analysis

### Cooperative Game
Pandemic is a fully cooperative game where all players work together against the game system. There is no competitive element: either all players win by discovering all four cures, or all players lose together. Players openly discuss strategy and coordinate their actions each turn.

### Action Points
Each turn, a player receives exactly 4 action points to spend on any combination of available actions. Actions include movement (Drive/Ferry, Direct Flight, Charter Flight, Shuttle Flight), Treat Disease, Build a Research Station, Share Knowledge, Discover a Cure, or Pass. The same action can be repeated multiple times in a single turn if the player has actions remaining.

### Point to Point Movement
The board depicts a network of 48 cities connected by red lines. The basic Drive/Ferry action moves a pawn along one connection. Additional movement options (Direct Flight, Charter Flight, Shuttle Flight) allow longer-range travel at the cost of discarding specific cards or requiring Research Station infrastructure. Movement is strictly point-to-point along the defined network, not area-based.

### Hand Management
Players draw 2 cards per turn from the Player Draw Pile and must stay at or below a 7-card hand limit. Cards serve multiple competing purposes: they can be used for Direct Flight (travel to the pictured city), Charter Flight (travel from the pictured city to anywhere), building Research Stations (play the card matching your current city), or held to form a set of 5 same-color cards for discovering a cure. Deciding which cards to keep, play for movement, or trade is a central strategic tension.

### Set Collection
The primary win condition requires collecting sets of 5 cards of the same color (4 for the Scientist role) and discarding them at a Research Station to discover a cure. Since there are four diseases (Blue, Yellow, Black, Red), players must collectively assemble four such sets. This drives the entire strategic arc of the game.

### Trading (Share Knowledge)
The Share Knowledge action allows players to transfer city cards between each other, but both pawns must be in the same city, and only the card matching that city can be traded (with the exception of the Researcher role, who can give any card). This constrained trading mechanism forces players to coordinate movement and plan meetups.

### Variable Player Powers
Each player receives a randomly assigned role with unique abilities:
- **Medic**: Removes all cubes of one color with a single Treat Disease action; automatically removes cured disease cubes upon entering a city.
- **Scientist**: Needs only 4 cards (instead of 5) to discover a cure.
- **Researcher**: Can give any card during Share Knowledge, not just the city card matching the current location.
- **Operations Expert**: Can build a Research Station without playing the matching city card.
- **Dispatcher**: Can move other players' pawns as if they were his own and can move any pawn to a city containing another pawn.

### Complexity Assessment (2.4/5.0)
Pandemic sits at the upper end of family-game complexity. The rules are straightforward and the turn structure is simple (4 actions, draw 2 cards, infect cities). However, the cooperative decision space is significant: players must balance short-term crisis management (treating outbreaks) against long-term strategy (collecting card sets for cures). The Epidemic mechanic, which reshuffles the infection discard pile on top of the draw pile, creates escalating pressure. The variable difficulty (4, 5, or 6 Epidemic cards) allows scaling. BGG community rates it at approximately 2.39/5.0.

### Player Count and Duration
The game supports 2 to 4 players, with hand sizes adjusted accordingly (4 cards each for 2 players, 3 for 3 players, 2 for 4 players). The box and BGG list 45 minutes as the typical play time, which is accurate for experienced groups. First games may run closer to 60 minutes.
