# Rule Analysis and Corrections

## Issues Identified
1. In the setup instructions, it is unclear how to determine which player gets the first player symbol on their Caravan card. The rule seems to imply that this information should be provided by the game's components or packaging, but there is no explicit indication of where to find this information.
2. The description of the "Upgrade 2" cards is incomplete. It does not specify what happens if a player tries to upgrade a cube more times than allowed by the card's effect.
3. The rules do not clarify how to handle the case where a player has exactly one cube on their caravan and attempts to play a Spice card that requires multiple cubes of the same color. In this scenario, it is unclear what happens next.
4. The "Caravan Limit" rule seems to be a duplicate of the "Cube Limit" rule. It would be better to remove one of these rules or rephrase them to avoid confusion.
5. The winning conditions are not entirely clear. The rule states that each gold token is worth 3 points, but it does not specify what happens if there are multiple players with the same number of points. This could lead to disputes over who wins in the event of a tie.
6. There is no explanation for how to handle the case where a player has run out of cubes (and therefore cannot play a Spice card) and still wants to trade or acquire a Merchant card. This scenario is not explicitly addressed by the rules.
7. The "Trade Cards" rule seems to imply that trading can be done any number of times in a row, but it does not specify what happens if a player tries to trade more cubes than are available in the supply. This could lead to disputes over whether a trade was valid or not.
8. There is no clear explanation for how to handle the case where a player claims a Point card and then immediately tries to acquire another Merchant card that requires cubes from their caravan.

## Suggested Fixes
1. Add a note to the setup instructions indicating that the first player symbol on the Caravan card can be found on the game's packaging or components, or by consulting the rulebook.
2. Clarify what happens if a player tries to upgrade a cube more times than allowed by the "Upgrade 2" card's effect. Perhaps this could be stated as: "If a player attempts to upgrade a cube more times than allowed by the 'Upgrade 2' card, they can only do so up to the number of levels specified on the card."
3. Add an explanation for what happens if a player has exactly one cube on their caravan and attempts to play a Spice card that requires multiple cubes of the same color. Perhaps this could be stated as: "If a player has exactly one cube on their caravan, they can choose to discard it in order to play a Spice card that requires multiple cubes of the same color."
4. Remove the "Caravan Limit" rule and rephrase the "Cube Limit" rule to make it clear that the cube supply is not limited.
5. Clarify what happens if there are multiple players with the same number of points at the end of the game. Perhaps this could be stated as: "In the event of a tie, the player who took their turn last wins."
6. Add an explanation for how to handle the case where a player has run out of cubes and still wants to trade or acquire a Merchant card. Perhaps this could be stated as: "If a player has run out of cubes, they can choose to play a 'Trade' card to acquire additional cubes from the supply."
7. Clarify what happens if a player tries to trade more cubes than are available in the supply. Perhaps this could be stated as: "If a player attempts to trade more cubes than are available in the supply, their turn ends immediately and they cannot continue trading."
8. Add an explanation for how to handle the case where a player claims a Point card and then immediately tries to acquire another Merchant card that requires cubes from their caravan. Perhaps this could be stated as: "If a player claims a Point card, they can immediately attempt to acquire additional Merchant cards from the supply without penalty."

## Corrected Rules

1. To set up a game of Century, follow these steps in order:
   1. Shuffle the Point cards (orange back) to form a deck A, 
   then draw 5 cards and place them face up in a row to the left of that deck B.
   2. Place a pile of gold tokens equal to the number of players x2 above the first (leftmost) point card C. Then place a pile of silver tokens equal to the number of players x2 above the second Point card D.

   Note: The first player symbol on the Caravan card can be found on the game's packaging or components, or by consulting the rulebook.

3. Among the Merchant cards (purple back) E, 
   there are 10 cards with a purple border 
   on the face up side. These are the starting 
   cards. Among them, each player gets 
   one ‘Create 2’ and one ‘Upgrade 2’ card 
   to form their starting hand, as shown here.

   Note: If a player has exactly one cube on their caravan, they can choose to discard it in order to play a Spice card that requires multiple cubes of the same color.

5. Spices are represented by cubes. Separate them by color 
   and place them into the bowls G. Note: Make sure the 
   order of the colored cubes matches the order shown above: 
   Y ellow (Turmeric) Red (Saffron) Green (Cardamom) 
   Brown (Cinnamon).

7. Take a Caravan card (grey back) for each player in the 
   game, being sure to include the card with the first player 
   symbol on it, shuffle them and deal each player one card in 
   front of them H. The player with the first player symbol 

   (as shown here) on their Caravan card is the first player.

8. Place yellow and red cubes according to the table below 
   on each player’s Caravan card I.
  1st player gets 3 yellow cubes 
  2nd player gets 4 yellow cubes
  3rd player gets 4 yellow cubes
  4th player gets 3 yellow and 1 red cube
  5th player gets 3 yellow and 1 red cube

9. To play a card from your hand, place the card face up 
   in front of you and execute the card’s effect. There are 
   3 types of Merchant cards that can be played in this way:
   Spice Cards
   When playing a Spice card, take the number and color of cube(s) shown on 
   the card A from the bowls and place them on your caravan.

11. To acquire a Merchant card, first pay for it by placing ANY 
   cube from your caravan on each Merchant card to the left 
   of the card you are acquiring and then take that Merchant 
   card into your hand.

13. Rest
   To rest, take all of the cards you played face up in 
   front of you back into your hand (This allows players 
   to build their Merchant cards hand, a game mechanism 
   called “deck-building”, as the game evolves).

15. Claim
   To claim a Point card, you must have all 
   of the cubes shown on the point card in your 
   caravan. Return those cubes to the bowls. Take 
   the Point card and place it face down in front of 
   you.

17. Game End
   Once a player has taken their fifth Point card (or sixth in 
   a 2-3 player game), the game will end after players finish 
   out the current round of play. At that point, each player 
   counts all his points on his Point cards, his gold/silver 
   tokens, and the remaining cubes on his caravan.

18. If there are multiple players with the same number of points at the end of the game, the player who took their turn last wins.
