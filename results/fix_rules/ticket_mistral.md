# Rule Analysis and Corrections

## Issues Identified
1. **Missing rule for claiming Double Routes in 2-3 player games:** It's mentioned that only one of the Double-Routes can be used, but it doesn't specify what happens if a player claims one and then another player wants to claim the other route.
2. **Unclear winning condition for ties:** While it's clear who wins in case of a tie for most points or Destination Tickets, it's unclear what happens if two players are tied for both the most points and completed Destination Tickets.
3. **Undefined term - "Adjacent cities":** The rulebook mentions claiming routes between two "adjacent" cities but doesn't define this term.
4. **Missing rule for when no cards left in the deck or discard pile:** Although it's mentioned that players can't draw Train Car cards if there are none left, it doesn't specify what happens if there are also no discards (because players are hoarding many cards in their hands).
5. **Unclear rule for Locomotive cards when all three face-up cards are Locomotives:** It says that all five cards are discarded and replaced, but it's unclear whether the player who drew the third Locomotive still gets to keep it or if they lose it.
6. **Missing edge case - Ending the game with an insufficient number of Destination Tickets:** The rulebook doesn't mention what happens if there aren't enough Destination Ticket cards left for each player to draw their final three cards when one player is running out of trains.

## Suggested Fixes
1. For **Double Routes in 2-3 player games**, add: "If a route has been claimed, it remains closed to other players for the rest of the game."
2. To clarify **winning conditions for ties**, add: "In case two or more players are tied for both the most points and completed Destination Tickets, the player who has played the fewest turns wins."
3. Define **"Adjacent cities"** as "Cities connected by a single route on the map."
4. For **when no cards left in the deck or discard pile**, add: "In this case, players cannot draw Train Car cards and must either claim a route or draw Destination Ticket cards."
5. To clarify **Locomotive cards when all three face-up cards are Locomotives**, add: "The player who drew the third Locomotive keeps it but still discards one of the other two face-up Locomotive cards, then replaces both discarded cards."
6. For **ending the game with an insufficient number of Destination Tickets**, add: "If there aren't enough Destination Ticket cards left for each player to draw their final three cards when one player is running out of trains, distribute the remaining cards as evenly as possible among all players."
